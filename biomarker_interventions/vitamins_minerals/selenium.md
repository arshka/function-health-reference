# Selenium (Plasma) — Intervention Research

> Adult RDA = 55 µg/d. Brazil nuts are the densest food source — 2 nuts/day (~100 µg Se as selenomethionine) is bioequivalent to 100 µg/d selenomethionine supplement (Thomson 2008 *AJCN*: plasma Se +64% in 12 wk). Selenium 200 µg/d as selenomethionine reduces TPO antibodies in Hashimoto's thyroiditis by SMD −0.46 to −0.96 across meta-analyses (Huwiler 2024 *Thyroid* — 2358 participants). **The SELECT trial (n=35,533) found no prostate cancer prevention from 200 µg Se + 400 IU vit E and was halted early; vitamin E alone significantly INCREASED prostate cancer risk by 17% (Klein 2011 *JAMA*)** — undermining the routine-supplementation case in replete populations. **Toxicity (selenosis) at >400 µg/d (IOM UL) or >255 µg/d (EFSA UL) — hair loss, brittle nails, garlic breath, peripheral neuropathy.** SELENOP (rs3877899, rs7579) genotype modulates response.

**Cross-link:** Selenium is not in the standard Function Health panel but is critical as a TPO Ab modifier (relevant if anti-TPO is positive — see `../thyroid/TPO_antibodies.md` if present). Background biology in `../../nutrients_vitamins_minerals.md`.

## 1. Foods that RAISE plasma selenium

Adult RDA 55 µg/d; UL 400 µg/d (IOM) or 255 µg/d (EFSA 2023). Soil selenium content varies enormously by region — US (Great Plains) is Se-rich, much of Europe and China are Se-poor. Brazil nuts are wildly variable (~50–500 µg per nut depending on soil).

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:**
  - For *adequacy* (most US/Canada adults already adequate): no supplement needed; 1–2 Brazil nuts every other day or fish 2×/wk is sufficient.
  - For *Hashimoto's TPO Ab reduction*: selenomethionine 200 µg/d × 3–6 months (consider as time-limited trial; don't supplement indefinitely if TPO Ab response is null at 6 mo).
  - For *low-Se region (UK, parts of EU)*: 1–2 Brazil nuts/d or 100 µg/d selenomethionine to reach adequate plasma Se.
- **Best stack of 3 (for Hashimoto's-targeted use):**
  1. Selenomethionine 200 µg/d × 3–6 months
  2. Adequate iodine (not excess — both deficiency and excess can worsen Hashimoto's)
  3. Vitamin D adequacy (Hashimoto's is associated with low vitamin D; correction may modestly modify autoimmunity)
- **Realistic 12-week delta:**
  - From low baseline (plasma Se ~50 µg/L): +30–50 µg/L on 100–200 µg/d selenomethionine.
  - From adequate baseline (~100 µg/L): plateau effect; minimal further increase.
  - TPO Ab reduction in Hashimoto's: ~25–40% lower at 6 months (highly variable by individual).
- **Where pharmacotherapy / specialty intervention becomes necessary:**
  - Severe Se deficiency with cardiomyopathy (Keshan disease) — IV selenite, dietary repletion.
  - Selenium toxicity (selenosis): discontinue source; supportive care; chelation experimental and rarely needed.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- Soil selenium varies across the Indian subcontinent — some regions (Punjab) have adequate Se; others (Bihar, parts of South India) are Se-poor.
- SA vegetarians outside high-Se soil regions may have marginal Se status. Brazil nuts (1–2/d) are the most practical fix.
- No SA-specific Se deficiency epidemiology comparable to the universal vitamin D or vegetarian B12 picture.
- Hashimoto's prevalence in SA women is rising; if anti-TPO is positive, the Se 200 µg/d × 3–6 mo trial is reasonable.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **SELENOP (selenoprotein P) rs3877899** — A234T amino acid substitution; minor allele A carriers have higher SELENOP concentrations after Se supplementation **[RCT — Méplan 2008; Cominetti 2014 SU.BRA.NUT]**. Minor allele frequency varies by ancestry.
- **SELENOP rs7579** — 3' UTR variant affecting Sec insertion; modulates Se response.
- **GPX1 rs1050450 (Pro198Leu)** — modulates glutathione peroxidase activity response to Se intake.
- **GPX4 rs713041** — affects GPx4 activity; relevant for lipid peroxidation defense.
- **SELS rs34713741** — selenoprotein S; inflammation-related.
- **DIO1, DIO2, DIO3** — deiodinase enzymes (Se-dependent); variants affect thyroid hormone conversion.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Statins** — minor decrease in serum Se (cholesterol-Se transport interaction); not clinically meaningful.
- **Cisplatin** — Se may modulate cisplatin nephrotoxicity (limited evidence).
- **Levothyroxine** — co-supplementation with high-dose Se may modestly affect T4 dosing requirements; minor.
- **Anticoagulants (warfarin)** — high-dose Se may potentiate anticoagulation; modest.

**Lifestyle confounders:**
- **Soil Se geography** — by far the dominant determinant of population Se status.
- **Vegetarian diet in low-Se region** — primary risk factor.
- **Heavy alcohol** — Se ↓.
- **Smoking** — modest ↓ in plasma Se.
- **Pregnancy/lactation** — increased requirement.

**Timing/assay artifacts:**
- **Plasma/serum Se** is the standard marker; reflects intake over weeks–months.
- **Whole-blood Se** is also used; reflects long-term status.
- **Hair / nail Se** — reflects very long-term exposure; useful for forensic/toxicological assessment.
- **GPx activity** — functional Se marker; plateaus at plasma Se ~90–100 µg/L (above this, supplementation provides no further GPx benefit).
- **Inflammation lowers serum Se** (acute-phase reactant); recheck when well.
- **SELENOP (selenoprotein P)** — the most sensitive functional Se status marker; reflects hepatic Se export and is the primary Se transport protein. Less commonly available than plasma Se.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal plasma Se range.** Plasma Se ~80–120 µg/L is "adequate"; GPx activity plateaus around 90–100 µg/L. Targets above this provide no clear functional benefit and approach toxicity range. Stay 80–150 µg/L for general health.
- **SELECT trial implications.** SELECT (Lippman 2009 *JAMA* PMID 19066370; Klein 2011 *JAMA* PMID 21990298) tested 200 µg Se + 400 IU vit E in 35,533 men for prostate cancer prevention. Stopped early — no Se prostate cancer benefit; vit E alone *increased* prostate cancer 17% (statistically significant). This dramatically shifted the field away from routine high-dose Se supplementation in already-replete populations. The Stranges 2007 finding of *increased* T2DM risk with Se supplementation in already-replete subjects further argues against routine supplementation.
- **Hashimoto's: Se for TPO Ab.** Multiple meta-analyses (Huwiler 2024 *Thyroid* — n=2358; Wichman 2016; Frontiers 2024 network meta) confirm TPO Ab reduction with selenomethionine 200 µg/d. *Whether this translates to slowed progression to overt hypothyroidism, reduced thyroid disease, or reduced levothyroxine requirement is much less established.* The TPO Ab biomarker effect is robust; the hard outcome is uncertain. Reasonable as time-limited trial (3–6 mo) in TPO-positive patients; reassess at 6 mo and discontinue if no benefit.
- **Selenium + myo-inositol combination for Hashimoto's.** Network meta-analysis PMID 39650307 (2024) suggests Se + myo-inositol may be more effective than Se alone. Mechanism unclear; replication needed.
- **Selenium for thyroid eye disease (Graves' ophthalmopathy).** Marcocci 2011 *NEJM* (n=159, 200 µg/d × 6 mo): improved eye disease severity and quality of life vs placebo in mild Graves' ophthalmopathy. Established indication in EU thyroid eye disease guidelines. Not relevant for routine TPO Ab management.
- **Selenium and immune function.** Adequate Se supports immune response; supraphysiological doses do not provide additional benefit.
- **Selenium in HIV.** Hurwitz 2007 RCT (n=174): 200 µg/d Se reduced viral load progression. Niche application.
- **Selenium toxicity threshold.** IOM UL 400 µg/d; EFSA UL 255 µg/d (more conservative; 2023 update). Selenosis features (hair loss, brittle nails, garlic breath, peripheral neuropathy) emerge with sustained intake >800 µg/d; UL adds safety margin. Most adult selenosis cases in US are from manufacturing-error supplements (e.g., Total Body Formula incident 2008: ~200× over-dosed).
- **Brazil nut variability.** Per-nut Se content ranges 50–500 µg depending on soil. Long-term consumption of >4–5 nuts/d can exceed UL. Recommend cap at 2/d for routine supplementation; do not "stack" Brazil nuts + Se supplement.
- **Form preference.** Selenomethionine (organic) better incorporated into proteins than sodium selenite (inorganic); selenium yeast is intermediate. For TPO Ab trials, selenomethionine is the standard.
