# Urine Leukocyte Esterase — Intervention Research

> Leukocyte esterase (LE) is **negative in healthy adults**. Positive LE indicates **pyuria** — neutrophils in urine — most commonly from UTI but also from urethritis (chlamydia, gonorrhea), prostatitis (men), vaginitis (women, often contamination), interstitial nephritis (drug-induced especially NSAIDs/PPIs), kidney stones, glomerulonephritis, genitourinary tuberculosis, and sterile pyuria. Sensitivity ~72–96%, specificity ~78–90% for UTI. Interventions are largely identical to those for nitrites/UTI prevention but with broader scope: address gram-positive UTIs (*S. saprophyticus*, *Enterococcus*) and STIs that nitrite misses. Also manage drug-induced interstitial nephritis (stop NSAID/PPI). Pre-test: clean-catch midstream technique critical (vaginal/preputial contamination is #1 false-positive cause); hold high-dose vitamin C 24–48 h.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation in `../../urinalysis_panel.md` §11 (Leukocyte Esterase). Highly correlated with §10 nitrites and §13 WBC microscopy. UTI prevention overlaps with `urine_nitrites.md`.

## 1. Foods that LOWER LE positivity (UTI/pyuria prevention)

(Mostly identical to nitrite-lowering foods — see `urine_nitrites.md` for detailed evidence on hydration, cranberry, D-mannose, probiotics. Listed here in compact form.)

| Food | Dose | Effect | Evidence | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Water (≥2 L/d) | Sustained | UTI ↓ ~48%, pyuria recurrence ↓ proportionally | **[RCT]** | Hooton et al. *JAMA Intern Med* 2018 (PMID 30285042) |

| Blueberries (PACs) | Daily | Anti-FimH adhesin blockade similar to cranberry | **[Mechanism]** | Howell *Phytochemistry* 2005 |

### UTI Treatment (turning LE negative by treating cause)

(Same first-line agents as in `urine_nitrites.md`: nitrofurantoin 100 mg BID × 5d, TMP-SMX DS BID × 3d, fosfomycin 3g single dose. Per IDSA 2010 — Gupta et al. PMID 21292654.)

For STIs causing LE positivity:
- **Chlamydia trachomatis**: doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7 d (preferred) or azithromycin 1 g single dose (CDC STI Treatment Guidelines 2021)
- **Neisseria gonorrhoeae**: ceftriaxone 500 mg IM single dose (CDC 2021)
- **Mycoplasma genitalium**: doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7 d → moxifloxacin 400 mg/d × 7 d (CDC)
- **Trichomoniasis**: metronidazole 2 g single dose (women) or 7-day course (men)

For drug-induced acute interstitial nephritis (sterile pyuria + ↑Cr):
- **Discontinue offending drug** (NSAID, PPI, antibiotic, diuretic) — first-line
- **Glucocorticoids** (prednisone 1 mg/kg taper) considered for non-resolving AIN

For genitourinary tuberculosis (sterile pyuria + acidic urine):
- **Standard 4-drug RIPE × 6 mo** (rifampin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide, ethambutol)

For interstitial cystitis / painful bladder syndrome:
- **Lifestyle**: bladder training, dietary trigger avoidance, pelvic floor PT
- **Pentosan polysulfate** 100 mg TID (limited efficacy)
- **Amitriptyline** 25–75 mg HS
- **Hydroxyzine** 25 mg HS
- **Intravesical DMSO**, hyaluronic acid, heparin

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** for active UTI (LE+, symptoms): **appropriate antibiotic per IDSA + local antibiogram** (nitrofurantoin or TMP-SMX first-line for uncomplicated cystitis).
- **Highest-leverage single intervention for prevention** of recurrent UTI/pyuria: **hydration ≥2.5 L/d** (Hooton 2018, RR 0.52).
- **Best stack of 3 (recurrent UTI prevention)**:
  1. Hydration ≥2.5 L/d
  2. D-mannose 2 g/d OR cranberry PAC 36 mg/d
  3. Methenamine hippurate 1 g BID (long-term)
  Combined: ~60–80% UTI reduction.
- **For sterile pyuria** (LE+, culture negative): workup includes STI panel (CT/GC NAAT), prostate exam (men), TB screen if exposure, NSAID/PPI history, urology referral if persistent.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM with positive LE + symptoms → diagnostic workup (urine culture, STI testing) and targeted treatment. UTI in young male is uncommon and warrants urology referral.
- **Where pharmacotherapy / referral becomes necessary**:
  - Symptomatic UTI → IDSA-guided antibiotics
  - Pyelonephritis → ciprofloxacin or ceftriaxone
  - STI (chlamydia, gonorrhea) → CDC-guided treatment
  - GU TB → 4-drug RIPE × 6 mo
  - Recurrent UTI → low-dose prophylaxis or methenamine
  - Suspected interstitial cystitis → urology / gyne
  - Suspected drug-induced AIN → discontinue drug, nephrology consult, possibly steroids
  - Bladder cancer in situ (sterile pyuria with hematuria) → urgent urology

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- UTI in SA young male is uncommon — first or recurrent UTI warrants urology workup (anatomic anomaly, instrumentation, prostatitis, residual urine).
- Genitourinary tuberculosis: South Asia is high-prevalence region. Persistent sterile pyuria + acidic urine + hematuria + history of TB exposure → screen with urine AFB ×3 and urine TB PCR.
- South Asian men with diabetes have higher candiduria prevalence — yeast on UA + glucosuria → check HbA1c.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**False positives (positive LE without true pyuria):**
- **Vaginal contamination** (most common in women) → high LE + high squamous cells
- **Preputial contamination** (uncircumcised men, inadequate retraction)
- Trichomonas vaginalis
- Strenuous exercise (mild WBC elevation)
- Phenazopyridine color
- Highly contaminated specimen (squamous cells >15/HPF)
- Lubricants from instrumentation

**False negatives (true pyuria but negative LE):**
- **Vitamin C** (~8% per Nagel 2016)
- **High glucose, high specific gravity, high protein** (suppresses LE chemistry)
- **Antibiotics** (cephalosporins, gentamicin, tetracyclines, nitrofurantoin specifically depress LE chemistry)
- **Lymphocyte-predominant inflammation** (LE is neutrophil-specific):
  - Chronic interstitial nephritis
  - Lupus nephritis
  - Transplant rejection
  - Some viral cystitis
- **Boric acid preservative** in container
- **Severe neutropenia** (chemotherapy, marrow failure) — no neutrophils to esterase

**Sterile pyuria differential (LE+, culture negative):**
- Recently treated UTI (within 2 weeks)
- Chlamydia, gonorrhea, mycoplasma, ureaplasma (urethritis)
- Genitourinary TB (acidic urine, hematuria, weight loss)
- Interstitial cystitis / painful bladder
- NSAID, PPI, β-lactam, sulfa, allopurinol — drug-induced AIN
- Renal stones (inflammation around stone)
- Foreign body, indwelling stent
- Bladder cancer especially CIS
- Schistosomiasis (endemic exposure)
- Fungal UTI (Candida — usually identified on microscopy)
- Pregnancy (mild physiologic pyuria)
- Sjögren syndrome with interstitial nephritis

**Combined LE + nitrite for UTI screening (per IDSA / AAFP):**
- Both positive → UTI PPV ~85% in symptomatic
- Either positive → reasonable sensitivity 70–90%
- Both negative → strong NPV 90–95% in low-prevalence
- Symptomatic patient with both negative still warrants culture in some settings

**Drug interactions / modifiers:**
- Active antibiotic — sterilizes urine
- Steroids high-dose — neutropenia → false negative
- Chemotherapy / biologics — false negative
- Phenazopyridine — orange color interference with all dipstick
- Vitamin C — false negative
- Methenamine — suppresses bacteria in vivo

**Genetic / disease modifiers:**
- Diabetes → recurrent UTI, sterile pyuria from glycosuria-related mild inflammation
- Pregnancy → physiologic mild pyuria; ASB needs treatment
- Immunosuppression → LE may be negative despite infection
- Spinal cord injury, neurogenic bladder → chronic bacteriuria + pyuria; treat only if symptomatic
- Indwelling catheter — chronic bacteriuria + pyuria expected
- BPH, urethral stricture — outflow obstruction, recurrent UTI risk

**Timing / artifacts:**
- Old specimen (>1 h at room temp) → WBCs lyse, false negative
- Refrigerate >1 h
- Vaginal/preputial contamination → repeat with proper clean-catch
- First UTI in young male → urology workup (anatomic) per AUA

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal duration of low-dose antibiotic prophylaxis** vs. methenamine — ALTAR shows non-inferiority over 12 mo; longer-term unclear
- **D-mannose Cochrane 2022** found insufficient evidence — needs larger trial
- **Cranberry strain / PAC standardization** — biggest cause of trial heterogeneity
- **Probiotic strain specificity** — *L. crispatus* CTV-05 most evidence; oral strains weaker
- **UTI vaccines** (Uromune, Solco-Urovac, Stro-Vac) — promising in EU, not US
- **First-UTI workup in young male**: routine vs. selective imaging — guideline-supported but evidence base modest
- **Distinguishing sterile pyuria from contamination on first sample** — ideally second sample with clear technique; criterion variable
- **Whether persistent low-grade LE positivity in asymptomatic patient warrants any intervention**: per IDSA 2019, no — do not treat asymptomatic bacteriuria/pyuria except in pregnancy or pre-procedure
