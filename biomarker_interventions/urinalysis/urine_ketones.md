# Urine Ketones — Intervention Research

> Urine ketones are **negative in healthy adults eating mixed diets**. They turn positive whenever fatty-acid oxidation outpaces carbohydrate metabolism — i.e. **fasting >12–24 h**, **carbohydrate restriction <50 g/d (ketogenic diet)**, **prolonged endurance exercise**, **alcohol binge**, **diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)**, **euglycemic DKA on SGLT2 inhibitors**, **pregnancy with hyperemesis**, **alcoholic ketoacidosis**, and **starvation**. Ketones in a person on keto/fasting/SGLT2i with normal glucose = expected and benign. Ketones + plasma glucose >250 mg/dL + symptoms = DKA, EMERGENCY. The dipstick chemistry (sodium nitroprusside) detects acetoacetate strongly and acetone weakly, but **misses β-hydroxybutyrate entirely** — so during DKA recovery urine ketones can paradoxically RISE while the patient improves.

## 2. Foods that RAISE urine ketones (induce ketonuria)

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention to ELIMINATE ketones**: eat carbohydrates ≥100–150 g/d. Within 24–72 h, ketonuria resolves.
- **Highest-leverage single intervention to INDUCE ketones (for nutritional ketosis)**: keep carb intake <50 g/d, protein moderate (~1 g/kg), fat ad lib. Within 3–14 days achieves stable ketonuria.
- **Best stack of 3 (induce nutritional ketosis)**:
  1. Carb <30 g/d
  2. 16:8 time-restricted eating
  3. MCT oil 1–2 tbsp/d as adjunct
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM:
  - Eating mixed diet → urine ketones negative
  - On strict keto → small–moderate (15–40 mg/dL) sustained
- **Where pharmacotherapy / emergency intervention required**:
  - **DKA (urine ketones positive + glucose >250 + acidosis)** → IV insulin + fluids + electrolytes; ICU
  - **Euglycemic DKA on SGLT2i** (urine ketones moderate–large, glucose normal, acidosis) → hold SGLT2i, IV insulin + dextrose + fluids
  - **Alcoholic ketoacidosis** → IV thiamine + dextrose + fluids
  - **Salicylate poisoning** → urinary alkalinization, hemodialysis if severe

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- South Asian young males with new diabetes can present with DKA at first diagnosis (especially LADA / Type 1.5). Urine ketones + glucosuria + symptoms always merits urgent evaluation.
- Higher prevalence of T1DM / LADA in some SA cohorts than appreciated; consider antibody panel (GAD-65, IA-2, ZnT8) in lean male presenting with DKA.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**The dipstick (sodium nitroprusside) chemistry caveats:**
- Detects **acetoacetate** strongly, **acetone** weakly (with glycine), and **β-hydroxybutyrate NOT AT ALL**.
- In DKA, β-OHB is the dominant ketone (~10:1 over AcAc) — dipstick **underestimates** true ketosis.
- During DKA RECOVERY, β-OHB is oxidized back to acetoacetate, so urine ketones can paradoxically RISE while the patient is improving — **rising urine ketones during DKA treatment is NOT worsening; trust serum/cap β-OHB**.
- Diabetes Care 2022 (Misra & Oliver, PMID 35040904) recommends point-of-care capillary β-OHB over urine ketones for DKA management.

**False positives:**
- Free sulfhydryl drugs: captopril, mesna, **N-acetylcysteine** (very common cause; oncology and acetaminophen overdose), penicillamine
- Levodopa metabolites
- Phenazopyridine (color)
- Highly concentrated urine
- Acidic urine (mild over-read)
- Beet pigments

**False negatives:**
- Predominantly β-OHB ketosis (any DKA, AKA, starvation)
- Old / improperly stored sample (acetoacetate volatilizes; ketone strip should be sealed)
- Dilute urine
- Vitamin C (mild)

**Drug interactions:**
- SGLT2 inhibitors increase risk of euglycemic DKA, especially during fasting, surgery, illness, low-carb diet, alcohol — patients on SGLT2i should NOT do prolonged fasting or strict keto without monitoring
- Glucocorticoid taper / discontinuation in steroid-dependent diabetics → DKA risk
- Insulin pump failure (occluded line) → rapid DKA
- Stress doses of steroids before surgery → glycemic decompensation

**Genetic / disease modifiers:**
- T1DM — ketosis-prone at any insulin deficit
- T2DM — usually more insulin reserve; HHS more common than DKA, but DKA possible
- Latent autoimmune diabetes in adults (LADA) — present like T2 but ketosis-prone
- MODY (HNF1A, HNF4A, GCK) — generally not ketosis-prone
- Inborn errors of fatty acid oxidation (MCAD, VLCAD) — paradoxically LOW ketones during fasting/illness with hypoglycemia (a "hypoketotic hypoglycemia" pattern; emergency)
- Glycogen storage diseases — fasting hypoglycemia + variable ketosis depending on type

**Timing / artifacts:**
- First-morning urine after overnight fast often trace-positive in keto/IF
- Ketones evaporate from open container — process within 1 h or seal
- Refrigeration generally OK (not photosensitive like bilirubin)

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal urine ketone level for therapeutic ketogenic diet (epilepsy, brain tumors)** — pediatric epilepsy diet aims for moderate ketosis but blood β-OHB 4–6 mmol/L, urine measurement is unreliable for titration
- **Long-term safety of sustained nutritional ketosis** — Virta Health 2-year data shows no harm in T2DM remission cohort but longer-term cardiovascular data limited
- **Exogenous ketone supplementation effects on athletic performance** — mixed, most well-designed trials show no benefit (Leckey *J Sports Sci* 2017, PMID 27710043)
- **Whether persistent low-grade ketonuria has any long-term harm** in a healthy individual — no clear evidence either way
- **EuDKA on SGLT2i during physiologic fasts** — incidence likely underreported; precise risk by patient subgroup unclear
