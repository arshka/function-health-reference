# Urine Specific Gravity — Intervention Research

> Specific gravity is a hydration-status proxy with a transient, dose-dependent response to fluid intake (~0.005 SG drop per 500 mL water within 1–2 h). The dominant levers are total daily fluid (1.5–3 L target SG 1.010–1.020), timing of last void (first-morning is most concentrated), and pharmacologic diuresis (caffeine, alcohol, loop diuretics). Persistent SG <1.005 with polyuria → diabetes insipidus workup; fixed SG ~1.010 → CKD with isosthenuria.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../urinalysis_panel.md` §3 (Specific Gravity). This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend
| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology/physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial |

**Why SG matters clinically.** Hydration status proxy. Above 1.025 → dehydration (stone risk, AKI risk in heat/exercise). Below 1.005 with polyuria → DI workup. Fixed at 1.010 across samples → isosthenuria from advanced CKD. Independent of pathology, SG modifies interpretation of every other dipstick parameter (a "1+" protein in dilute urine = more loss than a "1+" in concentrated urine).

## 3. Supplements & Drugs

### Lowering SG / increasing urine output

| Supplement / Drug | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Caffeine (single dose, naive user) | 250 mg | Acute SG ↓ 0.005 (diuretic effect) | 1–3 h | **[RCT]** | Maughan & Griffin *J Hum Nutr Diet* 2003;16:411 (PMID 19774754) | Tolerance in regular users; chronic SG effect ~0 |
| Loop diuretics (furosemide 40 mg) | Single dose | SG ↓ 0.005–0.015 | 1–4 h | **[Mechanism]** | Brater *NEJM* 1998 review | Used for HF/edema |
| Thiazides (HCTZ 25 mg) | Daily | Mild SG ↓; paradoxical SG ↑ in DI (used to treat nephrogenic DI) | Days | **[Mechanism]** | Loffing & Kaissling | Paradox: thiazides reduce urine output in DI |
| SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin 10 mg) | Daily | SG ↑ 0.002–0.005 (glucose adds to SG); urine vol ↑ ~300 mL/d | Days | **[RCT]** | Inzucchi et al. *NEJM* 2015 (EMPA-REG OUTCOME) | Glucosuria ~50–80 g/d |
| Mannitol IV | 0.25–1 g/kg | SG ↑ to >1.030 (osmotic agent) | 1 h | **[Mechanism]** | Used for ICP, AKI prophylaxis | Renders SG uninterpretable for tubular function |

| ADH / desmopressin (DDAVP) | 0.1 mg PO | SG ↑ 0.005–0.020 | 2–6 h | **[Mechanism]** | Used for central DI; nocturia | Therapeutic agent for low SG / DI |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention**: Drink 500 mL water 1–2 h before specimen collection → SG drop of ~0.005–0.010 within 90 min. For chronic targeting: total fluid 2–3 L/d → steady-state SG 1.010–1.015.
- **Best stack of 3** (achieving SG ≤1.020 reliably):
  1. ≥2.5 L water/day, distributed (not bolus)
  2. Avoid caffeine + alcohol within 6 h of specimen
  3. Avoid intense exercise/sauna within 2 h of specimen
- **Realistic 12-hr delta** for healthy 20yM at baseline 1.025 (mild dehydration): With 1 L water within 2 hr → SG falls to 1.010–1.015.
- **For chronic kidney-stone prevention**: Borghi RCT (1996) showed maintaining urine vol ≥2 L/d and SG ≤1.010 cuts stone recurrence ~50% over 5 years. Single most effective stone-prevention intervention.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**:
  - DDAVP / desmopressin for central diabetes insipidus (raise SG)
  - HCTZ for nephrogenic DI (paradoxically raises SG)
  - Loop diuretic for fluid overload (HF, edema) — lowers SG

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- South Asian summer urine SG often >1.025 due to ambient heat + lower habitual fluid intake. Stone disease prevalence elevated.
- Lean young males underestimate sweat losses; first-morning SG of 1.025–1.030 common in athletes who drink primarily during/after workouts but not throughout the day.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **Glucose adds ~0.004 per 1 g/dL** — positive glucose dipstick must be corrected when interpreting SG (Pranger 1980; *Clinical Methods* NBK302).
- **Protein adds ~0.003 per 1 g/dL** — heavy proteinuria inflates SG.
- **IV iodinated contrast** can drive SG to >1.040 — sample uninterpretable for tubular function within 24 h of contrast.
- **Mannitol, dextran, hetastarch** — exogenous osmolar agents inflate SG; flag specimen.
- **Alkaline urine (pH >7)** can artifactually depress dipstick SG by ~0.005 (a polyelectrolyte chemistry quirk). Refractometry preferred when pH high.
- **Drug interactions**:
  - DDAVP, vasopressin → SG ↑
  - Loop, thiazide, K-sparing diuretics → SG ↓ acutely
  - SGLT2 inhibitors → SG ↑ via glucose
  - Lithium → can cause nephrogenic DI → low SG
  - Demeclocycline → SIADH treatment, raises SG
- **Genetic modifiers**:
  - AVPR2 mutations → X-linked nephrogenic DI → SG <1.005
  - AQP2 mutations → autosomal nephrogenic DI
  - Familial neurohypophyseal DI (AVP gene) → low SG, responds to DDAVP
- **Timing**: First-morning void typically 1.020–1.030 (8 h concentration); random afternoon sample after lunch fluids may be 1.005–1.015. Document.
- **Pregnancy**: Plasma volume expansion → mild physiologic dilution; SG slightly lower.

---

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- "Optimal" hydration for population SG target is not standardized. Borghi 1996 used SG <1.010 for stone-formers; healthy populations have no established target.
- "Hydration challenge" tests (DDAVP test, water deprivation test) are diagnostic for DI but not interventions for SG normalization in general population.
- Smart-toilet urine SG sensors (e.g., Withings U-Scan) are commercially nascent; calibration/accuracy data limited.
- Effect of intermittent fasting on chronic SG values not well-studied.
