# Cadmium (Whole Blood / Urine Cd) — Intervention Research

> The single highest-leverage intervention for cadmium is **smoking cessation**. Cigarette smoke is the dominant non-occupational Cd source in the US; chronic smokers have blood Cd geometric means 3–4× higher than non-smokers (~1.5 vs 0.3 µg/L; NHANES). Cd has an extremely long biological half-life (10–30 years in kidney cortex), so blood Cd primarily reflects recent absorption while **urine Cd indexes lifetime body burden**. After smoking cessation blood Cd halves over months to years (kidney burden persists for decades). Dietary Cd from leafy greens (especially spinach, lettuce), shellfish (especially oysters, mussels, scallops), organ meats (liver, kidney), cocoa, sunflower seeds, flax, and rice grown in contaminated soil contributes the rest. **Iron sufficiency (ferritin >30 µg/L) reduces Cd absorption ~30–50%** — Fe-deficient women absorb up to 10× more Cd via shared DMT1 transporter (Berglund 1994 *EHP*; Akesson 2002). No effective dietary or supplement chelator exists for Cd; **EDTA chelation** is reserved for symptomatic acute poisoning and may worsen renal toxicity if misused (chelated Cd-EDTA is itself nephrotoxic). Marketed Cd "detox" supplements have **no controlled human evidence** — flagged throughout as **[no-evidence]**.

**Cross-link:** Cadmium is not currently in the FH panel core but is biologically and epidemiologically important (renal cortex deposition, osteoporosis, CKD, lung cancer, all-cause mortality — Tellez-Plaza 2012 *EHP*; Larsson 2015 *EHP*). This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs / cohorts |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial |
| **[no-evidence]** | Marketed claim with no controlled human evidence |

Population caveat: most quantitative human Cd data comes from occupationally exposed workers (battery, smelting, pigment) or high-exposure dietary populations (rice-paddy regions of Japan, China, Thailand). For US adult background exposure, blood Cd geometric mean is ~0.3 µg/L (non-smokers) to ~1.5 µg/L (smokers); urine Cd geometric mean ~0.2 µg/g creatinine.

## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

Like other heavy metals, blood/urine Cd has **no established direct relationship with wearable-tracked physiological metrics**. The single most informative wearable-equivalent metric is **smoking status logged via app** or **vape session frequency**.

| Wearable metric | Direction of relationship | Magnitude / effect size | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Smoking app log (cigarettes/d, vape sessions/d) | Direct; predictive of blood Cd | Each pack-year of smoking ≈ small steady increment to blood Cd; current smokers ~3–5× higher than non-smokers | **[Cohort]** | NHANES smoker Cd analyses | Largest single behavior input. |
| Dietary tracking (oyster, dark chocolate, sunflower seed, spinach frequency) | Direct; modest predictive | Each weekly oyster meal ≈ +0.05–0.15 µg/L blood Cd at steady state | **[FDA + EFSA monitoring]** | EFSA 2009 | Secondary to smoking. |
| GPS / occupational location (battery / smelting / electroplating workplaces) | Possible exposure ↑ | Occupational air Cd exposure can dominate | **[OSHA + cohort]** | OSHA Cd Standard | Niche. |

The actionable tracking layer for Cd is **smoking status + dietary log of oysters / dark chocolate / sunflower seeds / spinach + iron status (ferritin)**, not a wearable physiological signal.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** **Smoking cessation** (cigarettes, cigars, pipe, vape, marijuana). Blood Cd ↓ ~50% over 1 year (slow vs Pb/Hg); kidney cortex burden persists for decades. Co-benefits: CRP ↓ 30–50%, WBC ↓ 1–1.5 ×10⁹/L, CV mortality ↓, lung function preserved, PhenoAge ↓ 1.5–3 years (see `phenoage.md`).
- **Best stack of 3:** (1) Smoking cessation (above), (2) Iron sufficiency (ferritin >30 µg/L) — cuts ongoing GI Cd absorption ~30–50%, (3) Dietary diversification — limit oysters / mussels to ≤1–2/wk, dark chocolate to <30 g/d, sunflower seeds to ≤1 oz/d, rotate leafy greens.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (non-smoker, blood Cd <0.3 µg/L, urine Cd <0.2 µg/g cr):** Essentially no actionable change. Cd at population floor; assay variance dominates.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a current smoker (~1 pack/d, blood Cd 1.5 µg/L, urine Cd 0.5 µg/g cr):** Smoking cessation: blood Cd ~1.0 µg/L at 6 months, ~0.8 µg/L at 12 months (slow due to long blood half-life); kidney cortex Cd burden largely unchanged at 12 weeks (decades to clear). Co-benefits in CRP / WBC visible at 12 weeks.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a heavy oyster / dark chocolate consumer (non-smoker, blood Cd 0.7 µg/L from diet):** Reduce oysters to 1/wk + dark chocolate to <30 g/d + iron sufficiency → blood Cd ~0.4 µg/L by 12 weeks (modest).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** **Symptomatic acute Cd poisoning ONLY** (rare in US — usually inhalation injury from welding galvanized steel, fume fever, or large oral ingestion). Even then, chelation is **controversial** because Cd-EDTA complex is nephrotoxic and may worsen kidney injury. **For chronic / smoking-related / dietary Cd, no chelation is indicated.** Standard of care is source elimination + nutritional support + monitoring renal function (eGFR, urine albumin, B2-microglobulin).

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Vegetarian SA males / females** with low ferritin are at higher GI Cd absorption (DMT1 upregulation). Maintain ferritin >30 µg/L to cut absorption ~30–50%.
- **Plant-protein-heavy diets** (vegan, Jain, lacto-vegetarian) often emphasize sunflower / flax / hemp seeds, brown rice, spinach, dark chocolate / cocoa — all of which can accumulate Cd. Diversify protein and seed sources.
- **Smoking** (including bidi, hookah, gutka chewing tobacco) — bidi smoke Cd content is comparable to or higher than cigarettes per stick. Hookah sessions can deliver large Cd doses.
- **Imported rice from Cd-contaminated regions** (some Asian sources) — variable; US-grown rice is generally low Cd. Itai-itai disease in postwar Japan was the classic Cd-rice-paddy syndrome.
- **Ayurvedic / rasa shastra preparations** documented for Pb/Hg/As primarily; Cd less commonly tested but reported in some samples.
- **Iron deficiency anemia is common in SA women** (and some lean SA males with restrictive diets); the iron-deficiency-driven Cd absorption pathway is high-yield to address.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **HFE C282Y / H63D (hereditary hemochromatosis)**: iron overload → reduced DMT1 expression → reduced Cd absorption. Carriers have lower Cd absorption but iron-overload risks dwarf any Cd benefit.
- **MT1 / MT2 (metallothionein) polymorphisms**: modulate tissue Cd sequestration capacity; population-level signals, not individually actionable.
- **GSTM1 / GSTT1 deletions**: GSH-conjugation efficiency; modest effect.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Iron supplementation** in non-deficient: do not give without measured low ferritin — iron overload risks outweigh Cd-absorption reduction.
- **Calcium supplementation** at high doses (>2000 mg/d): CV / kidney stone risks; modest additional Cd-absorption reduction not worth trade-off.
- **Pharmacologic chelators** (EDTA, DMSA, DMPS): limited efficacy for Cd; EDTA may worsen renal toxicity.
- **NSAIDs** (chronic high-dose): can worsen Cd-induced nephrotoxicity via additive kidney injury.

**Confounding lifestyle factors:**
- **Smoking** is the dominant driver — current smokers will have 3–5× higher blood Cd than non-smokers regardless of all other interventions. Smoking status must be documented before interpreting Cd values.
- **Iron deficiency**: dramatically increases Cd absorption (10× in severely deficient). Document ferritin alongside Cd.
- **Pregnancy / lactation**: Cd accumulates in placenta; partner Cd burden affects fetal exposure.
- **Renal function**: impaired kidneys reduce Cd excretion → more accumulation (vicious cycle).

**Timing artifacts:**
- **Blood Cd half-life: 75–128 days** in soft-tissue compartment; reflects months of exposure.
- **Urine Cd reflects body burden** (kidney cortex Cd is the main reservoir; t½ 10–30 years there). Urine Cd is a better long-term burden index than blood Cd for chronic exposure.
- **Whole-blood Cd vs serum Cd**: whole blood preferred (Cd partitions to RBCs).
- **Recent shellfish meal** can elevate blood Cd modestly; pre-draw avoidance for 3–7 days advisable for accurate baseline.
- **Sample contamination**: trace metals require strict pre-analytic discipline (royal-blue-top tube, no rubber stoppers).

**Distinction: blood Cd vs urine Cd:**
- Blood Cd: recent exposure (days–months).
- Urine Cd: chronic body burden / kidney cortex exposure (decades).
- Both can be elevated in active smokers; only urine remains elevated long after smoking cessation as kidney cortex slowly releases Cd.

## 9. Open Questions / Weak Evidence

- **Effect size of dietary diversification at population-floor blood Cd**: unclear. Most evidence is at high exposure.
- **Cocoa / dark chocolate Cd**: well-documented in monitoring, but human RCT of dark-chocolate restriction lowering blood Cd not done. Magnitude estimates from food-frequency × content modeling.
- **Tea polyphenol Cd binding**: mechanistic in vitro; minimal human evidence.
- **Cilantro / chlorella / oral GSH / NAC / modified citrus pectin / zeolite**: persistently marketed as Cd "chelators." **No human RCT supports blood/urine Cd reduction.** Treat as **[no-evidence]**.
- **EDTA chelation for Cd**: contraindicated in chronic exposure (Cd-EDTA complex is itself nephrotoxic; Cantilena 1982 animal data; ATSDR caution). Even in acute poisoning, efficacy is limited because Cd is bound to metallothionein in kidney too tightly for EDTA to extract.
- **DMSA / DMPS for Cd**: limited efficacy; not standard of care.
- **Sauna fractional excretion**: real but small.
- **Long-term low-level Cd cardiovascular and CKD signal**: Tellez-Plaza 2012 *EHP* (NHANES) showed urinary Cd at 0.8 µg/g cr associated with HR 1.43 for CV mortality vs 0.2 µg/g cr — meaningful at population level but not individually actionable beyond smoking cessation + Fe sufficiency + dietary moderation.
- **Bone Cd / osteoporosis**: chronic Cd disrupts bone mineralization (itai-itai disease historical; modern OSCAR study Sweden). At low US background levels, magnitude of bone effect is debated.
- **Vape product Cd content**: variable across products; analytical chemistry surveys ongoing. Not as well-characterized as cigarette Cd.
- **Marijuana Cd**: cannabis is a documented Cd hyperaccumulator; chronic users have measurably higher blood Cd. Often overlooked in "natural product" framing.

- Cited primary literature (PubMed PMID where available); FDA / EPA / ATSDR / EFSA / OSHA official documents.
