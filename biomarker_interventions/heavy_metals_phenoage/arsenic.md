# Arsenic (Total / Speciated Urine or Blood As) — Intervention Research

> Inorganic arsenic (iAs — As(III) and As(V)) is the toxicologically relevant species; **organic arsenic** species in seafood (arsenobetaine, arsenocholine) are largely non-toxic and pass through unchanged. The single highest-leverage intervention is **identifying and eliminating the source**: contaminated well water (US EPA MCL: 10 µg/L; WHO provisional guideline: 10 µg/L), rice and rice-based products (especially brown rice, rice cereals, brown rice syrup, infant rice cereals), and certain occupational/contaminated-soil exposures. Cooking technique modifications for rice (rinse 4–6×, cook with 6:1 water:rice ratio, drain excess) reduce iAs by **30–60%** (Carey 2015 PLOS One; FDA 2016 inorganic As guidance). White basmati / jasmine rice contains roughly half the iAs of brown rice. Folate / B12 / methionine sufficiency supports endogenous As methylation and excretion (Gamble 2006 *AJCN*). Marketed As "detox" supplements (chlorella, cilantro, oral glutathione, "metal-binding" zeolite) have **no controlled human evidence** of lowering blood/urine As — flagged throughout as **[no-evidence]**. Pharmacologic chelation (DMSA, BAL) is reserved for symptomatic acute poisoning, not for routine dietary As.

**Cross-link:** Arsenic is not yet covered in the biology files at the repo root (the FH panel covers Pb and Hg). Reference for biology / clinical interpretation: ATSDR Toxicological Profile for Arsenic (2007/updates), FDA inorganic arsenic in rice guidance (2016/2020), and EPA arsenic in drinking water rule. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs / cohorts |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation; no direct human RCT |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial worth knowing |
| **[no-evidence]** | Marketed claim with no controlled human evidence |

Population caveat: most quantitative epidemiology comes from high-exposure regions (Bangladesh, West Bengal, parts of Chile, Taiwan, Inner Mongolia, US Southwest) where well water As >100 µg/L is common. In US adults at background exposure (geometric mean total urine As ~7 µg/L; iAs typically <5 µg/L) the dynamic range is small.

## 3. Supplements

### Lowering / improving (evidence-supported)

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Folic acid | 400–800 µg/d | In Bangladeshi folate-deficient adults: blood As ↓ ~14% over 12 weeks via enhanced methylation; effect smaller in folate-replete US adults | 8–12 weeks | **[RCT]** | Gamble et al. 2006 *AJCN* PMID 16687477 (FACT trial: Folic Acid and Creatine Trial); Peters 2015 *Curr Environ Health Rep* review | Most relevant in folate-deficient subgroups. In US adults with adequate dietary folate (esp. since 1998 fortification), incremental benefit small. |
| Vitamin B12 (if deficient) | 250–1000 µg/d cyanocobalamin or methylcobalamin | Mechanism: supports methionine cycle and methyl-donor pool for As methylation; small As ↓ in deficient subjects | Weeks–months | **[Mechanism + small trials]** | Bangladesh FACT II trial (Howe 2017 *Environ Health Perspect*) | Replete dietary B12 first. | Selenium (organic Se from food; Se-yeast) | 100–200 µg/d | Mechanism: forms biliary As-Se complex; small Bangladeshi trials inconclusive on urinary As change | Weeks | **[1-Trial + Mechanism]** | George 2013 *J Nutr Biochem* (rat); George 2014 *Environ Health Perspect* (Bangladeshi pilot) | Stay ≤200 µg/d unless deficient — selenosis at >400 µg/d. |
| N-acetylcysteine (NAC) | 600–1200 mg/d | Mechanism: precursor to GSH; theoretical As-GSH conjugation enhancement; **no robust human As-lowering RCT** | — | **[Mechanism only]** | Animal data (Pamphlett 1994 *Toxicology*); no robust human RCT for body-burden As | NAC has legitimate uses (acetaminophen overdose, mucolytic). | Methionine (SAMe-related amino acid) | Adequate dietary intake (eggs, dairy, meat, legumes, sesame seeds, Brazil nuts) | Provides methyl donors for As methylation pathway | Continuous | **[Mechanism]** | Methionine cycle biochemistry | Diet-level intake is sufficient; supplementation rarely needed in non-restrictive diets. |

## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

Like other heavy metals, blood/urine As has **no established direct relationship with wearable-tracked physiological metrics**.

| Wearable metric | Direction of relationship | Magnitude / effect size | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dietary tracking apps — rice / grain consumption frequency, brown vs white | Direct; predictive of urinary iAs | Each daily serving of brown rice / brown rice product ≈ +5–15 µg/L urine iAs at steady state | **[Cohort + Mechanism]** | Davis 2017 *JAMA Pediatr*; Gilbert-Diamond 2011 *EHP* (US population rice-As) | Logging rice species + frequency in a tracking app is the actionable layer. |
| GPS / location data (proximity to former / current mining, smelting, ore processing, US Southwest groundwater regions) | Possible exposure ↑ | USGS arsenic in groundwater map shows hotspot regions | **[Cohort / GIS]** | USGS National Water Quality Assessment | Indirect; relevant if on private well in known-As region. |
| Sauna minutes | Marginal urine As ↑ via sweat (small) | Effect size minor | **[Mechanism]** | Genuis 2011 | Not a meaningful primary intervention. |

The actionable tracking layer for As is **dietary log of rice / grain choices + private well water testing**, not a wearable physiological signal.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Depends on the dominant exposure pathway. For private-well households: **install NSF/ANSI 58 RO or NSF 53 As-certified water filter** if well water As >5 µg/L. For municipal-water households with rice-heavy diet: **switch from brown rice to white basmati + adopt rinse-and-drain cooking technique**. For US infants: **switch from rice cereal to oat / barley / multigrain cereal**.
- **Best stack of 3:** (1) Test private well water (one-time); install RO filter if >5 µg/L. (2) Rice strategy: substitute oats / quinoa / wheat for rice as primary starch when possible; for rice meals, choose white basmati or jasmine + rinse 4–6× + cook with 6:1 water + drain. (3) Folate / B12 repletion via diet (leafy greens, legumes, eggs, dairy, fortified grains).
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (urine total As <10 µg/L, primarily organic from occasional seafood):** Essentially no actionable change. iAs at population floor; assay variance dominates. Use speciation to confirm organic vs inorganic dominance.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for someone with elevated baseline (e.g., urine iAs 25 µg/L from daily brown rice + private well water As 20 µg/L):** RO water filter + rice substitution → urine iAs ~10 µg/L at 4–8 weeks, ~5 µg/L at 12 weeks. Most of the change reflects half-life of As in urine (~4 days for iAs / MMA / DMA after exposure ends).
- **Realistic 12-week delta for an SA male with daily rice + Ayurvedic supplement use and urine total As 50 µg/L:** Stop Ayurvedic supplement (audit first), switch to white basmati with rinse-and-drain, request speciated As at follow-up to confirm iAs vs organic dominance → urine iAs ↓ to 5–15 µg/L by 12 weeks if Ayurvedic was the dominant source.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** **Symptomatic acute / subacute As poisoning** (GI symptoms, encephalopathy, peripheral neuropathy, garlic breath, Mees lines, hyperkeratosis) — DMSA (succimer, oral) or DMPS (compounded; not FDA-approved in US) under medical toxicology supervision. **For asymptomatic dietary / environmental As at any level, chelation is NOT indicated.** Source elimination + nutritional support is the standard approach.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Rice is a cultural staple** for SA / East / Southeast Asian populations. Per-capita rice consumption is 5–10× higher than US Western average. Even with white basmati + rinse-drain cooking, total iAs intake from rice is meaningful.
- **Indian / Pakistani / Thai-grown basmati and jasmine rice are among the lowest-iAs rice categories** in FDA monitoring data. **US-grown rice (Texas, Arkansas, Louisiana — historical pesticide-As legacy in paddies) is among the higher-iAs categories.** Choose origin accordingly.
- **Bangladeshi / West Bengali tube-well water** remains a major global iAs exposure crisis (HEALS cohort: Argos 2010 *Lancet*, Chen 2011 *BMJ*); SA users with family history of well-water exposure may carry historical As burden.
- **Ayurvedic / rasa shastra preparations** are a documented major As source (some intentionally contain haratal — As sulfide). Saper 2008 *JAMA*: ~21% of US/India-sourced Ayurvedic pills contained Pb, Hg, or As. Audit Ayurvedic / Unani / Siddha use first.
- **Vegetarian SA males** with low B12 / folate may have impaired As methylation → higher tissue retention. Maintain B12 ≥400 pg/mL and dietary folate ≥400 µg/d.
- **Infant weaning practices** with rice-based first foods (idli batter, rice porridge, brown rice cereal) — substitute oat / barley / multigrain options.
- **AS3MT genetic variants** more frequent in some SA populations may modify methylation efficiency (see §8).

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **AS3MT (arsenite methyltransferase) polymorphisms** (rs10748835, rs3740393, rs7085104, rs11191439): modulate As methylation efficiency. Variants associated with higher MMA% (less efficient methylation) → higher tissue retention and higher disease risk per unit exposure (Engström 2011 *EHP*; Pierce 2012 *PLOS Genet*). Andean populations show signatures of selection for protective AS3MT haplotypes after millennia of high-As exposure (Schlebusch 2015 *Mol Biol Evol*).
- **GSTM1 / GSTT1 deletions**: GSH-conjugation efficiency; modest effect on As methylation phenotype.
- **MTHFR C677T**: affects folate metabolism / methyl donor pool → modulates As methylation (Steinmaus 2007 *Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev*). TT genotype with low folate intake may have impaired As methylation.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Folate / B12 supplementation** (in deficient individuals): enhances methylation, lowers blood As (Gamble 2006).
- **Pharmacologic chelators** (DMSA, DMPS): only for symptomatic poisoning.
- **Arsenic trioxide for APL**: by definition raises blood/urine As during treatment (therapeutic; not relevant to environmental As).

**Confounding lifestyle factors:**
- **Recent seafood consumption**: a single shellfish / seaweed meal can elevate "total urine As" 5–10× via non-toxic organic As (arsenobetaine). **Standardize: avoid seafood for 3–7 days before draw OR request speciated As (iAs + MMA + DMA)**.
- **Rice consumption frequency**: brown rice and rice-based gluten-free products are major iAs sources; document frequency.
- **Private well vs municipal water**: most US municipalities meet 10 µg/L MCL; ~10% of US private wells exceed.
- **Smoking**: ↑ urinary As ~10–30%.
- **Folate / B12 status**: deficiency impairs methylation, raises tissue As at same exposure.

**Timing artifacts:**
- iAs / MMA / DMA urinary half-lives: ~4 days post-exposure; reflects 3–7 day exposure window.
- Whole blood As: reflects ~2–4 week window.
- Hair / nail As: integrate over months.
- Organic As (arsenobetaine) from seafood: peaks 4–48 h post-meal, eliminated within 72 h.

**Sample integrity:**
- Trace metals require strict pre-analytic discipline (royal-blue-top tube, no rubber stoppers).
- **Speciated urine As is the gold standard** (iAs + MMA + DMA); total urine As confounded by seafood organic As.
- Spot vs 24-h urine: spot urine is acceptable for screening; creatinine-normalize for variability.

**Distinction: total urine As vs speciated As:**
- Total urine As high but speciated MMA + iAs + DMA low → likely organic (seafood). Reassuring.
- Speciated iAs + MMA elevated → real toxicological exposure; investigate source.

## 9. Open Questions / Weak Evidence

- **Folate effect at US background exposure**: Bangladesh FACT trial showed clear blood As ↓ with folic acid in folate-deficient adults; effect in folate-replete US adults (post-1998 fortification) is much smaller and not robustly quantified.
- **Selenium for As body burden**: mechanism (biliary As-Se complex) is established in animal models; small Bangladeshi pilot (George 2014 *EHP*) inconclusive on urinary As change.
- **NAC for As**: mechanistic story; no robust human As-lowering RCT.
- **Cilantro / chlorella / oral GSH / spirulina / modified citrus pectin / zeolite**: persistently marketed as As "chelators." **No human RCT supports blood/urine As reduction.** Treat as **[no-evidence]**.
- **Sauna fractional excretion**: real but small.
- **"Chelation challenge" provocation tests** (DMSA / DMPS challenge with subsequent urinary As measurement, marketed by integrative practitioners): unvalidated; produces artifactually elevated urinary As in essentially everyone after challenge. ACMT position statement against this practice.
- **At population floor (urine iAs <5 µg/L)**: assay imprecision and within-person variability dominate; trends require multiple draws.
- **Long-term low-level As cardiovascular signal**: Strong Heart Study (Moon 2013 *Ann Intern Med*) showed CV mortality association at urinary As 10–60 µg/g creatinine in American Indians. Causal interpretation at US-background levels (<10 µg/L water) debated.
- **Brown rice nutritional trade-off**: brown rice has higher fiber, B vitamins, magnesium, manganese than white. The iAs penalty must be weighed against the nutritional benefit. For very heavy rice consumers (>1 cup cooked/d), white basmati + diversified grain plate is often the better integrated trade-off than 100% brown rice.
- **As speciation in commercial labs**: not all labs report speciated As by default; insurance coverage variable. For accurate trending, request speciation explicitly.
