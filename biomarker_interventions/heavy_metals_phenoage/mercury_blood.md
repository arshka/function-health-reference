# Mercury (Whole Blood Total Hg) — Intervention Research

> Whole-blood Hg in adults is dominated (~70–75%) by **methylmercury (MeHg) from large predator fish** (Caldwell 2014 *Environ Res* NHANES 2011–12). The single highest-leverage intervention is **eliminating top-tier predator fish** (shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish, bigeye tuna) and substituting low-Hg / high-omega-3 species (salmon, sardines, anchovies, herring, trout). Blood MeHg has a half-life of ~50–80 days, so dietary change is visible in 2–4 months. **Selenium** mitigates MeHg toxicity at the tissue level (Brazil nuts, ocean fish) without lowering blood Hg directly. Inorganic Hg from dental amalgam contributes a small steady-state increment to urinary Hg; **amalgam removal is NOT a Hg-reduction strategy** — the drilling process transiently spikes Hg vapor exposure (Dunn 2008). Marketed Hg "detox" supplements (chlorella, cilantro, oral GSH/NAC, "metal-binding" zeolite) have **no controlled human evidence** of lowering blood Hg — flagged throughout as **[no-evidence]**. Pharmacologic chelation (DMSA / DMPS) is reserved for symptomatic acute poisoning, not for routine dietary Hg.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../heavy_metals_and_biological_age.md` §2 and `../../effect_sizes_hematology_other.md` §Mercury. This file is INTERVENTION-only — what moves the marker, by how much, in how long, with what evidence.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs / cohorts |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation; no direct human RCT |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial worth knowing |
| **[no-evidence]** | Marketed claim with no controlled human evidence |

Population caveat: Asian Americans run ~2× higher MeHg geometric mean than the overall US population (1.58 vs 0.50 µg/L; Caldwell 2014) — driven by higher fish frequency, not by physiology. Effects are quantified relative to baseline; floor effects shrink magnitudes in lean adults at near-optimal baseline.

## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

Like lead, blood Hg has **no established direct relationship with wearable-tracked physiological metrics** (HR, HRV, VO₂max, sleep, steps). Exposure is dietary / occupational / environmental and tracks with food choices and location, not physiology.

| Wearable metric | Direction of relationship | Magnitude / effect size | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dietary tracking apps (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, Lose It) — fish-meal frequency | Direct; predictive of blood Hg | Population correlation: each weekly serving of high-Hg fish ≈ +1–3 µg/L blood Hg at steady state | **[Cohort + Mechanism]** | Mahaffey 2009 NHANES (women eating ≥9 fish meals/30 days had 7× blood Hg); FDA Hg ppm × meal frequency | Logging fish species + frequency in a tracking app + weekly Hg-burden estimator is the actionable wearable layer. |
| Sauna minutes (Garmin / Apple HealthKit / Oura) | Marginally lowers blood Hg via sweat | Small effect (~few percent of urinary Hg output via sweat) | **[Mechanism]** | Genuis 2011 | Not a meaningful primary intervention for Hg. |
| GPS / location data (proximity to chlor-alkali, gold mining, coal-fired power plant fallout zones) | Possible exposure ↑ | Coal-fired power plant Hg deposition is regional; near-source residence may slightly elevate ambient inorganic Hg | **[Cohort / GIS]** | EPA Mercury and Air Toxics Standards modeling | Indirect; minor effect for most US residents. |

The actionable tracking layer for Hg is **dietary log of fish species + frequency**, not a physiological wearable signal. Periodic blood Hg retesting (every 6–12 months for high-fish eaters) is the verification step.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** **Eliminate top-tier predator fish** (shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish (Gulf), bigeye / bluefin tuna, marlin) and limit albacore / yellowfin tuna to ≤1 serving/wk. Substitute salmon, sardines, anchovies, herring, trout, mackerel (Pacific/Atlantic, NOT king), shrimp, oysters. Blood Hg drops with ~50-day MeHg half-life; expect ~50% reduction at 50 d, ~75% at 100 d, ~90% at 150 d from heavy-predator-fish baseline.
- **Best stack of 3:** (1) Predator-fish elimination + low-Hg high-omega-3 substitution, (2) Selenium repletion via 1–2 Brazil nuts/d or 100–200 µg Se from food (improves Hg:Se ratio toxicologically), (3) Dietary tracking app to log fish species + frequency for ongoing self-monitoring.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (blood Hg 0.5–1.0 µg/L, low-fish diet):** Essentially no actionable change. Hg is at population floor; assay imprecision (~±0.3 µg/L) dominates.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for someone with elevated baseline (e.g., blood Hg 8 µg/L from weekly albacore/bigeye consumption):** Predator-fish elimination + skipjack/salmon substitution → blood Hg ~4 µg/L at 50 d, ~2 µg/L at 100 d, <1.5 µg/L by 150 d (6 months).
- **Realistic 12-week delta for an Asian-American SA male with daily sushi habit and blood Hg 5–10 µg/L:** Switch sushi orders to salmon/eel/shrimp/mackerel saba (skip tuna varieties), cook tuna at home with skipjack only → blood Hg ↓ ~50% per 50-day half-life; expect ~50% reduction at 60 d, ~75% by 4 months.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** **Symptomatic acute MeHg poisoning** (paresthesias, cerebellar ataxia, dysarthria, visual field constriction) at blood Hg roughly >50–200 µg/L — DMSA (succimer, oral) under medical toxicology supervision. **For asymptomatic dietary MeHg of any level, chelation is NOT indicated.** For inorganic Hg (occupational, dental, skin-lightening cream): DMPS (compounded; not FDA-approved in US). **Do NOT undertake DIY "chelation challenge" tests** — they are unvalidated, marketed by integrative practitioners, and cause artifactual urinary Hg elevations.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Asian Americans run ~2× higher MeHg geometric mean** than the overall US adult population (1.58 vs 0.50 µg/L; Caldwell 2014 *Environ Res* NHANES 2011–12) — driven by higher fish frequency. An SA / East Asian / Pacific Islander 20yM with blood Hg 1.5 µg/L is at population median for that subgroup; >5 µg/L is more concerning.
- **High-yield SA dietary patterns:** sushi / poke / seared tuna culture (yellowfin, bluefin, bigeye) → switch to salmon nigiri, eel (unagi), shrimp tempura, mackerel saba, tako (octopus), ikura (salmon roe). Indian / Bangladeshi rui (rohu) and other freshwater carp are generally low Hg.
- **Ayurvedic / rasa shastra preparations** are a documented major Hg source (some intentionally contain bhasmas — calcined Hg compounds). Saper 2008 *JAMA*: ~21% of US/India-sourced Ayurvedic pills contained Pb, Hg, or As. Audit any Ayurvedic / Unani / Siddha supplement use first in SA male with unexpected Hg elevation.
- **Skin-lightening creams** imported via SA / East Asian / African informal supply chains often contain Hg salts (calomel = Hg chloride). FDA import alerts list specific brands.
- **Mehndi / kohl / surma cosmetics** rarely contain Hg (more often Pb, antimony) but worth screening.
- **Selenium status in vegetarian SA males**: Brazil nuts (1–2/d) provide enough Se to maintain Se:Hg ratio buffer.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **Selenoprotein gene variants** (SEPP1, GPX1) may modify tissue Se-Hg interaction at the toxicodynamic level; not actionable individually. No well-validated SNPs robustly modify MeHg pharmacokinetics at clinically relevant levels.
- **GSTM1 / GSTT1 deletions** (glutathione S-transferase): may modestly affect MeHg conjugation and elimination; effect size in epidemiology is small and not actionable for individual recommendations.
- **MRP2 (ABCC2) variants**: affect biliary GSH-MeHg conjugate excretion; mostly research-level data.

**Drug interactions:**
- **N-acetylcysteine (NAC)**: theoretical mechanism for raising tissue GSH; no human blood-Hg-lowering RCT.
- **Pharmacologic chelators** (DMSA, DMPS): only for symptomatic acute poisoning; outside this indication causes essential metal depletion (Zn, Cu) without meaningful body-burden Hg reduction.
- **Dental procedures** (amalgam drilling, scaling): transient Hg vapor spike during procedure; steady-state effect minimal. Use rubber dam during amalgam removal.

**Confounding lifestyle factors:**
- **Recent fish consumption**: a single high-Hg fish meal 2–3 days before draw can roughly double background blood Hg in a low-baseline person. **Standardize: avoid predator fish for 7+ days before draw** for accurate trough.
- **Total Hg vs MeHg fraction**: in NHANES, MeHg is ~70–75% of total. If total is high but MeHg fraction is low, suspect dental amalgam (inorganic) or occupational/environmental inorganic exposure — request speciated whole-blood Hg test.
- **Selenium status**: high dietary Se (Brazil nuts, ocean fish, organ meats, eggs) reduces Hg toxicity at the tissue level even at the same blood Hg.
- **Hair color treatment**: can affect hair Hg measurement (not blood); use blood Hg for trending if hair-dyed.
- **Body composition**: MeHg distributes preferentially to muscle and brain; lean mass differences modestly shift kinetics.
- **Pregnancy / lactation**: MeHg crosses placenta with concentration ratio ~1.5× (fetal:maternal); breast milk MeHg is lower than maternal blood.

**Timing artifacts:**
- Blood MeHg t½ ~50 days → reflects ~2–3 month exposure window.
- Hair Hg integrates over ~3 months per centimeter (hair:blood ratio ~250:1 at steady state).
- Urine Hg reflects inorganic / elemental exposure (industrial, dental amalgam, gold mining) — NOT MeHg.

**Sample integrity:**
- Trace metals require strict pre-analytic discipline (royal-blue-top tube, no rubber stoppers).
- Whole blood for Hg (not serum / plasma) — Hg partitions strongly to RBCs.

## 9. Open Questions / Weak Evidence

- **"Chelation challenge" provocation tests** (DMSA/DMPS challenge with subsequent urinary Hg measurement, marketed by integrative / functional medicine practitioners): unvalidated; produces artifactually elevated urinary Hg in essentially everyone after challenge. AAP, ACMT (American College of Medical Toxicology), and AACT have all published position statements against this practice. Do not interpret a "high post-DMSA urinary Hg" as evidence of body-burden Hg or as indication for ongoing chelation.
- **Cilantro / chlorella / oral GSH / NAC / ALA / modified citrus pectin / zeolite / fulvic acid**: persistently marketed as Hg "chelators." **No human RCT supports blood Hg reduction.** Mechanistic stories at best. Treat as **[no-evidence]**.
- **Sauna fractional excretion**: real but small (~few percent of urinary Hg output). Marketed as "detox" but body-burden impact is minimal.
- **Selenium dose-response for Hg toxicity buffering**: 1–2 Brazil nuts/d is mechanistically plausible; precise dose-response not RCT-defined.
- **Fish oil for omega-3 substitution**: distillation removes Hg; well-validated. Some products still contain detectable Hg — choose IFOS / USP-verified.
- **Amalgam removal Hg-spike duration**: well-documented acute increase; long-term effect minimal but variable. Use rubber dam + high-volume suction during removal.
- **MeHg-to-cardiovascular-disease causal pathway**: Salonen 2005 *ATVB* Eastern Finnish men cohort showed CV mortality signal at high freshwater-pike-derived MeHg. The signal does not consistently appear in ocean-fish-eating cohorts (where Se buffer differs). Pure MeHg cardiac risk is debated; net cardiovascular effect of fish consumption is favorable in most populations because of omega-3 / vitamin D / Se co-loading.
- **At population floor (blood Hg <1 µg/L)**: assay imprecision dominates; trends require multiple draws over 6–12 months for any inference.
- **Asian American baseline**: 2× higher than overall US; the "concerning" threshold may need to be calibrated to subgroup distribution rather than the overall population reference.
