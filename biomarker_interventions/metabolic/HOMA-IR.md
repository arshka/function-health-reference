# HOMA-IR — Intervention Research

> **HOMA-IR is a derived metric**: HOMA-IR = (FPG mg/dL × insulin µIU/mL) / 405. Anything that moves FPG or fasting insulin moves HOMA-IR — usually with insulin as the dominant driver in early IR. **Highest leverage**: combined aerobic + resistance exercise (HOMA-IR −0.47 to −1.05 in 12 wk, partly independent of weight loss); 5–10% weight loss (HOMA-IR −0.5 to −1.5). Low-carb diet (−1.0 to −2.0 in 12–24 wk) — fastest acute response. Inositol gives largest single-supplement effect (HOMA-IR −1.96 WMD) but mostly in PCOS women. **In a near-optimal lean young male (HOMA-IR <1.2), interventions move the needle <0.3 — assay noise dominates.** **South Asian threshold**: HOMA-IR >1.5 in lean SA male warrants attention even if lab "normal" is <2.7.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / interpretation lives in `../../metabolic_panel.md` §3.2 (HOMA-IR formula and cutoffs) and `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §"Fasting Insulin & HOMA-IR". This file extends with deeper food/supplement coverage on the upstream interventions that move both glucose and insulin. Companion files: `fasting_glucose.md` and `fasting_insulin.md` cover individual upstream moves. **Most of the content here repeats the upstream files because HOMA-IR has no independent intervention pathway — it is a ratio.** What's specific to this file: which interventions move both numerator and denominator coordinately, and how to interpret HOMA-IR-only changes.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs with consistent direction |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational; can't establish causality |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization — genetic proxies estimating causal effect |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation; no direct human RCT for *this specific effect* |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial worth knowing but not yet replicated |

Population caveat: most cited evidence is from overweight/obese, prediabetic, or T2D populations. Effect sizes shrink in lean young adults at near-optimal baseline (HOMA-IR <1.2). South Asian–specific threshold framework critical here.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** (in elevated baseline HOMA-IR ≥2.0): combined aerobic + resistance exercise → HOMA-IR −0.5 to −1.0 over 12 wk, with effect amplified by simultaneous weight loss.
- **Best stack of 3** (additive but with diminishing returns):
  1. ≥150 min/wk aerobic + 2× resistance training (HOMA-IR −0.5 to −1.0)
  2. Mediterranean or low-carb diet (HOMA-IR −0.65 to −2.0)
  3. 5–10% weight loss (especially visceral fat) + sleep regularization (7–8 hr regular timing)
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (HOMA-IR <1.0): 0 to −0.2 (within assay noise; insulin assay variability dominates).
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for someone with elevated baseline (HOMA-IR 2.0–3.5): −0.7 to −1.5 with full stack adherence.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**: Metformin (HOMA-IR ↓ modestly via reduced hepatic glucose output and modest peripheral effect), pioglitazone (HOMA-IR ↓↓ — most potent oral insulin sensitizer; weight gain limits use), GLP-1 agonists (HOMA-IR ↓↓ via weight loss + direct β-cell effects). Pharmacotherapy enters when prediabetes/T2D criteria met or central adiposity / NAFLD / family history with HOMA-IR persistently >2.5 despite lifestyle.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **HOMA-IR shifted higher in SA at every BMI**: SA have 30–50% higher HOMA-IR vs Europeans at identical BMI ([Kelly West Lecture 2024 *Diabetes Care*](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/47/1/7/154008/Diabetes-in-South-Asians-Uncovering-Novel-Risk)). Functional threshold "insulin resistant" in lean SA 20M is lower; **HOMA-IR >1.5 in lean SA male warrants scrutiny** even if lab "normal" is <2.7.
- **Tang 2015 (Asian)**: HOMA-IR ~2.0–2.5 cutoff, lower in Han Chinese ([Tang 2015 *Drug Discov Ther* PubMed 26781921](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26781921/)). South Korean general adult: 2.2–2.5 ([Korean DMJ](https://www.e-dmj.org/journal/view.php?number=561)).
- **Visceral fat / TOFI ("thin outside fat inside") phenotype**: common in SA at normal BMI. Drives HOMA-IR elevation at "normal weight." Consider DEXA for VAT / subcutaneous ratio if borderline HOMA-IR.
- **Fructose-rich SA dietary patterns** (mango lassi, sweetened chai, sweet desserts, fruit at every meal): contribute to HOMA-IR elevation independent of total caloric load. Eliminating SSB and limiting dessert frequency produces visible CGM TIR improvement within days.
- **White rice substitution**: highest-leverage SA dietary lever for HOMA-IR.
- **Lentils/dal**: protective traditional component — increase frequency.
- **TCF7L2 risk allele** (~30–35% SA): impaired GLP-1 → blunted incretin-mediated insulin reduction with food order intervention. Magnitude of food-order benefit may be smaller in TCF7L2 carriers.
- **Lean MASLD workup** if HOMA-IR elevated in lean SA male: ALT, GGT, ferritin, ultrasound. PNPLA3 rs738409 GG carriers higher liver fat at any BMI.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**HOMA-IR inherits all the assay noise of fasting insulin** plus the daily variability of FPG. See `fasting_insulin.md` §8 and `fasting_glucose.md` §8 for full confounders. Specific to HOMA-IR:

**The two-source noise problem**: HOMA-IR multiplies two noisy measures. Inter-lab CV for fasting insulin 15–30%; for FPG 2–4%. Combined CV for HOMA-IR ≈ 16–32%. **A change of <20% in HOMA-IR over time is usually within noise.**

**Key interpretive principle**: HOMA-IR can move even when neither component clearly moves outside its individual reference range. A change from FPG 90 + insulin 6 (HOMA-IR 1.33) to FPG 95 + insulin 8 (HOMA-IR 1.88) represents a real 41% increase in HOMA-IR — both components within "normal" but the ratio meaningfully changed. **Trend matters more than single value.**

**Genetic modifiers** (same as fasting insulin, but worth restating):
- **TCF7L2 rs7903146**: largest common-variant T2D risk; impaired GLP-1.
- **MTNR1B rs10830963**: elevated FPG, reduced first-phase insulin secretion.
- **PPARG Pro12Ala**: modifies pioglitazone response.
- **GCK (MODY-2)**: stable mild fasting hyperglycemia with normal insulin secretion — HOMA-IR may be deceptively elevated but β-cell function is normal at higher set-point.
- **HFE C282Y**: iron overload can raise both FPG and insulin → HOMA-IR ↑.

**Drug interactions** (a draw on these will mislead HOMA-IR):
- **Glucocorticoids**: raise both FPG and insulin → HOMA-IR ↑↑↑.
- **Atypical antipsychotics**: raise both via weight gain + direct.
- **Statins, niacin, β-blockers, thiazides**: small ↑ both → small HOMA-IR ↑.
- **Tacrolimus, cyclosporine, sirolimus**: variable.
- **Caffeine acute**: raises both FPG and insulin acutely → HOMA-IR ↑.
- **Acute exercise within 24–48 h**: usually lowers both → HOMA-IR ↓.
- **High-dose biotin**: doesn't affect biology, but **breaks insulin immunoassays**; computed HOMA-IR meaningless.

**Pre-analytical** (compound errors from FPG and insulin):
- **Truly fasting ≥10 h** required.
- **Coffee morning of draw**: avoid.
- **Recent intense exercise (within 48 h)**: usually lowers HOMA-IR.
- **Recent illness**: defer non-urgent draw 1–2 wk.
- **Same-lab same-assay** for serial monitoring.
- **Pulsatile insulin secretion**: single fasting draw catches one trough/peak in a 10–15 min cycle; CV across consecutive draws can be 20–30%.

**Alternative IR markers when HOMA-IR is unreliable**:
- **TG/HDL ratio**: surrogate; <1.5 optimal (Caucasian); higher acceptable in some Asian populations. Poor in African American populations.
- **Triglyceride-glucose index (TyG)** = ln[TG (mg/dL) × FPG (mg/dL)/2]. Less assay-dependent than HOMA-IR; cutoff ~8.5.
- **C-peptide-based** (HOMA2-IR uses C-peptide alongside insulin): preferable in cases of exogenous insulin or anti-insulin antibodies.
- **Adiponectin**: inversely correlated; not commonly in standard panels.
- **CGM data**: 2-week burst gives PPG and TIR, more granular than HOMA-IR.
- **OGTT with paired insulin (Kraft test)**: detects dynamic dysfunction missed by static HOMA-IR.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **HOMA-IR optimal cutoff in lean SA male**: <1.0 widely cited as "optimal" but not RCT-validated. Practical floor around 0.8–1.2 in lean SA young adults.
- **HOMA-IR vs HOMA2 vs Matsuda Index**: HOMA1 (the original Matthews 1985 formula) is what most labs report. HOMA2 (1998 update) requires online calculator and slightly different cutoffs (~1.8 in Caucasian). Matsuda Index (from OGTT) more sensitive for early dysfunction but rarely run.
- **Inositol in non-PCOS men**: most data PCOS women; effect in lean SA men less characterized but mechanism applies.
- **Cinnamon, chromium for HOMA-IR**: largely null in well-designed trials.
- **Berberine ancestry transportability**: most data Chinese populations.
- **TRE / IF effect on HOMA-IR independent of CR**: most pooled effect overlaps caloric restriction.
- **CGM-guided behavioral interventions for chronic HOMA-IR reduction**: reliably improve PPG/TIR; long-term HOMA-IR reduction at near-optimal baseline not RCT-proven.
- **Whether HOMA-IR <1.0 is a meaningful target** in lean young SA males with no other risk factors: optimization framing; not regulatory-grade.
- **Cold exposure / sauna for HOMA-IR**: pilot data in T2D (van der Lans 2013 — insulin sensitivity ↑ 43%); not yet replicated at scale in healthy young.
- **Pioglitazone in nondiabetic for IR**: ACT NOW trial showed prevention but at cost of weight gain, fluid retention, bone loss, bladder cancer signal. Not standard-of-care for nondiabetic.
- **Whether HOMA-IR or fasting insulin alone is preferable** as a single marker: debated; HOMA-IR has slightly better correlation with clamp-derived IR but inherits FPG noise.

## Source format conventions

- Cite primary literature (PubMed PMID where available) over secondary sources.
- Prefer meta-analyses → large RCTs → cohorts → mechanism papers.
- Use *Journal Year;Vol(Issue):Pages* format. Include PMID or DOI when available.
