# Fasting Glucose — Intervention Research

> **Highest leverage**: sustained 5–10% weight loss (≈1–2 mg/dL per kg lost) and ≥150 min/wk aerobic exercise produce the largest sustained reductions (−10 to −20 mg/dL combined, 8–16 wk). Carbohydrate restriction gives the fastest acute drops (−10 to −15 mg/dL in 1–4 wk) — partly via glycogen depletion. **In a near-optimal lean young male (FPG 75–85 mg/dL), the floor is biology — most interventions move the needle <5 mg/dL.** Pharmacotherapy (metformin, GLP-1, SGLT2i) provides scale that lifestyle cannot match in T2D.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../metabolic_panel.md` §1 (Glucose) and the cross-cutting effect-size summary in `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §"Fasting Glucose". This file is INTERVENTION-only — what moves the marker, by how much, in how long, with what evidence. Coverage extends to postprandial / glycemic-variability behaviors that don't show up in the standard fasting draw but predict 3-month A1c and CGM time-in-range.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs with consistent direction |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational; can't establish causality |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization — genetic proxies estimating causal effect |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation; no direct human RCT for *this specific effect* |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial worth knowing but not yet replicated |

Population caveat: most cited evidence is from NW European adults with elevated baseline. Floor effects shrink magnitudes ~30–60% in lean healthy young adults at near-optimal baseline (FPG 75–90). South Asian–specific data flagged where it exists.

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Oats (β-glucan) | 3 g/d β-glucan (~60–70 g dry oats) | FPG −2 to −5 mg/dL; A1c −0.13 to −0.21% | 4–8 wk | **[Meta]** | Whitehead, *Am J Clin Nutr* 2014; Ho, *Br J Nutr* 2016 (PMC5394769) | Effect plateaus above ~5 g/d β-glucan; viscosity/gel formation is the mechanism. | Barley (β-glucan) | 3–8 g/d β-glucan | FPG −5 to −10 mg/dL in T2D and prediabetes | 4–12 wk | **[Meta]** | AbuMweis, *Eur J Clin Nutr* 2010; Tosh, *Eur J Clin Nutr* 2013 | Same gel-forming β-glucan mechanism as oats; insoluble bran fraction adds satiety. |

| Legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans) | ≥1 cup/d (~30 g/d resistant starch + soluble fiber) | FPG −0.8 mmol/L (~−14 mg/dL) at 3 mo in T2D RCT (Jenkins 2012) | 8–12 wk | **[RCT/Meta]** | Jenkins, *Arch Intern Med* 2012 (PMID 23089999); Sievenpiper, *Diabetologia* 2009 meta (41 RCTs) | Effect attenuates with longer follow-up if displacement of refined carbs not maintained. | Vegetables eaten first (food order) | Vegetables/protein ≥10 min before carbs | PPG iAUC −73%; insulin iAUC −49% | Acute | **[RCT]** | Shukla, *Diabetes Care* 2015 (PMC4876745); Shukla, *BMJ Open Diabetes Res Care* 2017 (PMC5604719); Shukla, *Diabetes Obes Metab* 2019 (PMC7398578) | Mechanism: incretin (GLP-1) augmentation + delayed gastric emptying. | Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) | 2+ servings/wk (~250 mg EPA+DHA/d) | FPG: epidemiologically inverse; RCT effect on FPG modest (−2 to −4 mg/dL) | 12+ wk | **[Cohort/Meta]** | Wallin, *Diabetes Care* 2012 meta; Brown, *Br J Nutr* 2019 | Effect on TG much larger than on glucose; supplemental EPA+DHA 2–4 g/d may slightly *raise* FPG in some studies (FORWARD; not clinically meaningful). |
| Greek yogurt / fermented dairy | 100–200 g/d | T2D incidence ↓ 14–18% (cohort); FPG modest effect in RCT (~−2 mg/dL) | 12+ wk | **[Cohort]** | Aune, *Am J Clin Nutr* 2013 meta of cohorts; Chen, *BMC Med* 2014 | Probiotic + branched-chain amino acid + dairy fat (trans-palmitoleic acid) mechanisms hypothesized. | Vinegar (apple cider vinegar) pre-meal | 1–2 tbsp (15–30 mL) before carbohydrate-rich meal | PPG iAUC −20 to −31%; FPG −7 to −18 mg/dL after 8–12 wk in T2D | Acute + 8–12 wk | **[Meta]** | Shishehbor, *Diabetes Res Clin Pract* 2017 (PMID 28292654); Hadi, *Front Nutr* 2025 (apple cider vinegar GRADE; PMC11821484) | Effect concentrated when added to high-GI meal; minimal effect on low-GI meals. | Coffee (3–5 cups/d, habitual) | Caffeinated or decaf | T2D risk ↓ 25–30% per 4 cups/d; FPG epidemiologically inverse but RCT effect small (±2 mg/dL) | Chronic | **[Cohort/Meta]** | Ding, *Diabetes Care* 2014 meta (PMID 24929932); Carlström, *Nutr Rev* 2018 | Mechanism via chlorogenic acids + cafestol → improved insulin sensitivity. **Acute caffeine** raises insulin and reduces sensitivity — habitual ≠ acute effects. Decaf works ⇒ not just caffeine. |
| Berries (blueberries, strawberries) | 150 g/d (1 cup) | FPG −4 to −6 mg/dL; HbA1c −0.16% | 8–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Calvano, *Crit Rev Food Sci Nutr* 2019; Vendrame, *Antioxidants* 2016 | Anthocyanin mechanism (ANT inhibit α-amylase + improve endothelial insulin sensitivity). Effect dose-proportional. |
| Cinnamon (Cassia) — modest, oversold | 1–6 g/d | FPG −5 to −8 mg/dL pooled; high heterogeneity | 8–16 wk | **[Meta]** | Allen 2013 *J Acad Nutr Diet* (PMID 24019277); Cochrane 2012 (Leach) | **Oversold in lay press.** Higher quality trials trend null; effect smaller than psyllium or vinegar. | Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale, chard) | 1+ serving/d | T2D incidence ↓ 13% per 1.35 serving/d (cohort) | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Carter, *BMJ* 2010 meta of 6 cohorts (PMID 20724400) | Magnesium + folate + nitrate mechanisms. FPG effect small but T2D risk effect robust; one of the largest fruit/vegetable subgroup effects. |
| Mediterranean diet (olive oil, nuts, fish, vegetables) | High adherence (PREDIMED-style) | FPG −10 to −15 mg/dL vs. control diet | 12–24 wk | **[Meta]** | MDPI Nutrients 2024 meta of 7 RCTs (n=1,371); Esposito 2014 *BMJ Open* | Most data in T2D; smaller effect in healthy euglycemic. | Whole grains (intact kernels) | ≥48 g/d whole grain | T2D risk ↓ 20–32%; FPG modest effect | Chronic | **[Cohort/Meta]** | Aune, *Eur J Epidemiol* 2013 meta (PMID 24158434) | Intact-kernel grains (steel-cut oats, intact rye, farro, barley) preserve glycemic advantage; flour-form whole wheat has higher GI than intact. |
| Avocado (1/2 fruit pre-meal or with carbs) | 75–150 g | PPG iAUC ↓ 20–30% when added to high-carb meal | Acute | **[RCT]** | Wien 2013 *Nutr J*; Park 2018 *Nutrients* | MUFA + soluble fiber + lipid envelope delays gastric emptying. FPG effect not established at chronic dosing. |
| Olive oil (extra virgin) ~25 mL/d | Mediterranean dose | FPG −0.32 mmol/L (−5.8 mg/dL) vs. low-fat diet | 12–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Schwingshackl, *Lipids Health Dis* 2017 (PMID 29151226) | Polyphenol-rich EVOO (≥250 mg/L total polyphenol) has larger effect than refined olive oil. ## 2. Foods that RAISE fasting glucose (or worsen glycemic context)

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sugar-sweetened beverages (SSB) | 1–2 servings/d (≥25 g sugar/day) | T2D risk ↑ 26% per serving/d; FPG +3–5 mg/dL with high intake | Chronic; acute PPG large | **[Cohort/RCT]** | Malik, *Diabetes Care* 2010 meta (PMID 20693348); Stanhope, *J Clin Invest* 2009 (PMID 19381015) | **Fructose-specific effects** beyond glucose effects (see §uric_acid.md): IR, visceral fat, TG, GGT. Glucose-sweetened drinks have smaller effect at matched calories. |
| Fruit juice (orange, apple, grape) | ≥1 cup/d | T2D risk ↑ 7% per serving/d (smaller than SSB but real) | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Imamura, *BMJ* 2015 meta (PMID 26199070); Schulze, *JAMA* 2004 | Whole fruit *protective*; juice loses fiber matrix → glycemic spike. NHANES: 100% juice still raises T2D risk per serving. |
| Refined grains / white rice | ≥5 servings/wk white rice | T2D risk ↑ 11% per serving/d white rice; +27% Asian populations vs +8% Western | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Hu, *BMJ* 2012 meta (PMID 22422870) | Asian populations have larger effect — likely due to greater absolute intake. **South Asian context: white rice is a major lever.** Substituting brown rice ↓ T2D risk 16%. |
| White bread / refined wheat | ≥4 servings/d | Each 50 g/d ↑ T2D risk ~16% | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | de Munter, *PLOS Med* 2007; Aune 2013 | Refined wheat = high GI; pasta cooked al dente has lower GI than bread despite similar refined wheat. |
| Sweetened breakfast cereals | ≥30 g sugar/serving | Acute PPG +30–50 mg/dL above baseline; cumulative IR with chronic | Acute + chronic | **[Cohort/RCT]** | Hall 2018 PLOS Biol "Glucotypes" CGM data | High variability: some "normoglycemic" individuals reach prediabetic-range PPG (>140) on cornflakes. |
| Trans fat (industrial) | ≥1% of energy | T2D risk ↑ 39% per 2% energy from industrial trans | Chronic | **[Cohort/Meta]** | Mozaffarian, *NEJM* 2006 (PMID 16611951) | Largely eliminated from US food supply post-2018 FDA ban; still relevant in some imported / bakery products. |
| High-saturated-fat overfeeding | +750 kcal/d as palmitate-rich | Hepatic IR; FPG +3–7 mg/dL after 3 wk | 2–3 wk | **[RCT]** | Sobrecases 2010 *Diabetes Metab*; Rosqvist 2014 *Diabetes* (PMID 24550191) | Palmitic acid specifically drives hepatic DNL; PUFA matched-calorie overfeed produces less liver fat. | Ultra-processed foods (NOVA-4) | >50% energy intake | T2D incidence HR ~1.4–1.7 (high vs low UPF); FPG +5–8 mg/dL in feeding studies | Chronic | **[Cohort/RCT]** | Hall 2019 *Cell Metab* inpatient RCT (PMID 31105044); Srour, *JAMA Intern Med* 2020 (PMID 31886823) | Hall 2019: UPF caused +500 kcal/d ad lib intake vs unprocessed matched-macros. | Alcohol — heavy intake | >30 g/d ethanol | Acute: insulin suppression → glucose rise; chronic: modest FPG elevation in heavy users | Hours–weeks | **[Cohort]** | Howard, *Ann Intern Med* 2004 meta; Koppes 2005 *Diabetes Care* | J-shape: light/moderate intake (<15 g/d) inconsistent or modestly protective; heavy clearly worsens. | Late-night eating (chrononutrition) | Caloric intake ≥21:00 h | Increased PPG iAUC and dawn glucose; +5–10 mg/dL morning FPG vs early-eating arm | 2–4 wk | **[RCT]** | Vujović 2022 *Cell Metab*; Garaulet 2022 *Curr Nutr Rep* | Mechanism: melatonin antagonizes insulin secretion (MTNR1B); MTNR1B rs10830963 risk allele amplifies effect. |

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## 4. Activities & Behaviors

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aerobic exercise (continuous) | 150 min/wk moderate intensity | FPG −6 to −10 mg/dL in T2D; smaller in euglycemic (~−2 to −4) | 8–16 wk | **[Meta]** | Diabetes Care 2024 optimal-dose meta (PMID 38241499); Boulé 2001 *JAMA* meta | Plateaus around 200–250 min/wk. Effect mostly via improved insulin sensitivity. |
| Resistance training | 3×/wk, 8–12 reps × 8–10 exercises | FPG −7 mg/dL (MD) in T2D; insulin sensitivity ↑ even without weight loss | 12–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Yang 2014 *Sports Med* meta; Ishiguro 2016 *Sports Med* meta | Independent mechanism (GLUT4 upregulation in skeletal muscle). High intensity > moderate. |
| Combined aerobic + resistance | Both modalities, 12+ wk | FPG −6 to −9 mg/dL; additive over either alone | 12 wk | **[Meta]** | Sci Reports 2024 NMA; Sigal 2007 *Ann Intern Med* DARE trial | Best modality combination by network meta-analysis for glucose. |
| HIIT (high-intensity intervals) | 4×4 min @ 85–95% HRmax, 3×/wk | FPG −5 to −8 mg/dL; insulin sensitivity ↑ matched to MICT at lower time | 8–12 wk | **[Meta]** | Jelleyman 2015 *Obes Rev* meta (PMID 26481115); Maillard 2018 *Sports Med* meta | Time-efficient. | Walking after meals (postprandial) | 10–30 min walk ≤30 min after meal | Acute PPG iAUC −12 to −22%; peak glucose −10 to −20 mg/dL | Acute (per meal) | **[Meta/RCT]** | Bellini 2022 *Sports Med* meta (PMID 36715875; PMC10036272); Reynolds 2016 *Diabetologia* (PMID 27747394); 10-min post-glucose RCT 2025 (PMC12216464) | Even 2–5 min walking every 30 min ↓ PPG (Dempsey 2016 *Diabetes Care*). | Vegetables / protein first (food order) | Eat veg + protein ≥10 min before carbs | PPG iAUC −73% (T2D); −40%+ in prediabetes | Acute, sustained with habit | **[RCT]** | Shukla 2015 *Diabetes Care* (PMC4876745); Shukla 2017 *BMJ Open Diab Res Care*; Shukla 2019 *DOM* | Pure behavioral lever, zero cost, no food-elimination required. | Vinegar pre-meal (15–30 mL) | 1–2 tbsp diluted, before high-GI meal | PPG iAUC −20 to −31%; FPG falls in 8–12 wk RCT | Acute + 8–12 wk | **[Meta]** | Shishehbor 2017; Hadi 2025 *Front Nutr* GRADE | Dilute to avoid dental enamel and esophageal damage. |
| Fiber pre-load (e.g., psyllium, almonds, beans) | 5–10 g soluble fiber 10–15 min before meal | PPG iAUC −25 to −40%; chronic FPG ↓ | Acute + weeks | **[RCT]** | Bae 2018 *J Diabetes Investig* (PMC6123026); Jovanovski 2024 | Combine with food order intervention for additive effect. |

| Daily steps (overall NEAT) | +1000 steps/d | Each +1000 steps/d → A1c −0.11%; FPG −1.5 to −2 mg/dL | Months | **[Cohort/RCT]** | Diabetes Care 2024 (Look AHEAD step count analysis); Kraus 2019 *Med Sci Sports Exerc* | Effect attenuates above 7,000–10,000 steps/d. |

| Cold exposure (cold shower, cold-water immersion) | 10–14°C, 2–6 min/d, ≥4 wk | Acute insulin sensitivity ↑; FPG: small if any in healthy euglycemic | 4–6 wk | **[1-Trial/Mechanism]** | van der Lans 2013 *J Clin Invest* (10 d cold exposure ↑ insulin sensitivity 43% in T2D); Hanssen 2015 *Nat Med* | Mechanism via brown adipose tissue activation + skeletal muscle insulin sensitivity. ## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

CGM is the most relevant wearable for this biomarker group; however, even wearables that don't measure glucose (steps, sleep, RHR) correlate with FPG and A1c.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** (in elevated baseline): sustained 5–10% weight loss → FPG −5 to −15 mg/dL over 8–16 wk. In near-optimal baseline (FPG <90), the highest-leverage single intervention shifts to **food order + 10-min post-meal walk** (PPG iAUC −40 to −70%, with cumulative FPG drift −2 to −4 mg/dL).
- **Best stack of 3** (additive but with diminishing returns):
  1. ≥150 min/wk aerobic + 2× resistance training
  2. Mediterranean-style diet (high vegetables, olive oil, fish, whole grains, nuts; low SSB and refined carbs)
  3. Vegetables/protein-first food order + 10-min post-meal walk after main meals
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (FPG 80–88): 0 to −3 mg/dL. Most movement will be in PPG iAUC and CGM TIR, not fasting draw.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for someone with elevated baseline (FPG 100–115, prediabetic): −10 to −20 mg/dL with full stack adherence (exercise + Mediterranean diet + 5% weight loss).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**: Generally not in the prediabetic range with full lifestyle response. Consider metformin if (a) FPG persistently ≥110 despite 6 mo intensive lifestyle, (b) BMI ≥30 or central adiposity, (c) family history of T2D + South Asian ancestry. Metformin in prediabetes (DPP trial) ↓ T2D incidence 31% over 2.8 yr, vs lifestyle 58% — lifestyle wins where adherent. GLP-1 agonists in prediabetes increasingly used off-label but not yet standard-of-care.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **South Asian phenotype**: T2D develops at lower BMI thresholds. WHO Asian-specific overweight cutoff is BMI ≥23 (vs ≥25 European) and obesity ≥27.5 (vs ≥30) — not yet adopted by ADA. ([Misra 2009 *Diabetes Care*](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/32/6/1018/26727); [Kelly West Lecture 2024](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/47/1/7/154008))
- **FPG threshold concern in SA**: An FPG of 90–98 in a lean SA male warrants the same concern as 100–105 in NW European. Emphasize HOMA-IR and CGM data over FPG alone.
- **White rice as a major lever**: SA dietary patterns frequently include 1–2+ cups white rice/day. Substitution with intact-grain alternatives (parboiled rice, brown rice, millet, barley) ↓ T2D risk 16% per substitution.
- **Common SA confound: ghee + clarified butter**: contributes saturated fat load. Replace with mustard oil or olive oil for cardiometabolic improvement; modest direct FPG effect.
- **Lentils/dal**: a protective traditional component — high resistant starch and soluble fiber. Increase frequency, not decrease.
- **Cultural sweet beverages (mango lassi, sweetened chai, falooda)**: high fructose load; major glucose driver. Eliminating these often produces visible CGM TIR improvement within days.
- **TCF7L2 risk allele**: present in 30–35% of SA, contributes to GLP-1 deficiency and impaired post-prandial insulin secretion. Means food order and PPG behaviors have larger marginal benefit.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers**:
- **TCF7L2 rs7903146** (CT/TT carriers): largest common-variant T2D risk; impaired GLP-1 response; PPG excursions disproportionately high. Berberine and metformin response somewhat blunted; food order / fiber pre-load have larger marginal benefit.
- **MTNR1B rs10830963** (G allele carriers): elevated FPG and reduced first-phase insulin secretion; greater glucose rise with late-evening eating. Recommendation: avoid eating after sunset / 21:00.
- **GCKR rs1260326**: associated with both higher FPG and TG. Carriers have larger fructose-load response.
- **HFE C282Y**: hereditary hemochromatosis → iron overload can raise FPG via oxidative β-cell damage. Check ferritin if FPG elevated unexpectedly.

**Drug interactions / confounders** (a draw on these will mislead):
- **Glucocorticoids** (oral, inhaled high-dose, intra-articular within ~2 wk): raise FPG +20–80 mg/dL. Note recent steroid bursts.
- **Atypical antipsychotics**: olanzapine, clozapine, quetiapine — raise FPG and HbA1c; can precipitate T2D.
- **Thiazide diuretics**: small but real ↑ FPG (+3–5 mg/dL).
- **β-blockers (non-vasodilatory: atenolol, metoprolol)**: small ↑ FPG and impaired insulin sensitivity.
- **Statins**: small ↑ FPG (+2–4 mg/dL) and ↑ T2D risk by ~10% (HPS, JUPITER post-hoc); benefit usually outweighs in CVD-risk population.
- **β-agonists** (albuterol, salmeterol): acute glucose rise via β2 → glycogenolysis.
- **Tacrolimus, cyclosporine, sirolimus** (transplant immunosuppression): direct β-cell toxicity.
- **Quinolones, pentamidine, gatifloxacin**: dysglycemia (both hypo and hyper).
- **Niacin (high dose)**: ↑ FPG and new-onset T2D risk.
- **Caffeine** (acute): single dose 400 mg ↑ FPG and reduces insulin sensitivity transiently; do not draw on a coffee morning.
- **Biotin (high dose)**: doesn't directly affect glucose but interferes with immunoassays for insulin, A1c, T4.
- **Recent illness / surgery** (within 1–2 wk): stress hyperglycemia +10–30 mg/dL; defer non-urgent FPG draw.
- **Acute exercise within 24 h**: usually lowers FPG, but very intense (>2 hr endurance, >85% HR max intervals) can transiently raise via cortisol/catecholamines.
- **Sleep deprivation in past 24–72 h**: raises FPG ≥5–10 mg/dL (Spiegel-style restriction).
- **Acute pain / anxiety** (needle stick): catecholamine surge ↑ FPG +5–10 mg/dL.
- **Time of day**: morning peak (dawn phenomenon, 04:00–08:00) — rise ~10–20 mg/dL above 02:00 nadir.

**Pre-analytical**:
- **Fasting duration**: <8 h gives invalid FPG; ≥14 h may modestly raise via counter-regulatory hormones.
- **Tube type**: glycolysis ↓ glucose ~5–7%/h at room temp without fluoride/oxalate inhibitor. Use NaF/oxalate gray-top tubes or process within 30 min.
- **Hematocrit extremes**: very high or low Hct can skew glucose readings on some POC meters.

**Confounders for HOMA-IR (computed from FPG)**: see `HOMA-IR.md` §8.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Cinnamon**: meta-analyses positive but high heterogeneity; high-quality independent trials trend null. Don't recommend with confidence.
- **Vitamin D for non-deficient**: D2d trial (n=2,423, ≥4,000 IU/d for 2.5 yr) showed no T2D-prevention benefit in vitamin-D-replete adults. Don't supplement above repletion expecting glucose effect.
- **Fish oil for FPG**: small pooled benefit but FORWARD/ASCEND/VITAL trials don't show diabetes prevention. May modestly *raise* FPG at high doses (4 g/d). Indication is TG, not glucose.
- **TRE / IF effect on FPG vs CR**: most "TRE benefit" disappears after caloric matching (Liu 2022 *NEJM* TREATY trial — TRE = CR over 12 mo). TRE may help with adherence rather than provide independent glucose benefit.
- **Berberine ancestry transportability**: most data Chinese populations; SA / NW European RCTs sparse. Effect size in non-Asian populations not clearly established at the −0.7% A1c level.
- **CGM in nondiabetic adults**: actionability of CGM data for FPG/A1c reduction in healthy individuals remains uncertain. Behavior change (food order, walking) reliably improves CGM metrics; whether this translates to long-term FPG/A1c improvement at near-optimal baseline is not RCT-proven.
- **Cold exposure / sauna**: insulin-sensitivity benefit in T2D pilot trials (van der Lans 2013, Hanssen 2015); not yet replicated at scale in healthy young adults.
- **South Asian-specific intervention dose-response**: most RCTs use NW European or East Asian samples. Whether the same exercise dose / dietary change produces equivalent FPG reduction in lean SA young men is poorly studied.
