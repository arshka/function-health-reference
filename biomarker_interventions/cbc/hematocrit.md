# Hematocrit (Hct) — Intervention Research

> Hct tracks Hgb closely (Hct ≈ Hgb × 3 in adults) and shares the same intervention levers — iron repletion in IDA (+3–7% Hct), altitude / LHTH (+2–4% Hct), androgens / TRT (+3–10% Hct, frequently exceeds the Hct >54% donation threshold), endurance training plasma volume expansion (Hct apparent −1.5–4% without true RBC mass change). Hydration timing acutely shifts measured Hct ±2–3%. The clinically important high-end ceiling is **Hct 54%** (thrombotic threshold on TRT/AAS) and **Hct 49%** (men's polycythemia diagnostic cutoff).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology lives in `../../blood_cbc_indices.md` §4 and `../../effect_sizes_hematology_other.md` §Hematocrit. Most interventions overlap with `./hemoglobin.md` — see that file for primary citations. This file emphasizes Hct-specific dose-response and the lowering-side levers (TRT erythrocytosis management).

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

**Population caveat:** In iron-replete healthy young men, Hct sits near a physiologic ceiling. Most interventions producing Hct *increases* show 30–60% of the effect cited in anemic populations (floor effects). Hct *decreases* (donation, dilution, training) operate similarly in healthy populations.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention (in IDA, ferritin <30):** Oral iron 100–200 mg elemental Fe alternate-day → Hct +3–6% by 8–12 wk.
- **Highest-leverage single intervention (TRT-driven erythrocytosis, Hct >52%):** Therapeutic phlebotomy 1 unit q8–12 wk → Hct −3–4.5% per unit. Reduce TRT dose or switch route (gel < injectable for erythrocytosis).
- **Highest-leverage single intervention (already iron-replete healthy):** Altitude exposure (LHTH) 3+ wk at 2200–2500 m → Hct +1.5–4% within 6 wk.
- **Best stack of 3 (IDA context):** (1) Oral iron alternate-day + (2) co-meal vitamin C, off-meal calcium/coffee/tea + (3) treat root cause. Expected at 12 wk: Hct +4.5–7.5%.
- **Best stack of 3 (TRT erythrocytosis management):** (1) Periodic phlebotomy + (2) hydration optimization + (3) split TRT doses (lower trough peaks). Hct target <52%.
- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy 20yM, near-optimal baseline (Hct 44–48%):** ±1% spontaneous noise. Altitude trip adds +0.5–2% temporarily. Reducing aerobic load adds +0.5–1.5% via plasma-volume regression (apparent only).
- **Realistic 12-week delta — 20yM with Hct 38%, ferritin 15 (IDA):** Oral iron + dietary heme + vit C → Hct +4.5–7.5%; ferritin +30–60 µg/L.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** (a) IDA refractory to oral iron → IV iron. (b) ESRD/chemo anemia → rhEPO. (c) Polycythemia vera → hydroxyurea or ruxolitinib. (d) Symptomatic anemia in transfusion-dependent disease → transfusion. (e) TRT erythrocytosis Hct >54% refractory to phlebotomy → TRT route/dose change or pause.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **SA vegetarian men frequently sit Hct 40–44%** — within reference but lower than omnivore peers, mirroring ferritin/Hgb gradient (Pasricha 2010 *Lancet*).
- **β-thal trait + α-thal trait** — Hct 35–42% baseline with HIGH RBC count + microcytosis + LOW MCV. Iron does NOT raise Hct in thalassemia trait; confirm with Hb electrophoresis / HPLC before iron supplementation.
- **G6PD deficiency** — episodic Hct drops of 6–12% from triggers (fava beans, sulfa drugs, naphthalene, primaquine).
- **Mentzer index** (MCV / RBC): <13 → likely thal; >13 → likely IDA.
- **Realistic ceiling for SA vegetarian man:** Hct 44–47% with disciplined iron + vit C; >48% generally requires animal heme or supplementation.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **Genetic modifiers:**
  - *HFE* C282Y/H63D — Hct tends high-normal, iron over-accumulates. Phlebotomy therapeutic. Iron supplementation contraindicated.
  - *HBB* β-thalassemia — Hct 1–4% lower baseline + high RBC + low MCV. Iron does not raise.
  - *HBA1/HBA2* α-thalassemia — same phenotype.
  - *HBB* HbS — sickle cell trait usually normal Hct; SS disease chronic 18–27%.
  - *G6PD* — episodic hemolysis from triggers.
  - *EPAS1* / *EPO* — Tibetan / Andean high-altitude adaptation alleles; modest Hct shift.
  - *JAK2* V617F — polycythemia vera mutation; elevated Hct + platelets.
- **Drug interactions:**
  - **TRT / AAS** — predictably raises Hct. Single most common cause of erythrocytosis in healthy young men. Monitor at 3 and 6 mo after TRT initiation.
  - **PPIs / H2 blockers** — slow non-heme iron absorption; over months Hct trend down in marginal-IDA users.
  - **Metformin** — reduces B12 over years; may drive megaloblastic anemia → low Hct.
  - **NSAIDs / SSRIs / anticoagulants** — chronic GI bleed → Hct decline.
  - **Hydroxyurea / methotrexate / chemotherapy** — myelosuppression → Hct decline.
  - **rhEPO / darbepoetin** — directly raises Hct.
  - **Diuretics** — can transiently raise measured Hct via volume contraction.
- **Lifestyle confounders:**
  - **Aerobic training plasma volume** (CRITICAL): Hct apparent −1.5–4% within 2–6 wk of starting/intensifying endurance. RBC mass preserved/↑. Differentiate by checking RBC count + reticulocytes.
  - **Hydration timing** — pre-draw status shifts Hct ±1.5–3%. Standardize.
  - **Posture** — recumbent → ~5% lower; standardize seated 15+ min.
  - **Smoking** — chronic +1.5–3% above baseline.
  - **Time of day** — early morning peak ~5% above evening.
  - **Recent altitude trip / mountain residence** — chronic +0.5–2%.
  - **Recent blood donation** — Hct depressed 4–8 wk.
  - **Acute illness / inflammation / ACOD** — Hct −1.5–6%.
- **Timing artifacts:**
  - Recent intense exercise (within 24 hr) — modest Hct shift via plasma volume.
  - Tourniquet time at draw >1 min — slight hemoconcentration; standardize technique.
  - Spun microhematocrit (manual) reads ~2% higher than analyzer-calculated due to trapped plasma; rare in modern labs but flag if comparing different methods.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal Hct range for healthy young men** — 42–48% population reference. Whether mid-range is "better" than upper-normal in absence of erythrocytosis (Hct >49%) is not well-established.
- **Fluctuation noise floor** — Within-individual day-to-day Hct variation is 1–3% from posture/hydration alone. Detecting <2% true biological change requires standardized draw conditions.
- **Plasma-volume regression after detraining** — How quickly Hct rebounds when aerobic training stops is poorly characterized; estimates 2–6 wk.
- **Chronic mild dehydration in elderly / SA men** — May contribute to apparently elevated Hct in some users; not formally quantified.
- **Cold exposure (Wim Hof) effect** — anecdotal EPO claims; no RCT support for sustained Hct change.
- **Sauna effect** — Scoon 2007 small trial; not replicated.
- **Optimal phlebotomy frequency for TRT erythrocytosis** — 1 unit q8–12 wk standard; individual variability in iron repletion timing.
- **Polycythemia vera Hct target** — CYTO-PV trial established <45% target; whether tighter (e.g., <42%) confers added thrombotic protection is unclear.
- **Wearable-derived "estimated Hct"** — early consumer claims (some optical sensors) are not validated; do not use as substitute for venous CBC.
