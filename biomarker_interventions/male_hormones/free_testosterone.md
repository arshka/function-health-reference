# Free Testosterone — Intervention Research

> Free T tracks total T plus SHBG. Most "free T optimizers" simply move total T (see `total_testosterone.md`); the additional lever for free T specifically is **SHBG modulation** — and the SHBG-lowering route (insulin resistance, exogenous androgen, hypothyroidism) usually comes with worse net androgenic effect, not better. The cleanest free-T-specific gains come from (a) raising total T with diet/sleep/weight loss, and (b) treating high SHBG when present (correcting hyperthyroidism, restoring caloric adequacy in RED-S).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../hormones_male_panel.md` §2 (Free Testosterone). This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial; not yet replicated |
| **[no-effect]** | Null in trials despite marketing claims |

**Critical methodological note:** Free T can be measured by (1) equilibrium dialysis with LC-MS/MS (gold standard, ~50–210 pg/mL), (2) Vermeulen-calculated free T from total T + SHBG + albumin (clinical standard, ~70–235 pg/mL), or (3) direct analog immunoassay (commonly reported by Function/LabCorp at 9–26 pg/mL, **not interchangeable with the others**). Effect sizes here use the Vermeulen-equivalent scale unless noted. **Direct analog immunoassay for free T is explicitly NOT recommended by Endocrine Society 2018** for diagnostic decisions.

## 1. Foods that RAISE free testosterone (or improve direction)

Most foods that raise free T do so via raising total T — covered in `total_testosterone.md` §1. The free-T-specific levers below act primarily by lowering SHBG when SHBG is elevated, or by raising total T disproportionate to SHBG.

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Higher-protein diet** | 1.6–2.2 g/kg/d vs. <0.8 g/kg/d | SHBG **↓**; free T **↑ ~10–20%** despite total T not rising as much | 4–8 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Anderson et al. 1987, *Metabolism* 36:1031 (low- vs. high-protein crossover); Volek 1997 *J Appl Physiol* | Effect is on SHBG; modest. High-protein > 2.5 g/kg in old trials suggested risk; modern evidence mostly null on adverse effects. |
| **Higher-fat (≥35% kcal) Mediterranean-pattern** | EVOO + nuts + fatty fish | Total T modestly higher → free T higher proportionally | 8–12 wk | **[Meta]** | Whittaker & Wu 2021 *J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol* 210:105878 | Direction consistent; magnitude small in lean men. |
| **Adequate carb intake (vs. severe restriction)** | ≥3 g/kg/d carbs vs. <1 g/kg | Insulin support → SHBG suppression → free T modestly **higher** at given total T | Days–weeks | **[1-Trial]** | Anderson 1987; Volek 1997 | Bidirectional in obese: carbs raise insulin → SHBG ↓ → metabolic worsening. Healthy lean: insulin-driven SHBG modest decrease is fine. |
| **Caloric adequacy (correcting RED-S/anorexia)** | EA ≥45 kcal/kg LBM/d | Total T ↑ + SHBG ↓ from high-anorexia level → free T ↑ substantially | 3–6 months | **[Cohort]** | Loucks 2003 *J Appl Physiol*; Hooper 2017 *Med Sci Sports Exerc* | Reverse of underfueling-induced high SHBG / low free T. |
| **Eggs (whole)** | 3/day | Free T also rises with total T in Bagheri 2021; specifically reported | 12 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Bagheri et al. 2021, *Food Funct* 12:8919 | Same trial as in total T file. |
| **Brazil nuts (selenium repletion)** | 2/day | Indirect — selenium supports thyroid → if hyperthyroid contributing to high SHBG, normalization brings free T up | Months | **[Mechanism]** | Indirect via thyroid pathway | Only relevant if Se deficient. |

## 2. Foods that LOWER free testosterone (or worsen direction)

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Low-fat / very-low-fat diet** | <20% kcal fat | Total T ↓ → free T ↓ proportionally | 4–12 wk | **[Meta]** | Whittaker & Wu 2021 | Magnitude similar to total T effect |
| **Severe caloric restriction (anorexia/RED-S)** | EA <30 kcal/kg LBM/d | SHBG ↑ markedly, total T ↓; **free T can fall sharply** | Weeks–months | **[Cohort]** | Loucks 2003; multiple RED-S studies | High SHBG + low T = lowest possible free T phenotype |
| **Heavy alcohol use (chronic)** | ≥3 drinks/d chronic | Total T ↓ ~15–25%; SHBG complex; free T ↓ ~10% | Weeks–months | **[Meta]** | Santi 2024 *Andrology* 12:626 | Free T MD = −0.17 nmol/L in heavy drinkers |
| **Soy isoflavones (very high doses)** | >100 mg isoflavones/d | Total T mildly suppressed in some trials; free T similar | 8 wk | **[Meta]** | Hamilton-Reeves et al. 2010, *Fertil Steril* 94:997 | Reasonable dietary soy: null. |
| **Licorice (real, glycyrrhizin)** | 7 g/d glycyrrhetinic acid | Total T −26–35%; free T proportional | 1 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Armanini et al. 2003, *NEJM* 348:2116 | Real, dose-dependent; avoid concentrated licorice if T is concern. |
| **Sugar load (acute)** | 75 g glucose | Total T −25% acutely; free T proportional (likely amplified by acute SHBG dynamics) | 2 h | **[RCT]** | Caronia et al. 2013, *Clin Endocrinol* 78:291 | Transient. |

## 4. Activities & Behaviors

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sleep extension** | 5 → 8 h/night | Free T tracks total T (~+10–15% recovery) | 1 wk | **[RCT — extrapolated]** | Leproult & Van Cauter 2011, *JAMA* 305:2173 (PMID 21632481) | Trial measured total T; free T inferred |
| **Weight loss in obese baseline** | 10 kg loss | Total T +84 ng/dL; SHBG ↑ ~5–10 nmol/L; free T net **+~20 pmol/L** (rises but less than total T) | 3–6 months | **[Meta]** | Grossmann & Matsumoto 2013, *Eur J Endocrinol* 168:829 | "SHBG paradox" — free T gain is real but tempered |
| **Bariatric surgery** | RYGB / sleeve | Free T **+58 pmol/L** (substantially larger than diet-only weight loss) | 6–12 months | **[Meta]** | Same Grossmann meta | Largest free-T-specific lifestyle move |
| **Resistance training (chronic)** | Untrained → trained | Free T baseline **largely unchanged** in already-trained men | 12 wk | **[Meta]** | Vingren 2010 *Sports Med* 40:1037 | RT is not a free-T optimizer in trained men |
| **Endurance overtraining** | >80 km/wk | Free T ↓ proportional to total T ↓ | Months | **[Cohort]** | Hackney 2003; Hackney 2020 PMC5988228 | RED-S phenotype: high SHBG + low T = very low free T |
| **Caloric refeeding (correcting RED-S)** | EA ≥45 kcal/kg LBM/d | SHBG ↓ from high baseline; total T ↑; free T recovers substantially (sometimes 2× from nadir) | 3–6 months | **[Cohort]** | Hooper 2017 *Med Sci Sports Exerc* 49:467 | Highest-leverage intervention for low-free-T-from-underfueling |
| **Heavy alcohol cessation** | From ≥4 drinks/d | Free T recovers ~10–15% | 4–12 wk | **[Meta — extrapolated]** | Santi 2024 | Real but modest |
| **Stress reduction (chronic high cortisol)** | 8-wk MBSR / breath / sleep hygiene | Cortisol ↓ → modest SHBG/T improvement → free T modest gain | 8 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Cortisol-T mechanism studies | Small effect. |
| **Stop chronic glucocorticoid exposure** | Taper | Recovery of HPG axis → total + free T rise | Weeks–months | **[Mechanism]** | Standard endocrine pharm | Critical to identify exogenous steroid use. |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention**:
  - **Underfueled lean man (high SHBG / low free T):** caloric refeeding to EA ≥45 kcal/kg LBM/d → free T can double from nadir.
  - **Obese man with low free T:** weight loss (especially bariatric for severe obesity) → free T +20–58 pmol/L.
  - **Hyperthyroid man:** treat hyperthyroidism → SHBG normalizes → free T recovers.
  - **Eugonadal lean healthy 20yM:** no high-leverage intervention. Total T moves are the only real lever.

- **Best stack of 3** (additive):
  1. Sleep ≥7.5 h, regular schedule.
  2. Body composition (≤18–20% body fat for healthy 20yM).
  3. Adequate caloric intake (do not restrict severely).
  - Plus: confirm baseline SHBG to know whether you have a free-T problem distinct from a total-T problem.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy lean 20yM (Vermeulen free T ~150–200 pg/mL):** 0–10 pg/mL improvement at most.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — obese man (Vermeulen free T <70 pg/mL):** +20–50 pg/mL with weight loss + sleep.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — RED-S underfueled lean man (free T <70 pg/mL with SHBG >60):** +50–100 pg/mL with refeeding alone.

- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:**
  - Vermeulen free T < 65 pg/mL with symptoms → confirm 2× AM, then evaluate for hypogonadism (Endocrine Society 2018).
  - **Anastrozole 0.5 mg 2–3×/wk** → E2 −50%, LH ↑ → free T **+15–20%** (off-label; only if E2 elevated AND symptoms; over-suppression is harmful — see Finkelstein 2013).
  - **Clomiphene 25 mg/d** → total T +273 ng/dL; free T proportional rise + SHBG mostly unchanged → clean free T gain (Huijben 2022 meta).
  - **Exogenous T** drives SHBG down → high free T fraction.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- SA men have lower SHBG → free T fraction of total T is **higher** at any given total T compared with European-ancestry men. A total T of 350 ng/dL with SHBG 18 nmol/L in an SA man may compute to a free T well within range; the same total T with SHBG 35 in a European-ancestry man may compute below range.
- **Always use Vermeulen calculation** (ISSAM calculator: https://www.issam.ch/freetesto.htm) before concluding "low T" in an SA man.
- Insulin resistance is more prevalent in SAs at lower BMI; low SHBG in lean SA man flags **metabolic** issue (fasting insulin, HOMA-IR), even if free T calculates to normal.
- Free T direct-immunoassay results from Function (typical units 9–26 pg/mL) **are not interchangeable** with calculated/dialysis values; track within-assay only.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- `SHBG rs6258` (Ser156Thr → lower SHBG → free T fraction shifts up at given total T)
- `SHBG rs12150660` (intron 1 → SHBG set-point)
- `AR CAG repeat length` → AR sensitivity (longer CAG → less sensitive → may carry higher free T to compensate, with normal androgenic phenotype)
- `CYP19A1` → aromatase activity (T → E2)

**Drug interactions affecting SHBG/free T disproportionately:**
- Anti-epileptics (phenytoin, carbamazepine, valproate) → SHBG ↑ → free T ↓
- Levothyroxine over-replacement → SHBG ↑ → free T ↓ at constant total T
- Estrogens (rare in men; xenoestrogens in environmental exposure) → SHBG ↑
- Insulin / insulin resistance / metabolic syndrome → SHBG ↓ → free T ↑ at given total T (often masks low total T)
- Exogenous androgens / AAS → SHBG ↓ markedly + total T ↑ → free T disproportionately ↑ (signature pattern)
- Glucocorticoids → modest SHBG ↓ + central HPG suppression → total T ↓ overall

**Assay considerations:**
- **Direct (analog) immunoassay free T** (LabCorp 070218 etc.) is **not recommended** by Endocrine Society 2018 — unreliable cross-method.
- **Vermeulen-calculated free T** from total T (LC-MS/MS) + SHBG + albumin is the clinical standard. Free online ISSAM calculator.
- **Equilibrium dialysis with LC-MS/MS** (LabCorp 070282) is the gold standard but expensive send-out.
- Reference range depends on method. Values not interchangeable.

**Timing artifacts (same as total T):**
- Time of day: AM peak; draw before 10 AM.
- Fasting state.
- Recent illness, exercise, or biotin → assay artifact.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Is free T the better "health" predictor than total T?** Mixed. Cardiovascular outcomes data slightly favor total T (Yeap 2024 IPD meta). Symptomatic decisions in SA men with low SHBG benefit from free T calculation.
- **Anastrozole-induced free T rise (+15–20%) — does it produce measurable clinical benefit in eugonadal men?** No good RCT in eugonadal optimization context. Finkelstein 2013 shows over-suppression is harmful; sweet-spot range unclear.
- **Direct immunoassay free T comparability:** Single-patient longitudinal use of one assay is acceptable; cross-sectional or cross-method comparison is unreliable.
- **Whether SHBG-lowering interventions improve outcomes:** SHBG decrease via insulin resistance is a marker of metabolic disease, not a desirable optimization. SHBG-lowering "for free T" is the wrong frame.
