# Prolactin — Intervention Research

> Prolactin in healthy young men is **dominantly under tonic dopamine inhibition**. The biggest movers are (a) **drugs that block dopamine** (antipsychotics, metoclopramide → marked rise), (b) **stress / sleep / sex** (transient physiologic spikes — major source of false elevations on screening panels), (c) **opioids** (chronic raise), (d) **dopamine agonists** (cabergoline → normalizes hyperprolactinemia). The most important clinical action when reading a mildly-elevated prolactin is to **rule out the macroprolactin artifact** (PEG precipitation; 10–46% of mild apparent elevations are biologically inactive macroprolactin) before any imaging or treatment. There is **no evidence-based supplement for "lowering prolactin"** in eugonadal men.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

Same as other files. Reference range adult men: ~4.0–15.2 ng/mL (LabCorp); upper limit 15–20 ng/mL most labs. **Diagnostic thresholds (Endocrine Society 2011 / Pituitary Society 2023):**
- Mild elevation 16–40 ng/mL: rule out drugs, hypothyroidism, request macroprolactin assay if asymptomatic.
- 30–200 ng/mL: drug-induced, microprolactinoma, stalk effect, or other pituitary mass — MRI usually warranted.
- >150–200 ng/mL: almost always prolactin-secreting tumor.
- >500 ng/mL: very high specificity for macroprolactinoma.

**Optimal for healthy 20yM:** lower-end-of-normal (<10–12 ng/mL) is consistent with well-functioning HPG axis.

## 1. Foods that LOWER prolactin

Direct dietary influences on prolactin in healthy men are **small and inconsistent**. Most claimed "prolactin-lowering foods" lack RCT support.

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tyrosine / phenylalanine-rich foods** | High-protein meals containing dopamine precursors | Modest support of dopaminergic tone — direct prolactin effect minimal in eugonadal men | Acute | **[Mechanism]** | Tyrosine-dopamine pathway physiology | No RCT showing serum prolactin reduction; mechanism only |
| **Mediterranean diet pattern (general)** | Adherence high | Modest cross-sectional lower prolactin | Years | **[Cohort]** | Med-diet endocrine cohort studies | Confounded |
| **Low-saturated-fat diet** | Mediterranean / DASH | Modest cross-sectional effect; not RCT-confirmed | Months | **[Cohort]** | General nutrition-endocrine literature | Confounded |
| **Coffee (moderate)** | 2–3 cups/d | Coffee polyphenols: in vitro prolactin modulation; serum effect in humans not robustly demonstrated | — | **[Cohort]** | Cross-sectional cohort data | Direction inconsistent |

## 3. Supplements

### Lowering prolactin (when elevated)

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine)** | 100–300 mg/d | Modest prolactin reduction via cofactor for dopa decarboxylase (dopamine synthesis); effect ~−10–20% in some small trials | 4–8 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Krieger 1981 *Acta Endocrinol*; small trials in galactorrhea | Doses >100 mg/d: peripheral neuropathy risk with chronic use; effect modest |
| **Vitex agnus-castus (chasteberry)** | 20–40 mg/d (extract) | Modest prolactin reduction in mastalgia/PMS women's trials; data in men sparse | 8–12 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Wuttke 2003 *Phytomedicine* 10:348; multiple women's trials | Mostly women's trials; mild dopamine-agonist activity (DA2 receptor); effect in men unclear |
| **Mucuna pruriens (L-DOPA source)** | 5 g/d powder (~100 mg L-DOPA) | Modest prolactin reduction via dopaminergic activity; effect in infertile men trial | 12 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Shukla 2009 *Fertil Steril* 92:1934 (n=60 infertile men, prolactin −15–20%) | Caution: drug-interaction with MAOIs, SSRIs, antipsychotics; bioavailability variable |
| **Zinc (deficient men)** | 30 mg/d | Modest prolactin reduction in some hyperprolactinemic small trials (zinc → dopamine synthesis cofactor) | 3–6 months | **[1-Trial]** | Salman 1989 small cohort; pediatric cohort studies | Effect contingent on baseline zinc deficiency |
| **Ashwagandha** | 300–600 mg/d | Cortisol reduction (-14–28%); prolactin effect not directly studied but probably small | 8–16 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Lopresti 2019 (PMC6438434); Chandrasekhar 2012 *Indian J Psychol Med* (cortisol effect) | No direct prolactin endpoint |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** for elevated prolactin:
  - **Identify and remove drug cause:** review medication list (antipsychotics, SSRIs, opioids, methyldopa, metoclopramide). Switch to alternative (e.g., risperidone → aripiprazole).
  - **Treat hypothyroidism** if TSH elevated.
  - **Confirm with macroprolactin assay** (PEG precipitation) if mild elevation (15–50 ng/mL) and asymptomatic — 10–46% of mild apparent elevations are biologically inactive macroprolactin (Vilar 2009 PMC3719302).
  - **Rule out pre-test artifact** (recent orgasm, recent intense exercise, venipuncture stress).
  - **MRI pituitary** if confirmed elevation >40 ng/mL or symptoms (HA, visual field, gynecomastia, hypogonadism).

- **Best stack of 3** for elevated prolactin (after ruling out artifacts):
  1. Address removable cause (drugs, hypothyroidism).
  2. Stress reduction (breath, meditation, sleep).
  3. Pharmacotherapy (cabergoline 0.25 mg 2×/wk) when lifestyle ceiling reached and prolactinoma confirmed.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy lean 20yM with normal prolactin (<15):** essentially stable.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — drug-induced hyperprolactinemia (PRL 30–80):** with drug switch → normalization within weeks.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — confirmed prolactinoma with PRL >100 on cabergoline:** 80–90% normalize within 1–3 months.

- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:**
  - **Cabergoline 0.25 mg 2×/wk** → first-line for prolactinoma; 80–90% normalization rate (Verhelst & Abs 2003).
  - **Bromocriptine 2.5 mg/d** → alternative if cabergoline intolerant; more side effects.
  - **Quinagolide** → non-ergot DA agonist alternative.
  - **Levothyroxine** for hypothyroidism-driven hyperprolactinemia.
  - **Antipsychotic switch** for iatrogenic hyperprolactinemia.
  - **Surgery (transsphenoidal)** for macroprolactinoma resistant to DA agonists, or with persistent visual field deficits.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- Prolactin reference ranges are largely population-stable (4–15 ng/mL). No major SA-specific adjustment.
- **Mild elevation (15–25 ng/mL) without symptoms in a young SA man** → first action: **request macroprolactin assay** (PEG precipitation). Many South Asian endocrine labs offer this; LabCorp test 140707 is the US equivalent. If post-PEG recovery <40%, it's macroprolactinemia and requires no treatment.
- South Asian men have higher prevalence of vitamin D deficiency and IR → consider these as confounders in mild prolactin abnormalities.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- `DRD2` (dopamine D2 receptor) variants — affect dopamine inhibition of prolactin; rare loss-of-function variants → constitutive hyperprolactinemia.
- `MEN1` — multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 → prolactinoma + parathyroid + pancreatic tumors.

**Drug interactions (raise prolactin):**
- Antipsychotics (risperidone, haloperidol > olanzapine > aripiprazole/quetiapine)
- SSRIs (paroxetine > fluoxetine > sertraline)
- TCAs (clomipramine, amitriptyline)
- Metoclopramide / domperidone
- Methyldopa
- Verapamil
- Opioids
- Estrogens
- Cimetidine
- Anti-thyroid drugs (if cause hypothyroidism)
- Marijuana (heavy)

**Drug interactions (lower prolactin):**
- Dopamine agonists (cabergoline, bromocriptine, quinagolide)
- Levodopa (transient)
- Aripiprazole (partial DA agonist)
- Vitamin B6 high doses (modest)
- Mucuna pruriens (L-DOPA source; modest)

**Pre-analytical / lifestyle confounders — CRITICAL:**
- **Recent orgasm/sex** within 24 h: prolactin 3–5× baseline → up to 60+ min duration; mild elevation may persist 24 h. **Insist on patient confirming abstinence ≥24 h before draw** for clinically important measurement.
- **Recent intense exercise** within 24 h: prolactin 50–200% elevation acutely.
- **Venipuncture / draw stress**: 20–30 min seated rest recommended pre-draw.
- **Time of day**: nocturnal peak 4–7 AM; AM draws typically 30–60 min post-wake to allow nocturnal peak to subside.
- **Recent meal**: large meals → 1–2 h transient elevation.
- **Nipple/chest stimulation**: acute spike via neural reflex.
- **Acute illness / fever / surgery / seizure**: transient elevation.
- **Pregnancy** (irrelevant in men).

**Macroprolactin artifact — CRITICAL:**
- **Macroprolactin** (IgG-bound prolactin) is biologically inactive but measured as prolactin by standard immunoassays.
- 10–46% of patients with apparent hyperprolactinemia have macroprolactinemia, not true bioactive elevation. (Vilar 2009 PMC3719302; Endocrine Society 2011 guideline.)
- **Mild elevation (15–50 ng/mL) + no symptoms** → request PEG precipitation test before MRI or DA agonist. If post-PEG recovery <40%, it's macroprolactinemia.
- LabCorp test 140707 — Macroprolactin (PEG precipitation).

**Assay considerations:**
- Most prolactin immunoassays are reliable.
- Biotin: high-dose biotin can interfere with some prolactin assays (sandwich format → falsely low). Hold ≥48 h before draw.
- Hook effect in very high prolactin (>5,000 ng/mL — large macroprolactinoma): assay saturation can produce paradoxically lower-appearing reading. Lab dilution may be needed.

**Important pattern recognition:**
- **Mild PRL (15–40) + no symptoms** → drug, hypothyroidism, recent sex/exercise; macroprolactin assay first.
- **PRL 40–200 + symptoms (HA, visual fields, hypogonadism)** → drug-induced if antipsychotic; otherwise MRI for microprolactinoma or stalk effect.
- **PRL >200** → almost certainly prolactinoma; MRI mandatory.
- **PRL >500** → macroprolactinoma highly likely.
- **Low T + low LH + elevated PRL** → prolactin-mediated secondary hypogonadism.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Vitex (chasteberry) effect in men:** mostly women's data; mild dopamine agonism shown but men's RCT data sparse.
- **Mucuna pruriens reliability:** Shukla 2009 showed moderate effect in infertile men; herbal product variability is high; not a substitute for cabergoline in true hyperprolactinemia.
- **Vitamin B6 long-term safety:** doses ≥100 mg/d chronically risk peripheral neuropathy.
- **Whether stress reduction interventions reduce serum prolactin meaningfully outside of acutely-stressed populations:** small effect; probably indirect via overall well-being.
- **Macroprolactin prevalence is population-variable** (10–46% range across studies); regional variation in IgG-prolactin complex frequency partly genetic.
- **Whether SSRI-induced hyperprolactinemia matters clinically in young men with no symptoms:** no good outcome data; usually monitored not treated unless symptomatic.
- **Cardiac valve risk with cabergoline at endocrine doses (≤2 mg/wk):** historical Parkinson's-dose data showed valve risk; endocrine doses far lower; trials suggest no meaningful risk at standard prolactinoma doses but echo monitoring still standard.
