# Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG) — Intervention Research

> SHBG is regulated almost entirely by **insulin** (suppresses) and **thyroid hormone** (induces) at the hepatocyte. Practical movers: in **low-SHBG** patients (the IR/obese phenotype), the only meaningful raise comes from improving insulin sensitivity (weight loss, exercise, low-glycemic diet, metformin, GLP-1RAs). In **high-SHBG** patients (lean underfueled, hyperthyroid, anti-epileptic-treated), correct the underlying state. There is no specific "SHBG supplement" with credible evidence; most of the field is downstream of metabolic and thyroid management. SHBG is one of the **earliest serum markers of insulin resistance** in young men — often abnormal years before fasting glucose changes.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../hormones_male_panel.md` §3 (SHBG). This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

Same as other files. Critical pre-read: **U-shaped optimal** for SHBG. Very low (<15 nmol/L in young male) flags insulin resistance / metabolic syndrome; very high (>60 nmol/L in young male) flags hyperthyroidism, RED-S, liver disease, or anti-epileptic exposure. The "good range" for a healthy 20yM is roughly **25–45 nmol/L**.

## 1. Foods that RAISE SHBG (or improve from low baseline)

The dominant mechanism is reducing insulin / hepatic lipogenic flux → de-repression of HNF-4α → increased hepatic SHBG synthesis (Selva 2007 *J Clin Invest* 117:3979).

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Coffee (caffeinated)** | 4+ cups/day | SHBG **+10–25%** (cross-sectional NHANES); meta-analysis SMD +0.30 | Months | **[Meta]** | Goto et al. 2011, *Diabetes Care* 34:1320; NHANES coffee–SHBG analyses | Effect partly via caffeine, partly chlorogenic acid → improved insulin sensitivity. Decaf shows smaller effect. |
| **Plant-based / high-fiber diet** | High legumes, whole grains, vegetables | SHBG **+10–20%** vs. low-fiber Western diet | 8–12 wk | **[1-Trial / Cohort]** | Berrino 2001 (DIANA trial in women — soy/vegetarian → SHBG +25%); Allen 2002 vegan cohort — men +SHBG vs. omnivores | Insulin-mediated effect; partly attributable to lower BMI in plant-based eaters. |
| **Low-glycemic diet (Mediterranean)** | Adherence high vs. low | SHBG modestly **higher**; magnitude ~+5–10 nmol/L | Months | **[Cohort]** | Mediterranean cohort studies; Tahir 2024 review | Cross-sectional confound; effect is via overall metabolic improvement. |
| **Soy / legumes (moderate)** | 25–50 g soy protein/d | SHBG **+~10–15%** in some trials; mechanism = insulin sensitivity + isoflavone phyto-effect on HNF-4α | 8 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Goodin 2007 *Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev* (n=12, soy → SHBG ↑); not all trials replicate | Reasonable dietary soy is safe; "soy is feminizing" is overstated (Hamilton-Reeves 2010). |
| **Caloric restriction (in obese baseline)** | 500–1,000 kcal/d deficit | SHBG **+5–10 nmol/L** at 10 kg loss | 3–6 months | **[Meta]** | Grossmann & Matsumoto 2013, *Eur J Endocrinol* 168:829 | Insulin-mediated. |
| **Prolonged fasting / very low calorie diet** | Extreme | SHBG **markedly ↑** within days (low insulin) | Days | **[Cohort]** | Anorexia / RED-S literature | Excessive: pushes into high-SHBG / low-free-T phenotype |
| **High-fiber breakfast (β-glucan)** | 3–5 g β-glucan/d | Insulin smoothing → modest SHBG support | Weeks | **[Mechanism]** | β-glucan postprandial insulin trials (Whitehead 2014 meta) | Indirect; small effect |
| **Green tea (catechins)** | 4–6 cups/d | Cross-sectional ↑ SHBG; effect via insulin sensitivity | Months | **[Cohort]** | Several Asian cohort studies | Modest |
| **Low-fructose diet** | <25 g added fructose/d | Reduces hepatic lipogenesis → less HNF-4α suppression → SHBG ↑ | Months | **[Mechanism]** | Selva 2007 *J Clin Invest* 117:3979 (fructose specifically suppresses SHBG); Lê 2009 fructose loading studies | Strongest food-pathway mechanism |

## 2. Foods that LOWER SHBG (or worsen from already-low baseline)

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fructose / sucrose excess** | High soda/HFCS intake | SHBG **↓ markedly** via hepatic lipogenesis → HNF-4α suppression | Weeks–months | **[RCT/Mechanism]** | Selva et al. 2007, *J Clin Invest* 117:3979 (cell + cohort); Lê 2009 *Am J Clin Nutr* (fructose loading) | Most robust single-nutrient effect on SHBG. **Sugar restriction is the main dietary lever for low-SHBG patients.** |
| **Refined carbohydrates / high-glycemic diet** | High-GI Western diet | SHBG ↓ via chronic hyperinsulinemia | Months | **[Cohort]** | Selva 2007 mechanism; multiple metabolic-syndrome cohorts | Same insulin pathway |
| **Excess caloric intake (weight gain)** | Surplus chronic | SHBG ↓ as IR develops | Months–years | **[Cohort]** | Brand 2010 *Diabetes Care* (PMID 20368409) | Bidirectional: SHBG predicts T2D risk independent of BMI |
| **High-fat (very high) ketogenic diets** | <20 g carbs/d, high fat | Mixed: insulin-sensitivity improvement → SHBG ↑; some short trials show transient SHBG decline | Weeks–months | **[Mixed]** | Volek 2002–2008 trials; Hallberg 2018 T2D KD cohort | Complex; depends on weight loss component |
| **Alcohol (heavy chronic)** | ≥3 drinks/d | SHBG complex: acute ↑ in some studies, chronic heavy ↓ | Weeks–months | **[Meta]** | Santi 2024 *Andrology* 12:626 | Inconsistent direction; acute ≠ chronic |
| **Skipping breakfast / irregular meals** | Habitual | Insulin variability → modest SHBG suppression | Months | **[Cohort]** | Cross-sectional eating-pattern studies | Confounded by overall diet quality |

## 3. Supplements

### Raising SHBG (when low)

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Inositol (myo-inositol)** | 2 g 2×/d | SHBG **+~30%** in PCOS/IR women; data in men sparse but mechanism (insulin sensitization) extrapolates | 8–12 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Genazzani 2008 *Gynecol Endocrinol*; PCOS literature | Strong female evidence, sparse male data; safety good. |
| **Berberine** | 500 mg 3×/d | Improves insulin sensitivity → SHBG ↑ in metabolic syndrome | 12 wk | **[Meta]** | Lan 2015 *J Ethnopharmacol*; Yin 2008 (PMC2410097) | Mostly Chinese trials in T2D/metabolic syndrome; transportability to lean SA / European-ancestry less established. Effect on SHBG inferred from T2D cohort responses. |
| **Vitamin D (in deficient + IR)** | 2,000–4,000 IU/d | Small SHBG ↑ via insulin sensitivity in deficient + IR populations | 6 mo | **[1-Trial]** | Pittas 2012 *Diabetes Care*; D2D trial extensions | Modest effect, secondary to metabolic improvement. |
| **Omega-3 fish oil (EPA/DHA)** | 2–4 g/d | Modest SHBG ↑ in some metabolic-syndrome trials; mechanism = liver fat reduction | 12 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Spadaro 2008 *Dig Liver Dis* (NAFLD trial); modest | Effect via hepatic fat reduction. |
| **Selenium** | 200 µg/d | If hypothyroid contributing to low SHBG, selenium may help thyroid → indirect SHBG ↑ | Months | **[Mechanism]** | Toulis 2010 selenium-thyroid meta | Indirect only |

### Lowering SHBG (rarely desired; relevant only if pathologically high)

| Supplement / drug | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Evidence | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Exogenous androgens / AAS** | Any supraphysiologic | SHBG **↓ 30–50%** (direct hepatic suppression) | **[Mechanism/Cohort]** | Universal observation in AAS users; diagnostic flag |
| **Stanozolol / non-aromatizable orals** | Any dose | SHBG **↓ very markedly** (often 70–80%); used clinically in HAE | **[Mechanism]** | Stanozolol HAE literature |
| **Glucocorticoids** | Chronic | SHBG modest ↓ | **[Cohort]** | Steroid-treated cohort data |
| **Insulin therapy (T1D, T2D)** | Standard | SHBG ↓ via direct insulin suppression of HNF-4α | **[Mechanism]** | Selva 2007 |
| **Metformin** | 500–2,000 mg/d | SHBG **↑** (insulin-lowering) in PCOS / IR; less data in lean men | **[Meta]** | PCOS metformin meta-analyses | NOT a SHBG-lowering drug — opposite |

### "SHBG modulators" with no real evidence

| Supplement | Claim | Reality | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stinging nettle root** | "Binds SHBG, frees T" | In vitro SHBG-binding does not translate to serum SHBG change in humans | **[no-effect]** Lichius 1999 (in vitro only) |
| **Boron** | "Lowers SHBG" | Naghii 2011 (n=8) reported small SHBG decrease; uncontrolled and unreplicated | **[1-Trial — questionable]** |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention**:
  - For **low SHBG (<25 nmol/L)** in young man: **fat loss + reduced refined-sugar/fructose intake**. SHBG can rise 10–20 nmol/L over 3–6 months with substantial body composition improvement.
  - For **high SHBG (>55 nmol/L)** in lean active man: **caloric refeeding** to EA ≥45 kcal/kg LBM/d. SHBG can drop 15–25 nmol/L with weight restoration + reduced training stress.
  - For **high SHBG with hyperthyroidism**: treat thyroid (methimazole if appropriate). SHBG normalizes in weeks-months.

- **Best stack of 3 (low SHBG / IR phenotype):**
  1. Reduce added sugar / fructose to <25 g/d.
  2. Reduce body fat to ≤18–20% (specifically visceral fat).
  3. Aerobic + resistance training ≥150 + 2×/wk.
  - Add: sleep ≥7.5 h/night; coffee 3–4 cups/d if tolerated; consider berberine 500 mg 3×/d if metabolic markers warrant.

- **Best stack of 3 (high SHBG / RED-S phenotype):**
  1. Caloric refeeding to LBM-appropriate intake.
  2. Reduce training volume (especially endurance excess).
  3. Adequate carb intake (≥3 g/kg/d) — supports insulin tone.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy lean 20yM with normal SHBG (25–45 nmol/L):** generally remains stable; ±5 nmol/L is noise.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — IR/obese man with SHBG 15 nmol/L:** +5–10 nmol/L over 3 months with intensive lifestyle, +15–20 nmol/L at 12 months.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — RED-S lean active man with SHBG 70 nmol/L:** −10–20 nmol/L with refeeding within 3 months.

- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:**
  - **Metformin 1,500 mg/d** for IR with low SHBG → modest SHBG ↑.
  - **GLP-1 receptor agonists** (semaglutide, tirzepatide) for severe obesity → fat loss → SHBG ↑ substantially.
  - **Methimazole** for hyperthyroidism producing high SHBG.
  - **Levothyroxine** for hypothyroidism producing low SHBG (corrects to euthyroid range).
  - Anti-epileptic substitution (if AED is causing high SHBG and clinically permissible).

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- SA men have **lower SHBG set-point** at any given metabolic state — partly genetic, partly higher visceral adiposity at lower BMI. (McTernan 2007 PMID 17900299; Bhatt 2010 PMID 20550541.)
- **SHBG <20 nmol/L in a lean SA man (BMI <25) is a strong early flag for metabolic syndrome** even with normal fasting glucose. Order fasting insulin / HOMA-IR.
- The "thin outside, fat inside" (TOFI) phenotype common in SAs presents with low SHBG well before any glucose abnormality. SHBG is the most sensitive single serum marker available on a routine panel for this phenotype.
- Aerobic exercise + visceral fat reduction is the indicated lifestyle intervention; metformin is reasonable adjunct when HOMA-IR confirmed.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- `SHBG rs6258` (Ser156Thr) — lower SHBG; carriers tend to run 5–10 nmol/L below reference at baseline.
- `SHBG rs12150660` (intron 1) — affects SHBG set-point.
- `HNF1A` and `HNF4A` variants — affect hepatic SHBG transcription.

**Drug interactions (raise SHBG):**
- Anti-epileptics (phenytoin, carbamazepine, valproate)
- Estrogens, ethinyl estradiol
- Levothyroxine (over-replacement → SHBG ↑)
- Tamoxifen (modest)
- Anti-androgens (less predictable)

**Drug interactions (lower SHBG):**
- Exogenous androgens / AAS (signature pattern — diagnostic)
- Glucocorticoids (chronic)
- Insulin (therapeutic in DM)
- Stanozolol / non-aromatizable orals (very marked decrease)
- Growth hormone / IGF-1 excess (acromegaly)

**Lifestyle confounders:**
- Acute illness — may transiently affect SHBG (minor)
- Recent weight change — SHBG lags weight by weeks
- Severe caloric restriction or fasting → SHBG ↑ (may not reflect chronic state)

**Assay considerations:**
- SHBG immunoassay is generally reliable; less assay-variability concern than for thyroid hormones or sensitive E2.
- LabCorp adult male reference: 16.5–55.9 nmol/L (age 20–49); 19.3–76.4 (age 50+).
- SHBG normally rises with age 1–2% per year — adjust expectations for older men.
- Biotin: less affected than thyroid assays, but high-dose biotin can interfere with some SHBG assays. Hold biotin ≥48 h before draw.

**Important pattern recognition:**
- **Low SHBG + high total T + suppressed LH/FSH** = exogenous androgen use signature.
- **Low SHBG + low total T + normal LH/FSH** = obesity / IR-driven hypogonadism.
- **High SHBG + low total T + low LH/FSH** = secondary hypogonadism (RED-S, anorexia, opioids, severe stress).
- **High SHBG + low total T + high LH/FSH** = primary hypogonadism with adaptive SHBG.
- **High SHBG + normal total T + high T4/low TSH** = hyperthyroidism.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Inositol in men:** strong PCOS evidence; sparse RCT in IR men with low SHBG. Mechanism extrapolates well; trial confirmation pending.
- **Berberine transportability:** Chinese trials in T2D show metabolic + SHBG benefits; whether effect is equivalent in lean SA / European-ancestry men with normoglycemic IR is not well established.
- **Specific SHBG-targeting supplements:** None has proven directly effective. Focus on metabolic upstream.
- **Coffee mechanism:** Causal vs. confounded by overall lifestyle is not fully resolved (MR studies support causal direction).
- **Whether SHBG response to weight loss predicts hormonal/metabolic response speed:** Mechanistic plausibility but not formally tested as a biomarker.
- **GLP-1RA effect on SHBG specifically (independent of weight loss):** Currently being studied; not yet quantified independently.
