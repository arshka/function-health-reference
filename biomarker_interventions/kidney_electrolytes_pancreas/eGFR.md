# eGFR (CKD-EPI 2021, creatinine-based) — Intervention Research

> eGFR-cr is **derived from serum creatinine, age, sex** (CKD-EPI 2021 race-free; Inker NEJM 2021;385:1737). Every modifier of serum creatinine moves eGFR-cr inversely. **The single biggest "false low eGFR" lever in young men is creatine supplementation** (drops eGFR-cr ~5–15 mL/min with NO true GFR change). True GFR-protective interventions in CKD: BP control to <120 mmHg (SPRINT), SGLT2i (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY), GLP-1 RA (FLOW), finerenone (FIDELIO/FIGARO), ACEi/ARB, weight loss in obese (~+4–8 mL/min over 6–12 mo), tobacco cessation. Age-related decline is ~0.7–1.0 mL/min/yr after age 30 (Glassock & Rule, *Kidney Int* 2012;82:270).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../kidney_electrolytes_pancreas.md` §4 (eGFR) and §0.1 (creatine confound). This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology/physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small unreplicated trial |

Population caveat: most evidence comes from CKD trials (T2DM and non-DM), middle-aged and older. Effect sizes shrink in young healthy people because the eGFR equation plateaus near 120 mL/min/1.73 m². Young muscular males are SYSTEMATICALLY mis-estimated low by eGFR-cr — cystatin C is the discriminator.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** for a "low" eGFR-cr in a healthy young muscular male: **add cystatin C-based eGFR** (KDIGO 2024 Grade 1B). The combined eGFR-cr-cys (Inker NEJM 2021) outperforms either alone and resolves nearly all "Stage 2 CKD" mislabeling in lifters/creatine users.
- **Best stack of 3 to optimize "true" eGFR / preserve slope** in a young person:
  1. **BP <120/80** (lifestyle: Na <2.3 g/d, exercise 150 min/wk, weight at lean BMI). Each 10 mmHg SBP reduction → ~0.5 mL/min/yr slower eGFR decline.
  2. **Avoid chronic nephrotoxin exposure** — minimize chronic NSAID use, avoid PPI overuse, finish antibiotic courses without unnecessary repetition.
  3. **Glycemic optimization** — avoid SSB, manage post-prandial spikes, prevent T2DM (the dominant CKD driver).
- **Best stack of 3 to ensure clean eGFR readings** in a fitness population:
  1. Hold creatine 14 d before draw.
  2. Avoid cooked meat 12–24 h before draw, fasted morning sample.
  3. Don't draw within 48–72 h of intense training session or marathon.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM (eGFR-cr 95): essentially no change. Equation saturates ~120; absolute number above 90 is not informative.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for muscular 20yM with creatine + meat-rich diet (eGFR-cr 75): cystatin C eGFR likely 100+; stopping creatine + standardized draw → eGFR-cr 85–90.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** confirmed CKD with persistent eGFR <60 ×3 mo OR uACR ≥30. Then ACEi/ARB ± SGLT2i ± finerenone per KDIGO 2024. Pharmacotherapy never indicated for "low eGFR-cr" in a young person without confirmed CKD.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- CKD-EPI 2021 was not specifically calibrated against SA cohorts. Validation studies (Jessani S et al., *Am J Kidney Dis* 2014;63:49 — Pakistan; Kumar V et al., *Clin Chim Acta* 2017;471:11) suggest CKD-EPI tends to **overestimate** GFR by 3–8 mL/min in lean SA males.
- Lean SA males with low muscle mass may have **falsely elevated eGFR-cr** (low Cr from low muscle pool — masking declining true GFR). uACR is the higher-leverage screen in this population.
- *APOL1* G1/G2 high-risk variants are essentially absent in SA — not a risk modifier.
- IgA nephropathy is the most common GN in young SA males globally; can present with normal eGFR + uACR 30–300 + microscopic hematuria. Always pair eGFR with urine ACR + dipstick.
- T2DM and HTN drive a disproportionate burden of CKD in SA at younger ages (Gupta R et al., *Indian J Med Res* 2017;145:152) — early aggressive prevention warranted.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **Equation limitations:** CKD-EPI is validated for steady-state, age ≥18, not designed for AKI; saturates ~120 mL/min. CKiD U25 equation for adolescents (Pierce CB et al., *Kidney Int* 2021;99:948).
- **Cystatin C disposition:** Use combined eGFR-cr-cys when eGFR-cr is borderline 60–89 in a muscular young person (KDIGO 2024 Grade 1B).
- **Creatinine confounds (all flow through to eGFR):**
  - Creatine monohydrate (5–15 mL/min false drop)
  - Cooked meat <12 h (5–15 mL/min)
  - Trimethoprim, cimetidine, dolutegravir, cobicistat, fenofibrate (10–20 mL/min false drop)
  - High muscle mass (5–15 mL/min systematic underestimation)
  - Recent intense exercise (5–15 mL/min)
  - Acute dehydration (variable)
  - ACEi/ARB initiation: 5–20 mL/min EXPECTED, not pathological
  - SGLT2i initiation: 3–5 mL/min initial dip, then preserved
- **Genetic modifiers:**
  - *UMOD* rs4293393 — minor allele lower CKD risk, faster eGFR slope decline; not actionable individually (Köttgen A et al., *Nat Genet* 2010;42:376).
  - *APOL1* G1/G2 (West African ancestry) — major CKD risk multiplier.
  - *GCKR* rs1260326 — modest eGFR effect; pleiotropic.
  - *SHROOM3*, *DAB2*, *SLC34A1* — small additive effects in GWAS.
  - PRS for eGFR/CKD reaching modest predictive value (Wuttke M et al., *Nat Genet* 2019;51:957 — over 250 loci).
- **Drug interactions (TRUE GFR effects):**
  - Aminoglycosides, vancomycin (trough-dependent), cisplatin, IV contrast, tenofovir-DF (NOT TAF), amphotericin, calcineurin inhibitors (cyclosporine, tacrolimus).
  - Triple-whammy: ACEi/ARB + diuretic + NSAID — high AKI risk.
- **Age**: physiologic decline ~0.7–1.0 mL/min/yr after age 30 (Glassock RJ & Rule AD, *Kidney Int* 2012;82:270). Population mean eGFR-cr at age 70 is ~70 — not necessarily CKD.
- **Pregnancy**: GFR ↑ 40–50% in 2nd trimester; "low" Cr / "high" eGFR is normal.
- **Sample handling**: stable for typical lab transit times.
- **CKD diagnosis** requires the abnormality (eGFR <60 OR ACR ≥30) to be present **≥3 months** AND with structural/functional kidney damage marker. Single low eGFR is not CKD (KDIGO 2024).

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Eqn performance in lean SA young males** under-validated; cystatin C concordance studies emerging but reference ranges incomplete.
- **Optimal eGFR target for primary prevention** — DAPA-CKD and EMPA-KIDNEY enrolled patients with eGFR ≥20–25; no trials in eGFR ≥90 healthy people for prevention.
- **Effect of GLP-1 RA / SGLT2i in non-DM, non-CKD young adults** — mostly extrapolation; FLOW and DAPA-CKD did not include healthy young people.
- **Hyperfiltration (eGFR >120) clinical significance in young people** — likely benign in lean healthy individuals; pathological in obesity/T2DM.
- **Long-term safety of high-protein diet on eGFR slope in healthy** — Antonio 2016, Devries 2018 reassuring out to 1–2 yr; longer follow-up scarce.
- **Hydration level for slope preservation** — CKD-WIT (Clark 2018) was negative for over-hydration; healthy euhydration is safest assumption.
- **Polygenic risk scores for CKD** — emerging but not yet clinically deployed for individualized eGFR target setting.
- **Plant-based diet → measured GFR change** — still partially confounded by dietary creatinine effect.
- **Stopping creatine: full washout duration** — most studies use 7–14 d; total-body creatine pool t½ ~30 d, so a 14–28 d washout is ideal for clean baseline.
