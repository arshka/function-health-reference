# Non-HDL Cholesterol (non-HDL-C) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage framing:** Non-HDL-C = TC − HDL-C. It captures **all** atherogenic cholesterol — LDL-C plus VLDL-C plus IDL plus remnants plus Lp(a). It is **superior to LDL-C in patients with TG >150 mg/dL** (Boekholdt et al., *JAMA* 2012) because it captures remnant cholesterol that calculated LDL-C misses. For South Asian individuals (high TG-rich phenotype), non-HDL-C is the primary lipid target alongside ApoB. Effect sizes are approximately LDL-C × 1.1–1.2 since interventions that lower TG also lower VLDL-C. Non-HDL-C target in primary prevention: <130 mg/dL; in high-risk: <100 mg/dL; in established ASCVD: <85 mg/dL (parallel +30 mg/dL above LDL-C targets).

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: non-HDL-C interventions show smaller absolute changes in lean young adults at near-optimal baseline. The TG-component of non-HDL-C means insulin-resistant individuals see larger absolute reductions from carb restriction and weight loss than from pure LDL-C interventions.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention (non-pharm):** Mediterranean or Portfolio diet pattern (non-HDL-C −15–30 mg/dL). Captures both LDL-C and TG drops.
- **Best stack of 3 (additive ~70% of linear sum):**
  1. SFA reduction to <7% kcal + refined carb/sugar reduction: non-HDL-C −15–25 mg/dL
  2. Psyllium 10 g/d + plant sterols 2 g/d: non-HDL-C −15–20 mg/dL
  3. Marine omega-3 (2–4 g/d EPA+DHA) or fatty fish 2× weekly: non-HDL-C −10–15 mg/dL
  - Combined estimated delta from elevated baseline (non-HDL-C 160): −30–50 mg/dL (to ~110–130)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (non-HDL-C ~100 mg/dL):** −10 to −15 mg/dL with maximum lifestyle (floor effects)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (non-HDL-C >160 mg/dL):** −30 to −50 mg/dL with the 3-stack
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** Non-HDL-C >130 mg/dL persisting >6 months on max lifestyle with elevated risk factors; non-HDL-C >100 mg/dL with established ASCVD. Statin (rosuvastatin 5–10 mg) reduces non-HDL-C −40–50%.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Non-HDL-C is the preferred lipid metric for SA individuals** because of their high TG-rich phenotype that LDL-C alone misses. NLA 2021 statement (Patel et al., *J Clin Lipidol* 2021;15:402) endorses non-HDL-C and ApoB as primary risk markers in SA.
- MASALA cohort: SA individuals have mean LDL-C similar to Whites but higher TG (~125 vs 110 mg/dL) → higher non-HDL-C (~135 vs 130 mg/dL) → higher atherogenic burden than LDL-C alone suggests.
- Insulin resistance + visceral adiposity drive remnant cholesterol elevation in SA; non-HDL-C captures this; LDL-C does not.
- Implication: non-HDL-C target may be more clinically meaningful than LDL-C target for SA individuals at the same age and ASCVD risk score.
- Carbohydrate restriction (rice, refined flour, mithai) likely produces larger non-HDL-C drops in SA than in NW European populations, though SA-specific RCTs sparse.
- Vanaspati (partially hydrogenated vegetable oil) raises both LDL-C and TG → large non-HDL-C effect; eliminate.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers (composite of LDL-C, TG, VLDL drivers):**
- **APOE ε4:** Stronger non-HDL-C rise from SFA.
- **PCSK9 LoF:** Lifelong −15–20% lower non-HDL-C.
- **LDLR mutations (FH):** Markedly elevated non-HDL-C.
- **APOC3 LoF:** Lower non-HDL-C via TG drop.
- **LPL variants (LoF):** Higher non-HDL-C via TG/VLDL elevation.
- **APOA5 variants:** Elevate non-HDL-C via TG.
- **TM6SF2 rs58542926 T allele:** Lower non-HDL-C (lower hepatic VLDL) but higher hepatic steatosis.

**Drug interactions (composite of LDL-C, HDL-C, TG drug effects):**
- See LDL-C, HDL-C, and TG files for individual drug effects.
- **Statins:** Non-HDL-C drops ~45–52% on high-intensity (slightly larger % than LDL-C due to VLDL contribution).
- **PCSK9i (evolocumab):** Non-HDL-C −50–55%.
- **Bempedoic acid:** Non-HDL-C −10–13%.
- **Estrogen (oral):** LDL-C drops but TG rises → net non-HDL-C ±0.
- **Anabolic steroids:** Non-HDL-C variable (LDL-C can rise; HDL-C drops; TG up).
- **Glucocorticoids:** Non-HDL-C +10–20% via TG and LDL-C.
- **Atypical antipsychotics:** Non-HDL-C +10–25%.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing artifacts:**
- Non-HDL-C is **fasting-insensitive** (LDL-C and HDL-C are fasting-stable; only TG is fasting-sensitive, and non-HDL-C uses TC and HDL-C, not TG).
- Acute infection or inflammation: non-HDL-C drops as part of acute-phase response; wait 4–6 wk for stable trend.
- Pregnancy: non-HDL-C rises ~30–50%.
- Hypothyroidism (TSH >5): non-HDL-C +10–25%.
- Active liver disease: non-HDL-C variable.
- Recent rapid weight loss: non-HDL-C may transiently elevate during fat mobilization.
- Standing during draw or prolonged tourniquet: small artifactual elevation.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Non-HDL-C vs ApoB head-to-head:** Both are superior to LDL-C in TG-elevated phenotypes. ApoB is conceptually purer (one particle = one ApoB) but non-HDL-C is universally available and free (calculated from standard lipid panel). Magnitude of difference in risk prediction is small in most populations; ApoB favored in lipid expert consensus, non-HDL-C favored in primary-care guidelines.
- **Lean mass hyperresponder phenotype on keto:** Non-HDL-C may rise (LDL-C rise > TG drop) in lean keto adherents. Long-term outcomes not RCT-tested.
- **Pemafibrate paradox:** PROMINENT (NEJM 2022) showed pemafibrate reduced non-HDL-C ~13% but did not reduce CV events. Suggests non-HDL-C reduction via PPARα may not fully capture residual risk; ApoB or LDL-C reduction may be the more directly causally relevant target.
- **Bergamot polyphenol:** Most data from one Italian group.
- **Berberine non-HDL-C transferability:** ~80% of trials Chinese; magnitude uncertain in NW European / SA populations.
- **CGM-guided non-HDL-C reduction:** Logical but not RCT-tested.
- **Time-restricted eating independent of weight loss:** Lin 2024 meta suggests no independent non-HDL-C effect without weight loss.
