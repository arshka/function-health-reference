# Total Cholesterol (TC) — Intervention Research

> **Important framing:** Total cholesterol is a composite (TC = LDL-C + HDL-C + VLDL-C); it is the **least informative single lipid number** for intervention monitoring and risk stratification. In modern lipidology, TC is largely deprecated in favor of ApoB or non-HDL-C. This file documents how TC moves under the major lifestyle / pharmacologic interventions, with the caveat that *every TC change is the algebraic sum of LDL-C, HDL-C, and VLDL-C changes* — sometimes these cancel (e.g., fibrates lower TG/VLDL-C but raise HDL-C, so net TC change is small) and the TC alone obscures the directional picture. **For interpretation, always pair TC with HDL-C (giving non-HDL-C) or measure ApoB directly.**

**Cross-link:** Biology and clinical interpretation in `../heart_lipids_basic.md` §"Total Cholesterol". Existing summary in `../effect_sizes_cardiovascular.md` lines 199–201 (intentionally brief). This file is INTERVENTION-only and consolidates the LDL-C and HDL-C tables into TC magnitudes.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: TC is heavily influenced by HDL-C; lean active young adults with high HDL-C may show "high TC" that is metabolically benign. Always sub-decompose TC into LDL-C and HDL-C before drawing conclusions.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Re-frame:** TC alone is the wrong metric to optimize. Optimize ApoB, LDL-C, non-HDL-C, and HDL-C separately. TC is the algebraic sum and can be misleading (e.g., HDL-C rise from exercise + LDL-C drop from diet can produce a smaller TC drop than the LDL-C drop alone).
- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Same as LDL-C — Portfolio diet pattern (TC −20–30 mg/dL). For lifestyle.
- **Best stack of 3 (additive ~70% of linear sum):**
  1. SFA reduction to <7% kcal: TC −10–15 mg/dL
  2. Psyllium 10 g/d + plant sterols 2 g/d: TC −15–20 mg/dL
  3. Almonds 45 g/d in place of snacks: TC −5–7 mg/dL
  - Combined estimated delta from elevated baseline (TC 220): −25–35 mg/dL (to ~185–195)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (TC ~160 mg/dL):** −5 to −10 mg/dL with maximum lifestyle (floor effects); large changes typically come from HDL-C rise rather than LDL-C drop, so the TC change can be small or even null while non-HDL-C drops more
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (TC >220 mg/dL):** −20 to −35 mg/dL with the 3-stack
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** TC by itself is rarely the threshold; the underlying ApoB or LDL-C drives the decision. Consider statin if non-HDL-C >130 mg/dL or LDL-C >100 mg/dL with risk factors (Lp(a) high, family history, ApoB >90, calcium >0).

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- SA individuals often have TC in "desirable" range (per NCEP) but elevated ApoB and non-HDL-C due to small dense LDL particles and high TG. **TC alone systematically under-predicts ASCVD risk in SA populations.** Use ApoB or non-HDL-C alongside TC.
- MASALA cohort mean TC: ~190 mg/dL — within "desirable" — yet CAC scores and event rates are disproportionately elevated.
- Vanaspati (partially hydrogenated vegetable oil) historically raises TC via LDL-C and trans fat; eliminate if present.
- Coconut oil widely promoted in SA cuisine raises TC via LDL-C; replace with MUFA-rich oils.
- Cultural sweets (mithai) and refined-flour staples raise TC indirectly via TG/VLDL-C and via SFA from ghee.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers (same as LDL-C and HDL-C; TC is composite):**
- **APOE ε4:** Stronger TC rise from SFA.
- **PCSK9 LoF:** Lifelong −15–20% lower TC.
- **LDLR mutations (FH):** Markedly elevated TC.
- **APOB R3500Q (familial defective ApoB):** Elevated TC.
- **CETP variants:** Modify TC via HDL-C component.
- **APOA1, APOC3, LPL variants:** Modify TC via TG/VLDL-C component.

**Drug interactions (composite of LDL-C, HDL-C, TG drug effects):**
- See LDL-C and HDL-C and TG files for individual drug effects.
- Net TC change can be misleading; always sub-decompose.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing artifacts:**
- TC is fasting-insensitive (LDL-C and HDL-C are fasting-stable).
- Acute infection or inflammation: TC drops as part of acute-phase response (~−10–20%); wait 4–6 wk for stable trend.
- Pregnancy: TC rises ~30–40% across pregnancy.
- Hypothyroidism (TSH >5): TC +10–25%; correct thyroid first.
- Active liver disease: TC variable.
- Recent rapid weight loss: TC may transiently rise during fat mobilization.
- Standing during draw or prolonged tourniquet: small artifactual elevation.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Is TC ever a useful single number?** In the Framingham era yes; in the modern lipidology era, ApoB or non-HDL-C or LDL-C are preferred. TC remains useful as part of TC/HDL ratio and as a screening number, but for intervention monitoring, sub-decomposition is essential.
- **High TC in lean active young adults:** Often driven by high HDL-C (favorable). Don't intervene without checking HDL-C and LDL-C.
- **Lean mass hyperresponder phenotype on keto:** Lean keto adherents often see TC rise dramatically (LDL-C +50–100%, HDL-C +20%, TG −30%). Net TC rise; ApoB rise; long-term outcomes not RCT-tested.
- **Egg cholesterol — population-level effect:** Hyper-responders (~⅓) see TC rise; hypo-responders don't. 6-week before/after with eggs added/removed is the only reliable test.

## Source format conventions

Primary literature only. Cite PMID where available; first-author + journal + year + volume:page minimum.

**Closing reminder:** Always sub-decompose TC into LDL-C, HDL-C, and TG (to derive non-HDL-C and VLDL-C). Total cholesterol changes alone obscure direction; ApoB or non-HDL-C is the actionable target.
