# Total Cholesterol / HDL-C Ratio (TC/HDL) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage framing:** TC/HDL is a derived metric — improving it requires either lowering the numerator (TC, dominated by LDL-C with VLDL-C contribution) or raising the denominator (HDL-C). Most interventions move both directions favorably, but the magnitude of TC/HDL change reflects the **algebraic combination** rather than any single mechanism. Target: <3.5 (low risk); 4.0–5.0 (intermediate); >5.0 (high risk). The Framingham-era ratio is being supplanted by ApoB/ApoA1 and non-HDL-C in modern lipidology, but TC/HDL remains widely reported and useful for trend monitoring.

**Cross-link:** Biology and clinical interpretation in `../heart_lipids_basic.md` §"TC/HDL ratio". Existing summary in `../effect_sizes_cardiovascular.md` lines 205–212. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: TC/HDL ratio improvements are amplified in people whose interventions move both numerator and denominator (e.g., aerobic exercise lowers TC and raises HDL-C). Lean young adults already have favorable ratios; baseline-dependent floor effects apply.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Sustained aerobic exercise + Mediterranean diet (TC/HDL −0.3 to −0.6 combined). Both directions favorable.
- **Best stack of 3 (additive ~70% of linear sum):**
  1. Aerobic exercise ≥150 min/wk: TC/HDL −0.3 to −0.5
  2. Mediterranean / Portfolio diet pattern: TC/HDL −0.3 to −0.5
  3. Weight loss (5–10 kg if overweight) + smoking cessation: TC/HDL −0.3 to −0.5
  - Combined estimated delta from elevated baseline (TC/HDL 5.0): −1.0 to −1.5 (to ~3.5–4.0)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (TC/HDL ~3.5):** −0.1 to −0.3 (floor effects)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (TC/HDL >5):** −0.5 to −1.5 with the 3-stack
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** TC/HDL alone is rarely the threshold; underlying ApoB, LDL-C, or non-HDL-C drives the decision. A favorable TC/HDL ratio with elevated ApoB still justifies treatment.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- SA individuals have characteristically elevated TC/HDL ratios driven by **low HDL-C (denominator) more than high TC (numerator)**. Mean TC/HDL in MASALA ~4.5 vs ~3.8 in Whites.
- Strategies that raise HDL-C (aerobic exercise, weight loss, smoking cessation) have larger ratio impact in SA than in NW European populations because the denominator is the lever.
- Carbohydrate restriction reduces TG and indirectly raises HDL-C (CETP-mediated) — high-yield in SA insulin-resistant phenotype.
- TC/HDL in SA can mislead toward "low risk" if HDL-C is genetically low but particle function is normal; ApoB or non-HDL-C is more discriminating.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **APOE ε4:** Stronger TC rise from SFA → worse TC/HDL.
- **PCSK9 LoF:** Lower TC → better TC/HDL.
- **CETP variants (rs5882, rs708272):** Modify HDL-C; common in SA populations.
- **APOA1, ABCA1 variants:** Rare; affect HDL-C.
- **LDLR mutations (FH):** Markedly elevated TC → worse ratio.
- **APOC3 LoF:** Lower TC and higher HDL → better ratio lifelong.

**Drug interactions (composite):**
- See LDL-C, HDL-C, TG files for individual drug effects.
- **Statins:** TC/HDL improves typically −25–40% (LDL-C-driven; HDL-C minimally affected).
- **Fibrates:** TC/HDL improves via TG/VLDL drop + HDL-C rise — particularly favorable in atherogenic dyslipidemia.
- **Niacin:** TC/HDL improves substantially via HDL-C rise — but no CV outcomes benefit.
- **CETP inhibitors (obicetrapib):** TC/HDL improves dramatically via HDL-C rise; CV outcomes pending.
- **Anabolic steroids (oral 17α-alkylated):** TC/HDL worsens dramatically via HDL crash.
- **Atypical antipsychotics:** TC/HDL worsens via HDL drop and TG/LDL rise.
- **Estrogen (oral):** TC/HDL improves via HDL rise + LDL drop; offset by TG rise (which doesn't affect TC/HDL but does affect non-HDL-C).
- **Beta-blockers (non-vasodilating):** TC/HDL worsens slightly.
- **Thiazide diuretics:** TC/HDL worsens slightly.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing artifacts:**
- TC and HDL-C both fasting-insensitive.
- Acute infection: TC and HDL-C both drop; ratio variable.
- Pregnancy: TC up ~30–40%; HDL-C up ~40%; ratio relatively stable.
- Hypothyroidism: TC up 10–25%; HDL-C variable; ratio worsens.
- Acute alcohol: HDL-C transiently raised; ratio transiently improves.
- Recent intense exercise: HDL-C transiently raised.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **TC/HDL vs ApoB/ApoA1:** Both are ratio metrics. ApoB/ApoA1 is superior in MR studies (INTERHEART, Yusuf 2004) and is the EAS-recommended ratio. TC/HDL remains widely reported but is being supplanted.
- **TC/HDL in heavy drinkers:** Misleading favorable change due to HDL rise without ApoB improvement. Use ApoB.
- **TC/HDL in low-carb / keto:** Often paradoxically improves (HDL up + TC ±0) while LDL-C and ApoB rise. The ratio understates ApoB-driven risk in lean keto adherents.
- **TC/HDL in SA with low HDL-C:** Genetically low HDL-C may raise ratio without proportionate functional defect; ApoB or non-HDL-C more discriminating.
- **Anabolic steroid HDL crash reversibility:** Magnitude well-established but recovery timeline poorly characterized.

## Source format conventions

Primary literature only. Cite PMID where available; first-author + journal + year + volume:page minimum.

**Closing reminder:** TC/HDL is a derived metric. Always check ApoB or non-HDL-C in parallel. A favorable TC/HDL with elevated ApoB still warrants intervention; an unfavorable TC/HDL driven by high HDL-C in a fit lean person may not.
