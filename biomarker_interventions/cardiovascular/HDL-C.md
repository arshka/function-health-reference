# HDL Cholesterol (HDL-C) — Intervention Research

> **Important framing:** HDL-C is **not a therapeutic target.** Mendelian randomization (Voight, *Lancet* 2012) and every pharmacologic HDL-raising trial (torcetrapib ILLUMINATE, dalcetrapib dal-OUTCOMES, evacetrapib ACCELERATE, niacin AIM-HIGH and HPS2-THRIVE) show **HDL-C raising does not reduce CV events.** Use this file to (1) document what realistically moves HDL-C, (2) flag interventions that raise HDL-C without CV benefit, and (3) note that low HDL-C is more useful as a marker of insulin resistance / metabolic syndrome than as a target. Aerobic exercise, smoking cessation, and weight loss at stable weight are the only interventions with mechanistically coherent HDL benefit (improving HDL particle function, not just HDL-C mass).

**Cross-link:** Biology and clinical interpretation in `../heart_lipids_basic.md` §"HDL-C". Existing effect-size summary in `../effect_sizes_cardiovascular.md` lines 119–146. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: HDL-C interventions show smaller absolute changes in lean young adults at near-optimal baseline (>50 mg/dL); larger changes in those with low baseline (<40 mg/dL) tied to insulin resistance.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Re-frame:** Don't chase HDL-C as a target. Address the upstream metabolic state (insulin resistance, visceral adiposity, sedentary lifestyle, smoking) that causes low HDL-C — HDL-C will follow as a marker of progress, not as a cause of CV protection.
- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Sustained aerobic exercise (≥150 min/wk moderate or 75 min/wk vigorous). Magnitude: HDL-C +2–4 mg/dL; HDL function improvement is larger than HDL-C mass change. Time: 12–26 wk.
- **Best stack of 3 (additive ~70% of linear sum):**
  1. Aerobic exercise ≥150 min/wk + HIIT 1×/wk: HDL-C +3–5 mg/dL
  2. Smoking cessation: HDL-C +3.9 mg/dL (if smoker)
  3. Weight loss to stable reduced weight (10 kg): HDL-C +4.6 mg/dL
  - Combined estimated delta from low baseline (HDL-C 35): +8–12 mg/dL (to ~45–47)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (HDL-C ~50 mg/dL):** +1–3 mg/dL (floor effects)
- **Realistic 12-week delta for low baseline (HDL-C <40 mg/dL with metabolic syndrome):** +5–8 mg/dL with weight loss + exercise + smoking cessation
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** **Almost never for HDL-C as a target.** Pharmacologic HDL-C raising has failed in every CV outcomes trial (niacin AIM-HIGH/HPS2-THRIVE; CETP inhibitors ILLUMINATE/dal-OUTCOMES/ACCELERATE). If HDL-C is low because of the underlying metabolic state, treat the metabolic state. Obicetrapib (BROOKLYN/BROADWAY) raises HDL-C +130% but its CV benefit is attributed to LDL-C and Lp(a) reduction, not HDL-C rise.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- SA individuals have characteristically lower HDL-C: median ~48 mg/dL in MASALA cohort vs. ~55 mg/dL in White comparators (Kanaya et al., *Diabetes Care* 2014).
- Driver: insulin resistance and high TG, **not a primary HDL defect.** SA individuals have functionally normal HDL — they have low quantity because of high TG-driven CETP-mediated HDL catabolism.
- Implication: addressing the upstream metabolic state (TG reduction via carb restriction, exercise, weight normalization) raises HDL-C indirectly more effectively than targeting HDL-C directly.
- Alcohol and SA individuals: cohort data suggest moderate alcohol does raise HDL-C in SA but does not appear to confer the same cohort-level CV benefit seen in some European cohorts (PMR concerns; MR analyses suggest no causal benefit anywhere).
- *CETP* variants (rs5882, rs708272) are common and modify HDL-C levels; not actionable for intervention but relevant for interpreting baseline HDL-C.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **CETP variants (rs5882 I405V, rs708272 TaqIB):** Modify HDL-C ~5–10%. Carriers of high-HDL alleles do not have lower CV risk (per MR), reinforcing the non-causal relationship.
- **LIPC variants (hepatic lipase):** Affect HDL particle size and distribution.
- **APOA1 variants:** Rare; cause familial hypoalphalipoproteinemia.
- **ABCA1 variants:** Rare; Tangier disease causes very low HDL-C with paradoxical CV risk profile.
- **APOC3 LoF:** Lower TG, raise HDL-C, lower CV risk lifelong (genetic causality on TG/remnants, not HDL).
- **LCAT mutations:** Cause low HDL-C; renal effects.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Anabolic steroids (oral 17α-alkylated):** Most potent HDL suppressor known.
- **Progestins (some):** Lower HDL-C.
- **β-blockers (non-vasodilating):** Small HDL-C reduction.
- **Thiazide diuretics:** Small HDL-C reduction.
- **Retinoids (isotretinoin, acitretin):** Lower HDL-C and raise TG.
- **Atypical antipsychotics:** Lower HDL-C via metabolic syndrome induction.
- **Estrogen (oral):** Raises HDL-C +5–15% (mechanism: altered hepatic lipase activity).

**Lifestyle confounders / timing artifacts:**
- HDL-C measurement requires fasting (TG affects calculated HDL-C, but direct HDL-C is fasting-insensitive).
- Acute alcohol consumption (within 48 h) raises measured HDL-C.
- Recent intense exercise (within 24 h) raises HDL-C transiently.
- Acute infection or inflammation: HDL-C drops as part of acute-phase response (~−10–20%); wait 4–6 wk after illness for stable trend.
- Pregnancy: HDL-C rises ~40% across pregnancy.
- Hypothyroidism (TSH >5): HDL-C variable, often slightly low.
- Active liver disease: HDL-C variable.
- Recent rapid weight loss (active phase): HDL-C may transiently drop or plateau; the gain occurs at stable reduced weight.
- Cannabis use: cohort association with higher HDL-C, possibly confounded.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **HDL function vs HDL-C mass:** The Rohatgi *NEJM* 2014 and Khera *NEJM* 2011 papers established that HDL cholesterol efflux capacity predicts CV events independent of HDL-C mass. Direct measurement of efflux capacity is not yet clinical-grade. Surrogates (HDL particle size and concentration via NMR) are partial substitutes.
- **CETP inhibitors final word pending obicetrapib outcomes:** Three CETP inhibitors failed (torcetrapib, dalcetrapib, evacetrapib). Obicetrapib (BROOKLYN/BROADWAY) raises HDL-C +130% AND lowers LDL-C −36% AND lowers Lp(a) −45%. The PREVAIL CV outcomes trial (~2026 readout) will determine whether the HDL-C component contributes to benefit or whether all benefit is from LDL-C/Lp(a) reduction.
- **Anabolic steroid HDL effects:** Magnitude well-established, but reversibility timeline after cessation is poorly characterized. Anecdote suggests 3–6 months but not RCT-tested.
- **Coconut oil — unfavorable lipid pattern:** HDL-C rises but LDL-C/ApoB rises more. Despite popular health-food positioning, MR and intervention trials do not support a CV benefit. Treat as net unfavorable.
- **Cannabis and HDL-C:** Cohort signal is real but causal direction is uncertain (heavy cannabis users tend to be lean; lean status confounds HDL-C). No RCT.
- **HRV training and HDL-C:** No evidence that biofeedback or HRV training directly raises HDL-C beyond what general fitness/aerobic exercise provides.
- **Sauna and HDL-C:** Single small RCT (Pizzey 2021) showed +2–4 mg/dL; not yet replicated.
- **Lean South Asian male with low HDL-C:** Often genetically determined (CETP variants common in SA populations); intervention response may be smaller than population-average estimates suggest. Don't over-promise HDL-C improvements.

## Source format conventions

Primary literature only. Cite PMID where available; first-author + journal + year + volume:page minimum. Avoid commercial supplement promoter material and Function-Health-style opaque recommendations.

**Closing reminder:** HDL-C is a marker, not a target. The recommendation engine should never propose interventions whose primary justification is "raises HDL-C" without checking whether the same intervention also moves LDL-C/ApoB favorably. Net atherogenic particle reduction (ApoB) and TG reduction are the actionable targets.
