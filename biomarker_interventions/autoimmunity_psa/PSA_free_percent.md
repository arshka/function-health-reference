# % Free PSA (fPSA / tPSA × 100) — Intervention Research

> **Honest one-liner:** % Free PSA is a **diagnostic ratio** developed for the **gray zone of total PSA 4–10 ng/mL** in older men being screened for prostate cancer (Catalona 1998 JAMA). Its interpretive utility outside that range — and especially in men under 40 with low total PSA — is limited. **Direction of "better": HIGHER %fPSA = more benign-pattern; LOWER %fPSA = more cancer-associated** (in the screening-age population). The ratio is largely **not directly modifiable by diet or supplements**: lifestyle interventions that move PSA at all tend to move both fractions proportionally, leaving the ratio roughly unchanged. The two interventions that **selectively shift the ratio** are: (1) **5α-reductase inhibitors** (paradoxically RAISE %fPSA — complexed PSA falls more than free), and (2) **disease-state shifts** (cancer LOWERS %fPSA by shifting PSA toward complexed; BPH RAISES %fPSA; prostatitis can transiently lower %fPSA in acute inflammation). For a 20yM, **%fPSA is interpretively noisy at low total PSA** and should not drive decisions in isolation.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../prostate_psa.md` §1 ("Free vs. complexed forms"), §4 ("% Free PSA — how to interpret"), §10 ("decision logic"). For absolute free PSA, see `PSA_free.md`. For total PSA and the foundational pre-test conditions, see `PSA_total.md`.

> **CRITICAL FRAMING.** All major %fPSA interpretive thresholds (Catalona 25%, 20%, 15%, 10%) were derived in **men age 50+ with total PSA 4–10 ng/mL**. **In healthy 20yM with total PSA <2 ng/mL, the assay-level noise in measuring small free PSA values can dominate the ratio.** A %fPSA of 25% with total PSA of 0.4 ng/mL has very different uncertainty than %fPSA of 25% with total PSA of 6.0 ng/mL. **Do not over-interpret %fPSA at low total PSA.**

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

**Population caveat:** Almost no intervention literature reports %fPSA as a primary endpoint. Most data come from cohort studies of cancer biology or pharmacologic studies of 5-ARIs. Lifestyle interventions almost never report %fPSA as an outcome.

## Interpretive thresholds — the Catalona 1998 JAMA framework (reference)

| % Free PSA | Probability of prostate cancer (Catalona 1998, total PSA 4–10 ng/mL, age 50+) |
|---|---|
| 0–10% | **56%** |
| 10–15% | 28% |
| 15–20% | 20% |
| 20–25% | 16% |
| >25% | **8%** |

**Direction:** lower %fPSA = more cancer-associated; higher %fPSA = more benign-pattern.

This framework **only applies in the screening-age population at total PSA 4–10 ng/mL.** It is not validated for men <40 or for total PSA <2 ng/mL. (Catalona WJ et al., *JAMA* 1998; 279:1542-7.)

## 1. Foods that RAISE %fPSA (push it toward "benign-pattern")

**No food has been shown in any RCT to selectively raise %fPSA in healthy individuals.** Foods that move total PSA tend to move both fractions proportionally. The %fPSA-raising biology requires either pharmacologic 5-ARI or a true disease-state shift (resolution of prostatitis or PCa response to therapy, both of which can shift the equilibrium).

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude on %fPSA | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cooked tomatoes / lycopene | 30 mg/d | %fPSA: not specifically reported in lycopene trials. Both fractions presumably move down proportionally → %fPSA roughly unchanged. | **[no data]** | Inferred from Kucuk 2001, Bunker 1998 | Inferred. |
| Green tea | 400 mg EGCG/d | %fPSA: not specifically reported in Polyphenon E trials. Total PSA fell modestly (Kumar 2015); %fPSA effect not characterized. | **[no data]** | Kumar et al. *Cancer Prev Res* 2015 PMID 25873370 | Inferred. |
| Soy / isoflavones, pomegranate, cruciferous veg | Standard doses | %fPSA: not the endpoint in published trials. Inferred no selective ratio shift. | **[no data]** | Various | Inferred. |
| Mediterranean / plant-forward pattern | High adherence | %fPSA: not the endpoint; cohort data on PCa-specific mortality favorable | **[Cohort — inferred]** | HPFS (Kenfield 2014) | Pattern-level. |

## 2. Foods / Conditions that LOWER %fPSA (push it toward "cancer-associated pattern")

| Food / Condition | Pattern | Direction & magnitude | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acute bacterial prostatitis | Active infection | %fPSA can transiently fall (more PSA shifted into complexed form by acute inflammation); resolves with treatment | **[Cohort]** | Various prostatitis cohorts | Inflammation-driven. Re-test 4–6 wk post-resolution. |
| Western diet pattern (high SFA, processed meat) | Habitual | %fPSA: not specifically reported. Cohort association with PCa risk → presumably modest %fPSA-lowering effect over years if PCa develops. | **[Cohort — inferred]** | Various PCa cohorts | Indirect / years-scale. |
| Active PCa (especially higher-Gleason) | Disease state | **The defining biology**: PCa cells produce more intracellular ACT → more PSA enters circulation as already-complexed → lower %fPSA. Stronger effect with higher Gleason grade. | **[Cohort/Mechanism]** | Stenman et al. *Cancer Research* 1991; Björk et al. *Urology* 1994 | The reason %fPSA exists as a clinical test. |

## 3. Supplements

### Raising %fPSA

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Finasteride / dutasteride (5α-reductase inhibitors)** *(Rx)* | Finasteride 1 or 5 mg/d; dutasteride 0.5 mg/d | **%fPSA paradoxically RISES modestly on 5-ARI** because complexed PSA falls more than free PSA in the equilibrium shift. The total PSA falls ~50%; the free fraction falls less; the complexed fraction falls more → ratio rises. Magnitude: %fPSA increase of approximately **+3 to +6 percentage points** over baseline at steady-state. | 6–12 mo | **[RCT]** | Andriole et al. *Urology* 1998 PMID 9510311; Etzioni et al. (PSA adjustment in PCPT); Lee KJU 2010 | **Critical for interpretation:** if a man on finasteride has %fPSA = 28%, this is not necessarily reassuring — the ratio is artificially elevated by the drug's mechanism. The total PSA must be ×2-corrected, and the %fPSA threshold is shifted upward. |
| Other supplements (vitamin D, lycopene, soy, green tea, etc.) | Standard | %fPSA: not selectively shifted in any documented intervention | — | **[no data]** | Various | No data. |

## 4. Activities & Behaviors

The biggest %fPSA-affecting behavioral factors are **pre-test confounders that selectively shift fractions** (less by ratio than by absolute free PSA).

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude on %fPSA | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Avoid ejaculation 48 h pre-draw** | Abstain ≥48 h | %fPSA can transiently *fall* slightly post-ejaculation because complexed PSA has slower clearance and continues to rise as free PSA returns to baseline. Impact on ratio is smaller than impact on absolute fractions. | Hours–48 h | **[1-Trial]** | Tchetgen *J Urol* 1996 (total focus); Herschman *Urology* 1997 (split-fraction) | Wait 48 h. |
| **Avoid vigorous bicycling 24–48 h pre-draw** | Abstain ≥48 h | %fPSA: not specifically characterized; both fractions presumably rise proportionally → modest %fPSA effect | 48 h | **[1-Trial]** | Mejak *PLOS One* 2013 (total focus) | Wait 48 h. |
| **Avoid DRE / prostate exam 3 d pre-draw** | Wait 3 d | %fPSA: small effect | 72 h | **[1-Trial]** | Crawford 1999; Yuan 1992 | Less impactful. |
| **Avoid prostate biopsy 6 wk pre-draw** | Wait 6 wk minimum | %fPSA: complexed fraction rises massively post-biopsy → %fPSA falls; persists for weeks | 6 wk | **[Cohort]** | Various biopsy cohorts | Relevant only if recent biopsy. |
| **Treat any UTI / prostatitis before re-test** | Treat then wait 4–6 wk | %fPSA can transiently fall in acute prostatitis (PSA shifted toward complexed by inflammation); recovers as inflammation resolves | 4–6 wk | **[Cohort]** | Ulleryd *BJU Int* 1999 | Most clinically meaningful confounder. |

| Aerobic / resistance / HIIT exercise | Standard doses | %fPSA: not the endpoint in published trials | — | **[no data]** | — | — |
| Sleep, stress, alcohol, smoking | Standard | %fPSA: no direct effect documented | — | **[no data]** | — | — |

## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

**No wearable metric reliably correlates with %fPSA.** %fPSA is driven by prostate biology and pre-analytical handling. The same confounders that affect total and free PSA apply.

| Wearable metric | Direction of relationship | Magnitude | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cycling / sexual activity / illness logs | Indirect (via fractions) | Modest acute | **[Inferred]** | — | Pre-test instructions handle this. |
| Other (HRV, sleep, steps) | No documented correlation | — | **[no data]** | — | — |

**Practical recommendation:** %fPSA is more sensitive than total PSA to pre-analytical variability and small assay noise at low total PSA. Use it as confirmatory information when total PSA is in the gray zone (4–10 ng/mL in older men), not as a standalone metric in young men.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention (for accurate %fPSA measurement):** Same as total PSA — **standardize pre-test conditions** (no ejaculation 48 h, no cycling 48 h, no DRE 3 d, no biopsy 6 wk, treat UTI/prostatitis), AND ensure **strict pre-analytical sample handling** (prompt centrifugation, freeze, no thaw cycles). %fPSA is more sensitive to handling than total PSA.
- **Highest-leverage intervention for changing %fPSA:** **5α-reductase inhibitor** is the only intervention that reliably and selectively raises %fPSA. **This is interpretive, not therapeutic** — patients on 5-ARI need a recalibrated %fPSA threshold.
- **Best dietary stack of 3:** Not applicable — no food intervention selectively shifts %fPSA. The recommendations from PSA_total.md apply (cooked tomato, green tea, Mediterranean pattern), but they don't preferentially raise %fPSA.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline:** **None expected.** %fPSA in young men with low total PSA is interpretively noisy; small changes are within assay variability.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for someone with elevated baseline:**
  - **Resolution of acute prostatitis** → %fPSA returns to baseline (typically rises post-treatment).
  - **5-ARI initiation** → %fPSA paradoxically rises +3 to +6 percentage points.
  - **PCa progression** → %fPSA falls; magnitude varies with Gleason and disease burden.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** %fPSA is an interpretive tool, not a direct therapeutic target. The actions taken in response to a low %fPSA in the screening population (biopsy decision, MRI, etc.) are downstream of the value, not interventions on it.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **PCa biology and %fPSA distribution in SA young men:** No SA-specific %fPSA reference data exist. The Catalona thresholds were derived in US populations and have not been validated in SA cohorts at any age.
- For a 20yM SA, **%fPSA at low total PSA is interpretively even less informative** because there is no validated reference distribution for the demographic.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **Total PSA matters first.** If total PSA is <2 ng/mL, %fPSA carries meaningful assay noise and should not be over-interpreted in isolation. The Catalona thresholds (10%, 15%, 20%, 25%) are validated for total PSA 4–10 ng/mL.
- **Sample handling (CRITICAL):** Free PSA is more labile than complexed PSA. Prolonged room-temperature storage, freeze-thaw cycles, or delayed centrifugation can degrade free PSA selectively → falsely low %fPSA. **Strict pre-analytical handling matters more for %fPSA than for total PSA.** Most reputable labs aliquot and freeze rapidly.
- **5α-reductase inhibitors:** %fPSA paradoxically RISES on 5-ARI (+3 to +6 percentage points). A %fPSA of 25% on finasteride is roughly equivalent to ~22% off finasteride. (Andriole *Urology* 1998.)
- **Acute prostatitis:** %fPSA can fall (PSA shifted to complexed by inflammation). Re-test 4–6 wk post-treatment.
- **Recent ejaculation, cycling, DRE, biopsy, UTI:** All affect both fractions; net effect on ratio is smaller than effect on absolute fractions, but real.
- **Aging:** %fPSA naturally decreases modestly with age in cohort data; thresholds derived in older men.
- **Assay calibration:** Hybritech vs. WHO. Both total and free assays should be from the same calibration family for valid ratio.
- **Genetic modifiers:** BRCA2, BRCA1, HOXB13 G84E, Lynch syndrome inform PCa risk and therefore inform interpretation of low %fPSA when total PSA is elevated. Not modifiers of %fPSA generation per se.
- **Disease state:** PCa lowers %fPSA (cancer cells produce intracellular ACT → more complexed PSA in circulation). BPH raises %fPSA (BPH tissue produces less ACT → more free PSA in circulation).

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Does any food, supplement, or behavior selectively change %fPSA in healthy individuals with low baseline PSA?** Honest answer: **no.** The interventions that selectively shift %fPSA are pharmacologic (5-ARI) or disease-state-related (prostatitis resolution, PCa response to therapy).
- **Pre-analytical variability:** Free PSA's higher lability vs. complexed PSA can produce false-low %fPSA — a real source of "abnormal" results in real-world practice.
- **%fPSA reference ranges in young men:** **Not formally established.** Catalona thresholds do not apply.
- **PHI, 4Kscore vs. %fPSA:** The Prostate Health Index (combines tPSA + fPSA + [-2]proPSA) and 4Kscore (tPSA + fPSA + intact PSA + hK2) outperform %fPSA alone in predicting clinically significant PCa in older screening populations. They are downstream interpretive tools, not interventions for %fPSA itself.
- **Effect of NSAIDs, statins on %fPSA:** weak signal in cohort data; not pursued in RCT.
- **Effect of testosterone replacement on %fPSA:** total and free PSA both rise; ratio effect not consistently characterized.
- **Use of %fPSA at total PSA <2 ng/mL:** interpretation framework essentially absent; do not over-interpret.
