# Free PSA (fPSA) — Intervention Research

> **Honest one-liner:** Free PSA (the unbound, non-complexed fraction of total PSA, with serum half-life ~70 minutes) **does not have a well-established intervention literature** in its own right. Almost all published interventions report **total PSA** as primary endpoint and either don't measure free PSA or report it as a secondary metric. The single most important practical fact: **free PSA is interpreted almost exclusively as a ratio (% Free PSA = fPSA / tPSA × 100)**, and that ratio is what carries clinical meaning — see companion file `PSA_free_percent.md`. Most "moves" in absolute free PSA simply mirror the total: the same confounders that raise total PSA (ejaculation, cycling, prostatitis, biopsy) also raise free PSA. The few interventions that selectively change *free* vs. *complexed* fractions are 5α-reductase inhibitors (free fraction can rise modestly as complexed PSA falls more) and disease-state shifts (cancer drives PSA into complexed form, lowering % free).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../prostate_psa.md` §1 ("Free vs. complexed forms"), §4 ("% Free PSA — how to interpret"). Intervention biology that moves total PSA mostly moves free PSA in the same direction. **For interpretive interventions, see `PSA_free_percent.md`.** For pre-test conditions, see `PSA_total.md` §4.

> **CRITICAL FRAMING.** Same as for total PSA: essentially all literature is in men 40+ being screened for prostate cancer. Free PSA in a 20yM is rarely independently meaningful — it's typically reported only when total PSA is non-trivial (e.g., >2 ng/mL). At very low total PSA values, free PSA assay noise can dominate the ratio.

| Tag | Meaning |

| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |

**Population caveat:** Almost no intervention literature reports free PSA as primary endpoint. Effects below are **largely inferred from total PSA studies** + biological reasoning about the free / complexed equilibrium.

## 4. Activities & Behaviors

The same pre-test confounders that affect total PSA also affect absolute free PSA. Most of these will **proportionally affect % free PSA only modestly** — but the absolute free PSA reading shifts substantially.

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Avoid ejaculation 48 h pre-draw** | Abstain ≥48 h | Free PSA acutely rises with total after ejaculation; % free PSA can transiently *fall* slightly because complexed PSA has slower clearance and continues to rise relatively | Hours–48 h | **[RCT]** | Tchetgen *J Urol* 1996 (total PSA primary); Herschman *Urology* 1997 (split-fraction analysis) | The **% free** ratio impact is smaller than absolute free PSA impact. Still — wait 48 h. |
| **Avoid vigorous bicycling 24–48 h pre-draw** | Abstain ≥48 h | Both fractions rise transiently (perineal pressure on prostate) | 48 h | **[1-Trial]** | Mejak *PLOS One* 2013 | Wait 48 h. |
| **Avoid DRE / prostate exam 3 d pre-draw** | Wait 3 d | Modest rise in both fractions | 72 h | **[1-Trial]** | Crawford 1999; Yuan 1992 | Less impactful than ejaculation/biopsy. |
| **Avoid prostate biopsy 6 wk pre-draw** | Wait 6 wk minimum | Massive rise in both fractions | 6 wk | **[Cohort]** | Multiple biopsy cohorts | Relevant only if recent biopsy. |
| **Treat any UTI / prostatitis before re-test** | Treat then wait 4–6 wk | Both fractions can rise dramatically with prostatitis; **% free can fall transiently** in acute prostatitis (more PSA shifted into complexed form by inflammation) | 4–6 wk | **[Cohort]** | Ulleryd *BJU Int* 1999; CP/CPPS literature | Most clinically meaningful confounder in young men. |
| Aerobic / resistance / HIIT exercise | Standard doses | Free PSA: no direct trial; total PSA modest changes in active surveillance trials | — | **[no data]** | — | Indirect. |
| Sleep, stress, alcohol, smoking | Standard | Free PSA: no direct effect documented | — | **[no data]** | — | Indirect. |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention (for accurate free PSA measurement):** Same as total PSA — **standardize pre-test conditions** (no ejaculation 48 h, no cycling 48 h, no DRE 3 d, no biopsy 6 wk, treat UTI/prostatitis, disclose 5-ARI use).
- **Highest-leverage single intervention (for changing absolute free PSA):** **5α-reductase inhibitor** is the only intervention that selectively shifts the free vs. complexed equilibrium (free fraction relatively spared, complexed PSA falls more) → % free PSA paradoxically rises modestly on 5-ARI treatment.
- **Best stack of 3:** Identical to total PSA — cooked tomato + green tea + Mediterranean diet pattern. Effects on absolute free PSA are inferred from total PSA effects.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (free PSA ~0.1–0.3 ng/mL):** **None expected.** Floor effects dominate; modest dietary changes will not move a free PSA already at the population low-end.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for someone with elevated baseline:** Same as total PSA — driven by treating the confounder (antibiotics for prostatitis, abstinence pre-draw, etc.) or by 5-ARI initiation.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** Same indications as total PSA — 5-ARI for BPH, antibiotics for prostatitis, definitive therapy for PCa.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

Free PSA confounders **largely overlap with total PSA confounders** (see PSA_total.md §8). Specific to free PSA:

- **Sample handling:** **Free PSA is less stable than complexed PSA**; whole-blood storage at room temperature, freeze-thaw cycles, or delayed centrifugation can degrade free PSA selectively → falsely low free PSA → falsely low % free PSA. **Strict pre-analytical handling matters more for free PSA / %fPSA than for total PSA.** Most reputable labs aliquot and freeze rapidly.
- **Time at room temperature:** Free PSA can drop 5–10% after 24 h at room temperature; sample should be processed promptly.
- **Assay calibration:** Hybritech vs. WHO 96/670 calibration mismatch affects free PSA similarly to total PSA. Free PSA assays are calibrated against the WHO 90:10 standard or Hybritech proprietary calibrators.
- **5α-reductase inhibitors:** ↓ total PSA ~50%; ↓ free PSA proportionally less → **% free PSA modestly rises** (not falls). Disclose on every panel. (Andriole *Urology* 1998.)
- **Ejaculation, cycling, DRE, biopsy, UTI/prostatitis:** All raise free PSA proportionally (or, for prostatitis, can shift fraction toward complexed → falsely low % free).
- **Genetic modifiers:** Same as total PSA — BRCA2, BRCA1, HOXB13 G84E, Lynch syndrome inform interpretation, not free PSA generation per se.
- **Hemodilution (BMI):** Larger men have lower measured PSA fractions; dilution affects both proportionally.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Does any food, supplement, or behavior selectively change free PSA without proportional change in total PSA?** Honest answer: **only 5α-reductase inhibitors are well-documented to do this.** Disease-state shifts (cancer development) shift PSA toward complexed form (lowering %fPSA), but no lifestyle intervention has been shown to selectively raise %fPSA in healthy individuals.
- **Pre-analytical variability:** Free PSA is more sensitive to sample handling than total PSA. Lab-to-lab variability is meaningful.
- **Free PSA at low total PSA:** When total PSA is <1.5 ng/mL, the % free PSA ratio carries meaningful measurement noise. Single readings should not drive decisions.
- **Effect of NSAIDs, statins on free vs. complexed PSA fractions:** weak signal in cohort data; not pursued in RCT.
- **Effect of testosterone replacement on free vs. complexed PSA:** total PSA can rise modestly; effect on fractions less characterized.
- **Newer kallikrein-based assays (PHI, 4Kscore):** combine total PSA + free PSA + [-2]proPSA + hK2 in proprietary algorithms. These are downstream interpretive tools, not interventions for moving free PSA.
