# TSAT — Iron % Saturation (Transferrin Saturation) — Intervention Research

> Highest-leverage understanding: **TSAT is the most useful single-snapshot iron-status indicator**, especially when ferritin is confounded by inflammation. **TSAT = (Serum Iron / TIBC) × 100**, expressed as %. It estimates the fraction of transferrin's iron-binding sites currently loaded. Optimal range for a healthy young man is **25–40%**. Two main intervention contexts: **(1) raising TSAT from <20%** in iron deficiency via the same toolkit as ferritin (oral or IV iron); the early-response window is faster than ferritin (6–10 wk vs 3–5 mo). **(2) lowering TSAT from >45%** in iron overload, primarily via **therapeutic phlebotomy** in HFE hemochromatosis (TSAT ↓ ~3–5% per 500 mL session). TSAT is more sensitive than ferritin alone for **iron-overload screening** (HFE workup triggered by TSAT >45% on repeat fasting morning draws).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology and clinical interpretation lives in `../../nutrients_vitamins_minerals.md` §3 (TSAT) and `../../effect_sizes_nutrients.md` §TSAT. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

**Critical interpretation pearls:**
- **Iron deficiency:** TSAT <20%, often <16%
- **Hemochromatosis screening trigger:** TSAT >45% (AASLD 2011) or >50% (EASL 2022) in men, on **fasting morning** repeat draw
- **Functional iron deficiency in inflammation:** TSAT <20% with normal/high ferritin (CRP usually elevated)
- **Anemia of chronic disease:** TSAT 10–20%, low TIBC, high ferritin
- TSAT moves **transiently to 60–80%** for 24–72 h after IV iron — this is **expected, not toxicity**

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

**For LOW TSAT (<20%):**

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Same toolkit as ferritin — **alternate-day ferrous sulfate 60 mg** or ferrous bisglycinate 25 mg, fasting morning, with vitamin C, away from coffee/tea/dairy. TSAT is the **early-response** marker (rises within 4–8 wk vs ferritin which lags by months).
- **Best stack of 3 (oral repletion):** (1) Iron supplement on alternate days; (2) Increase heme iron meals (red meat 3×/wk, oysters/clams 1×/wk, fortified cereals); (3) Eliminate absorption inhibitors at iron-meal/dose times (separate tea/coffee/dairy by ≥2 h).
- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (TSAT 25–40%):** Negligible change — system is hepcidin-throttled at adequate iron status. Avoid supplementation unless deficiency confirmed.
- **Realistic 12-week delta — low TSAT (<20% with low ferritin):** TSAT ↑ to 25–35% by week 8–12 with adherent oral iron; IV iron achieves this within 1–2 weeks.
- **Where pharmacotherapy (IV iron) becomes necessary:** Same as ferritin — oral failure, GI intolerance, malabsorption, severe deficiency, ongoing blood loss outpacing oral repletion.

**For HIGH TSAT (>45–50%):**

- **Step 1: Confirm — repeat fasting morning TSAT.** Single-snapshot TSAT can be misleading due to diurnal variation, recent iron supplement, recent IV iron, recent meal.
- **Step 2: Pair with ferritin and CRP.** TSAT >45% + ferritin >300 (men) → HFE genotyping (AASLD 2011, EASL 2022). TSAT >45% + ferritin normal → may be early iron loading; recheck in 3–6 mo.
- **Step 3 — true overload (HFE+ / TSAT >50% on repeat):** Therapeutic phlebotomy 500 mL weekly until ferritin 50–100 µg/L (typically 1–2 yr induction); TSAT will fall ~3–5% per session.
- **Step 4 — non-overload elevated TSAT** (e.g., TSAT 45–50% with normal ferritin in young adult): May be normal physiologic variation; recheck fasting morning, ensure no recent iron supplement, no recent vigorous exercise affecting timing. If consistently >45% on repeat, consider HFE genotyping even with normal ferritin (early HH can have elevated TSAT before ferritin rises).
- **What does NOT work for high TSAT:** "Iron-binding" supplements (IP6), low-iron diets (impractical, only modest impact), curcumin (chelation magnitude tiny relative to body iron stores). Phlebotomy is the only effective intervention in true overload.

**For NORMAL TSAT (25–40%):** No intervention indicated. TSAT in this range is ideal.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Vegetarian SA male phenotype** with low-normal TSAT (15–22%) is common. Same intervention framework as ferritin (vitamin C with iron meals, separate chai, soak/ferment legumes, cast iron cookware, supplement if frank deficiency). TSAT is the early-response marker for repletion success.
- **TMPRSS6 (rs855791) T allele is more prevalent in SA** (~50–60% allele frequency vs 30–40% European). T allele → higher hepcidin → blunted oral iron absorption → smaller TSAT response per oral iron dose. **Recommend alternate-day dosing earlier; consider IV escalation if no TSAT response by 6–8 wk.**
- **HFE C282Y is rare in SA** (<1% allele frequency vs ~6% NW European). Elevated TSAT in SA male is more often DIOS (metabolic), alcohol-related, or transient/timing artifact than true HH. HFE genotyping useful but lower pre-test probability.
- **HFE H63D is moderately prevalent in SA** (~10%). H63D homozygotes / compound heterozygotes can have mildly elevated TSAT (35–50%) but rarely clinical overload. Monitor; don't immediately phlebotomize.
- **Lean SA male with insulin resistance** (HbA1c 5.7–6.4, low HDL, high TG) on westernized diet can show DIOS pattern: ferritin 300–800 with TSAT typically normal/mildly elevated (25–40%). Treat metabolic syndrome, not iron stores.
- **Cultural OCP use in SA women** (relevant for partner data context) — TSAT is mechanically lowered by estrogen-driven TIBC rise. Don't misclassify as iron deficiency without ferritin and serum Fe context.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **HFE rs1800562 (C282Y)** — homozygotes show elevated TSAT (often >50%) and ferritin from young adulthood; compound heterozygotes (C282Y/H63D) may show milder elevation. **Do NOT recommend iron supplementation in C282Y homozygotes.**
- **HFE rs1799945 (H63D)** — generally mild effect on TSAT; H63D/H63D rarely overloads.
- **TMPRSS6 rs855791** — T allele → blunted TSAT response to oral iron (high hepcidin); favor alternate-day or IV iron escalation.
- **TFR2** rare variants — hemochromatosis type 3 with elevated TSAT.
- **SLC40A1 (ferroportin disease)** — variable TSAT pattern (classical = high ferritin with normal/low TSAT; atypical = high TSAT also).

**Drug interactions (RAISE TSAT):**
- Recent oral iron supplement (within 24 h) → false-elevated serum Fe → falsely high TSAT (often 50–70%)
- Recent IV iron (within 4 weeks) → markedly elevated TSAT (60–80%)
- Vitamin A / retinoids high dose — mobilize iron, transiently raise serum Fe → TSAT
- Hemolytic anemias / hemolysis (any cause) — release of iron from RBC breakdown raises TSAT

**Drug interactions (LOWER TSAT):**
- **Estrogen-containing OCP / HRT** — raise TIBC denominator → mechanically lower TSAT by 10–25%. Major confound.
- **Pregnancy** — TSAT physiologically falls
- **PPIs (chronic ≥2 yr)** — IDA OR 2.49 (Lam 2017 PMID 27890768) → secondary low TSAT
- **H2 blockers (chronic)** — IDA OR 1.58
- **Calcium and zinc supplements** with iron — DMT1 competition, blunt iron absorption
- **Tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, levothyroxine, bisphosphonates** — chelation in gut, blunt iron absorption
- **Curcumin high-dose** — theoretical iron chelation
- **Glucocorticoids** — variable; may affect iron mobilization

**Lifestyle / physiologic confounders for TSAT (CRITICAL):**
- **Acute illness / inflammation (CRP >5)** — TSAT ↓ 5–20 percentage points within hours via hepcidin-ferroportin axis. **Always pair TSAT with CRP**; TSAT <20% in active inflammation does NOT mean iron deficiency.
- **Acute strenuous exercise (>90 min vigorous within 24 h of draw)** — TSAT ↓ via post-exercise hepcidin spike (peaks 3–6 h post-exercise)
- **Endurance training (chronic)** — TSAT trends downward over months
- **Diurnal variation** — peaks late morning (~11 AM); standardize to fasting morning
- **Day-to-day variability** — TSAT shows 30–50% within-person variation (driven mostly by serum Fe variation); don't act on a single outlier; repeat fasting morning
- **Recent meal (especially iron-rich meat)** — false elevation
- **Pregnancy** — physiologic decline
- **OCP / HRT** — mechanical decline via TIBC numerator
- **Frequent blood donation** — TSAT trends downward
- **Whole blood loss / occult GI bleed** — TSAT falls

**Timing artifacts (most important — repeat list for emphasis):**
1. Oral iron within 24 h → false-elevated TSAT (often 50–70%)
2. IV iron within 4 weeks → very high TSAT (60–80%)
3. Acute illness in past 2 weeks → false low TSAT
4. Hard endurance session within 24 h → false low TSAT
5. Afternoon/evening draw → ~30% lower than morning peak
6. Non-fasting state → false elevation if recent iron-rich meal

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal TSAT target for healthy young men** — conventional reference range 20–50% (lab-dependent); functional/sports medicine often targets 25–40%. **Insufficient RCT evidence** that targeting TSAT 35 vs 25 in non-deficient adults yields measurable benefit. Avoid optimization above ~40% (no evidence of benefit; small risk of subclinical iron loading trajectory).
- **TSAT cutoff for HH screening** — AASLD 2011 uses >45%; EASL 2022 uses >50%; clinical practice varies. The single-snapshot TSAT is unreliable; repeat fasting morning draws are essential before triggering HFE genotyping.
- **TSAT reliability in OCP/HRT users** — well-recognized confound but no validated correction algorithm. Some clinicians use TIBC-adjusted reference ranges; not standardized.
- **TSAT vs sTfR for IDA diagnosis in inflammation** — sTfR (soluble transferrin receptor) and the sTfR/log-ferritin index outperform TSAT and ferritin in active inflammation but are not on most consumer iron panels (including Function Health typical panels). Recommend ordering sTfR via Quest/Labcorp when CRP elevated.
- **Hepcidin as the gold-standard iron-status marker** — research-grade only; not yet widely clinical.
- **Single-snapshot reliability** — biological variability of TSAT is large enough (30–50% within-person) that single values often misclassify. Serial within-person tracking is more useful than population reference comparison.
- **TSAT response time to oral iron in non-anemic ID** — Stoffel data are in iron-depleted but not necessarily anemic women; transportability to males, healthy young adults, athletes is presumed but not directly tested in pivotal trial.
- **Hypophosphatemia as IV iron complication** — well-documented with FCM (~30–50% develop sub-2.5 mg/dL phosphate); lower with ferric derisomaltose. Worth screening if recurrent FCM dosing.
- **TSAT trajectory in DIOS / MASLD treatment** — most studies focus on ferritin; TSAT typically normal in DIOS so trajectory is less clinically actionable.
