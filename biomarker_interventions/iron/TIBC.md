# TIBC — Total Iron-Binding Capacity — Intervention Research

> Highest-leverage understanding: **TIBC is rarely the direct target of optimization** — it is a confirmatory pattern marker. TIBC reflects circulating transferrin (each transferrin molecule binds 2 Fe³⁺; TIBC ≈ transferrin × 1.41). It is **negatively regulated by iron stores** (high in deficiency, low in overload) and is **a negative acute-phase reactant** (low in inflammation, malnutrition, liver disease, nephrotic syndrome). The single most clinically valuable use is **distinguishing iron deficiency anemia (high TIBC) from anemia of chronic disease (low/normal TIBC)** alongside ferritin and TSAT. Interventions move TIBC indirectly through iron status, hepatic transferrin synthesis, or estrogen exposure (which raises hepatic transferrin output ~25–30%).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology lives in `../../nutrients_vitamins_minerals.md` §4 (TIBC) and `../../effect_sizes_nutrients.md` §TIBC. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

**Key conceptual frame:** TIBC is **not optimized in isolation**. The actionable lever is the iron-pattern interpretation: low Fe + high TIBC + low ferritin → IDA (treat with iron); low Fe + low/normal TIBC + normal/high ferritin → ACD (treat the underlying inflammation, iron supplementation often ineffective without IV iron + addressing inflammation). This file documents what moves TIBC so unexpected directions can be explained, not as standalone targets.

## 3. Supplements

### Lowering TIBC (raising iron stores → suppressing transferrin upregulation)

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Oral ferrous sulfate / ferrous bisglycinate** | 60 mg or 25 mg elemental Fe (alternate-day or daily) | TIBC ↓ from elevated (~400–450 µg/dL in IDA) toward 300–360 over 8–16 wk as ferritin replets | 4–12 wk | **[RCT, Meta]** | Cançado 2024 *Am J Med* meta; Stoffel 2017 PMID 29032957 | TIBC fall is the verification of effective repletion (negative correlation with ferritin) |
| **IV ferric carboxymaltose** | 1000 mg single dose | TIBC ↓ noticeably within 4–8 wk as iron stores replenish | 4–8 wk | **[RCT]** | AFFIRM-AHF; PIVOTAL trials | Faster TIBC normalization than oral; useful confirmation of repletion |
| **IV iron sucrose, ferumoxytol, ferric derisomaltose** | Standard regimens | TIBC ↓ comparably to FCM | 4–6 wk | **[RCT]** | Multiple comparison trials; PMC12099480 meta | Equivalent direction; magnitude per dose varies |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **TIBC is rarely the direct target.** The actionable use is **pattern interpretation**:
  - Low Fe + **high TIBC** + low ferritin + low TSAT → **IDA** — treat with iron
  - Low Fe + **low/normal TIBC** + normal/high ferritin + low TSAT → **ACD** — treat the underlying inflammation; iron alone often ineffective
  - High Fe + **low TIBC** + high ferritin + high TSAT → **iron overload** (hereditary or DIOS) — workup HFE, manage source
  - Normal pattern → no action

- **Highest-leverage single intervention to NORMALIZE TIBC (when high in IDA):** Iron repletion — same approach as ferritin (oral or IV iron). TIBC falls as ferritin rises, lagging by 2–4 weeks.

- **Highest-leverage single intervention to NORMALIZE TIBC (when low in ACD or malnutrition):** Treat the underlying cause — anti-inflammatory therapy, protein repletion, treat infection, treat liver/renal disease. TIBC alone is not the lever.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (TIBC 250–400 µg/dL):** No intervention indicated. TIBC is in physiologic range.

- **Realistic 12-week delta — IDA (TIBC ~450 µg/dL):** With adherent oral iron repletion, TIBC ↓ by 50–100 µg/dL over 12 wk as ferritin rises.

- **When pharmacotherapy is necessary:** Same as ferritin — oral failure, intolerance, malabsorption, or rapid correction needed.

- **Practical pearl:** A markedly elevated TIBC (>500 µg/dL) in a patient on OCP/HRT is often estrogen-driven and not iron-status pathology — verify the iron status pattern via TSAT and ferritin, not TIBC in isolation.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **No SA-specific TIBC data of note.** SA vegetarian males with chronic iron deficiency show the expected high-TIBC pattern; treatment is iron repletion as in any IDA.
- **Premenopausal SA women on OCP** often show high TIBC (estrogen effect) which mechanically lowers TSAT — interpret iron status via ferritin + serum Fe pattern, not TSAT in isolation.
- **TMPRSS6 T allele** (common in SA) → blunted iron absorption → may show higher TIBC than expected for given ferritin (compensatory upregulation); same management implications as elsewhere — escalate to alternate-day or IV iron.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **HFE C282Y/C282Y** → iron overload → low TIBC pattern. DO NOT recommend iron supplementation.
- **HFE H63D** → variable, generally minimal direct TIBC effect.
- **TMPRSS6** → minimally affects TIBC directly; effect is on iron absorption.
- **Atransferrinemia / hypotransferrinemia** (rare congenital) — markedly low TIBC with severe iron-loading anemia; specialist condition.

**Drug interactions (RAISE TIBC):**
- **Oral contraceptive pills, estrogen-containing HRT, transdermal estradiol** — TIBC ↑ 25–30% via hepatic transferrin upregulation. Major confound for TSAT calculation.
- **Pregnancy** — TIBC ↑ 25–30% by 2nd–3rd trimester
- **Iron deficiency** of any cause — compensatory TIBC ↑

**Drug interactions (LOWER TIBC):**
- **Iron repletion** (oral or IV) — TIBC ↓ as stores normalize
- **Glucocorticoids (chronic)** — can modestly lower TIBC via immunomodulation
- **Androgens, testosterone replacement** — may modestly lower TIBC vs estrogen effect

**Lifestyle / physiologic confounders for TIBC:**
- **Inflammation (CRP >5)** — TIBC ↓ as negative APR
- **Infection** — TIBC ↓ acutely
- **Liver disease (cirrhosis, severe MASH, hepatitis)** — TIBC ↓ from impaired hepatic synthesis
- **Nephrotic syndrome** — TIBC ↓ from urinary transferrin loss
- **Protein malnutrition / kwashiorkor** — TIBC ↓ from impaired hepatic synthesis
- **Chronic critical illness, cachexia (cancer, advanced HF)** — TIBC ↓
- **Frequent blood donors** — TIBC ↑ (compensatory iron-deficient response)
- **Pregnancy** — TIBC ↑ (estrogen + iron demand)

**Timing artifacts:**
- TIBC has minor diurnal variation (smaller than serum Fe)
- Less affected by recent meals or single iron doses than serum Fe
- **Less variable day-to-day than serum Fe** — reasonable single-snapshot reliability

**Lab method note:**
- Most modern TIBC assays directly measure transferrin and calculate TIBC = transferrin × 1.41 (some labs use 1.4 or 1.49). Old "iron-saturation" assays measured TIBC by adding iron to serum to saturate transferrin then back-titrating — this method is largely obsolete. Some labs report transferrin (mg/dL); some report TIBC (µg/dL) — convert via factor 1.41 if needed.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **TIBC reference ranges vary substantially across labs** (250–450 µg/dL is common; some labs use 240–410 or 260–470). Single-value interpretation requires knowing the lab's reference range.
- **Marginal utility of TIBC in modern panels** — soluble transferrin receptor (sTfR) and the sTfR/log-ferritin index outperform TIBC for diagnosing iron deficiency in inflammation and chronic disease, but sTfR is not on most consumer iron panels (including Function Health typical panels).
- **TIBC as a longitudinal marker of repletion** — useful conceptually but ferritin trends are more sensitive in routine practice.
- **Estrogen-driven TIBC elevation in OCP/HRT users** — well-established (25–30% rise) but the magnitude and interaction with iron status is poorly characterized in trials. Adjusted reference ranges for OCP/HRT users are not standard.
- **TIBC change with weight loss in MASLD/DIOS** — most studies focus on ferritin; TIBC trajectory in DIOS treatment is under-reported. Theoretically TIBC should normalize upward as MASLD-related inflammation resolves.
- **Optimal TIBC for healthy young men** — not meaningfully studied as a standalone target; conventional reference range (250–450) is the practical anchor.
- **TIBC vs unsaturated iron-binding capacity (UIBC)** — UIBC = TIBC − serum Fe; some labs report UIBC. UIBC has the same interpretation issues as TIBC and offers no additional clinical value over TSAT.
