# Serum Iron — Intervention Research

> Highest-leverage understanding: **Serum iron is a noisy snapshot.** Day-to-day within-person variability is 30–50%, with strong **circadian peaking late morning** (Kim 2017, PMID 28947322; AJH 2024 PMID via 10.1002/ajh.27447). It moves transiently 50–100 µg/dL after a single oral iron dose and falls 20–60 µg/dL within hours of any inflammatory event (hepcidin-mediated macrophage iron sequestration; Nemeth 2004 *Science*). **Serum iron has no clinically useful optimization target independent of TSAT** — its sole role is as the numerator of TSAT and as part of the iron-pattern interpretation. Interventions that move ferritin move serum iron in parallel but with much higher noise. The single most actionable point: **standardize draw timing** (fasting morning, oral iron held ≥24 h, no recent IV iron) before reading any value.

## 3. Supplements

### Raising serum iron

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ferrous sulfate (60 mg elemental Fe)** | Single oral dose | Serum Fe ↑ 50–100 µg/dL peak at 2–4 h post-dose; back to baseline by ~8–12 h in iron-replete; rise smaller in depleted (more avid uptake into stores) | 2–4 h peak; chronic 4–8 wk for sustained fasting Fe rise | **[RCT]** | Hallberg 1958 absorption studies; pharmacology | KEY DRAW HYGIENE: hold oral iron ≥24 h before iron-studies blood draw or get false-elevated result |
| **Ferrous sulfate (sustained dosing)** | 60 mg/d × 8 wk | Fasting serum Fe ↑ from 40–60 µg/dL (deficient) to 80–110 µg/dL over 8 wk; TSAT ↑ from 10–15% to 25–35% | 4–8 wk | **[RCT, Meta]** | Cançado 2024 *Am J Med* meta; Stoffel 2017 PMID 29032957 | Serum Fe is the early responder; ferritin lags by months |
| **Ferrous bisglycinate** | 25 mg elemental Fe | Acute serum Fe rise similar magnitude per equivalent absorbed Fe; sustained TSAT/Fe rise comparable to ferrous sulfate at half elemental dose | 8–12 wk sustained | **[RCT]** | Bovell-Benjamin 2000 *AJCN*; Milman 2012 meta | Better tolerability profile |
| **Vitamin C (with iron supplement)** | 100–250 mg co-ingested | Acute serum Fe rise larger than iron alone (×1.5–2 in fasting state); sustained gain modest in real-world adherence | Per dose; sustained 8–16 wk | **[RCT, conflicting]** | Cook & Reddy 2001 PMID 11124756; Li 2020 *JAMA Netw Open* (no added benefit over ferrous succinate alone in 440 IDA patients) | Single-meal effect real; chronic real-world effect smaller. Reasonable to combine but not a multiplier in routine practice |
| **IV ferric carboxymaltose** | 1000 mg infusion | Serum Fe ↑ markedly (often to 200–400 µg/dL) for 24–72 h; TSAT transiently 60–80%; settles by 4–6 wk | 24–72 h acute; weeks for plateau | **[RCT, Mechanism]** | FCM pharmacokinetics; AFFIRM-AHF | Transient very-high serum Fe is expected and not iron toxicity per se. Schedule iron-studies re-check at 4–8 wk |
| **IV iron sucrose** | 200 mg infusion | Serum Fe ↑ for 24–48 h; smaller per-dose peak than FCM | 24–48 h | **[RCT]** | Multiple nephrology RCTs | Lower max single dose |
| **Heme iron polypeptide** | 11 mg/tab | Modest acute serum Fe rise; less data than ferrous salts | Hours | **[1-Trial]** | Nissenson 2003 | Lower potency than marketed |

### Supplements that LOWER serum iron / blunt rise

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PPIs (chronic ≥2 yr)** | Standard doses | Hypochlorhydria → reduced non-heme Fe absorption → fasting serum Fe trends downward over months; IDA OR 2.49 (Lam 2017) | Months–years | **[Cohort]** | Lam 2017 *Gastroenterology* PMID 27890768 | Mechanism: gastric acid needed for Fe³⁺→Fe²⁺ conversion; PPI blunts |

## 4. Activities & Behaviors

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Acute infection / inflammation** | Any (URI, dental, gastroenteritis, vaccine reaction) | Serum Fe ↓ 20–60 µg/dL within hours; TSAT ↓ 5–20 percentage points; ferritin simultaneously ↑ — pattern mimics deficiency but isn't | Hours to days | **[Mechanism, Cohort]** | Nemeth 2004 *Science* (hepcidin-ferroportin axis) | Always defer iron-studies interpretation if CRP elevated or recent illness |
| **Acute strenuous exercise (>90 min vigorous)** | Single session | Serum Fe ↓ for 6–24 h (hepcidin spike at 3–6 h post-exercise blocks ferroportin) | 6–24 h | **[RCT]** | Peeling 2014 *PLoS ONE* PMID 24667916 | Don't draw iron studies within 24 h of hard endurance session |
| **Endurance training (chronic)** | >8–10 h/wk sustained | Fasting serum Fe trends downward over months; ferritin and TSAT also fall | 8–12 wk | **[Cohort]** | Burden 2015 meta; McClung 2013 | Three mechanisms: footstrike hemolysis, sweat losses (minor), chronic post-exercise hepcidin |
| **Whole blood donation** | 500 mL | Acute Fe drop (small immediate); over 6–18 mo, sustained reduction in ferritin and serum Fe trajectory | Per donation | **[Cohort]** | FIND'EM 2024 *Lancet*; RISE study | Iron supplementation post-donation accelerates recovery |

| **Fasting state** | — | Serum Fe slightly higher fasting than fed (no recent dietary iron load) but minimal overall | — | **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology | Standard convention is fasting morning draw |

## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

| Wearable metric | Direction of relationship | Magnitude / effect size | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Resting heart rate | Inverse — iron deficiency → ↑ RHR; magnitude small at non-anemic ID | 5–10 bpm rise at frank IDA | **[Cohort]** | Anemia + cardiovascular physiology | Confounded by training, hydration, sleep, caffeine |
| HRV | Modest reduction with frank IDA; partial recovery on repletion | ~10–20% RMSSD increase post-repletion in some studies | **[1-Trial]** | Limited primary data | Confounded by sleep, training |
| VO₂max / submaximal HR | Strong direct relationship via Hgb-mediated O₂ delivery (more relevant for ferritin/Hgb than serum Fe per se) | 5–10% VO₂max gain on iron repletion in anemic athletes | **[RCT, Meta]** | Burden 2015; DellaValle 2014 | Serum Fe is not the direct driver — Hgb/ferritin are |
| Step count / training volume | Indirect — high training volume drives iron decline (footstrike + hepcidin) | See ferritin file | **[Cohort]** | McClung 2013 | Threshold effect at >8–10 h/wk endurance |
| Sleep duration | Acute restriction → mild inflammation → may transiently lower serum Fe via hepcidin pathway | Small effect | **[Cohort]** | Inflammation literature | Not a direct lever |

Note: **Serum iron is a poor wearable-correlated marker** because of its high day-to-day variability and circadian noise. For exercise-correlated iron status, ferritin and TSAT are more useful trend markers.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention to RAISE serum iron when low:** Same as ferritin — alternate-day ferrous sulfate 60 mg or ferrous bisglycinate 25 mg, fasting morning, with vitamin C, away from coffee/tea/dairy. Serum Fe is the early-response marker (4–8 wk) before ferritin (3–5 mo).
- **Best stack of 3:** (1) Oral iron supplement on alternate days; (2) increase heme iron meals; (3) eliminate absorption inhibitors at iron-meal/dose times.
- **Realistic 12-week delta — healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (serum Fe 80–120 µg/dL):** Negligible — system is hepcidin-throttled; fasting serum Fe is already in target range.
- **Realistic 12-week delta — frank deficiency (serum Fe 30–50 µg/dL with TSAT <16%):** Fasting serum Fe ↑ to 70–100 µg/dL by week 8–12 with adherent oral iron; IV iron achieves this within 1–2 weeks.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** Same as ferritin — oral failure, intolerance, malabsorption, severe deficiency.
- **Critical practical point:** **Standardize draw conditions** (fasting morning, hold oral iron ≥24 h, no IV iron in past 4 weeks, no acute illness in past 2 weeks) before optimizing any serum-iron-driven decision. Most "abnormal" serum iron values are timing artifacts, not biology.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- Serum iron values in SA vegetarian males trend lower than Western omnivore averages, with higher within-day variability. Same vegetarian-diet caveats apply as for ferritin (vitamin C co-ingestion, separate chai, soak/ferment legumes, cast iron cookware).
- TMPRSS6 (rs855791) T allele common in SA → lower oral iron absorption → smaller serum Fe rise on standard daily oral iron → favor alternate-day or earlier IV escalation.
- HFE C282Y rare in SA; H63D moderate prevalence — does not generally drive elevated serum iron in SA populations.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- HFE C282Y/C282Y → elevated fasting serum Fe and TSAT (often TSAT >50%) — DO NOT recommend iron supplementation
- HFE H63D — generally minor effect on serum Fe
- TMPRSS6 T allele — blunted serum Fe response to oral iron

**Drug interactions (raise serum Fe):**
- Recent oral iron supplement (within 24 h) → false elevation 30–80 µg/dL
- Recent IV iron (within 4 weeks) → markedly elevated serum Fe and TSAT
- Hormonal contraceptives / HRT → raise transferrin → can lower TSAT but minor direct serum Fe effect
- Vitamin A / retinoids in high doses — mobilize iron, can transiently raise serum Fe

**Drug interactions (lower serum Fe / blunt rise):**
- PPIs (≥2 yr): IDA OR 2.49 (Lam 2017 PMID 27890768)
- H2 blockers: OR 1.58
- Antacids, sucralfate
- Calcium and zinc supplements — DMT1 competition, separate by ≥2 h
- Tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, levothyroxine, bisphosphonates — chelation, separate by ≥2–4 h
- Curcumin high-dose

**Lifestyle / physiological confounders:**
- **Acute illness / inflammation** — serum Fe drops within hours via hepcidin-ferroportin axis (Nemeth 2004 *Science*); pattern mimics IDA but ferritin is normal/high. **Always check CRP alongside serum Fe.**
- **Acute strenuous exercise** — serum Fe drops for 6–24 h via post-exercise hepcidin spike. Don't draw within 24 h of hard endurance session.
- **Diurnal variation** — peaks late morning (~11 AM); standardize draw time.
- **Day-to-day variability** — 30–50% within-person; don't act on a single outlier value, repeat fasting morning.
- **Recent meal** — false elevation if iron-rich meal within 4–6 h.
- **Alcohol (acute)** — can elevate via hepatic release; (chronic) — variable but ferritin elevation more consistent than serum Fe.
- **Pregnancy** — physiologic decline in serum Fe and TSAT (plasma volume expansion + fetal demand)
- **OCP / HRT** — elevate transferrin/TIBC, lower TSAT; serum Fe minimally directly affected

**Timing artifacts (most important):**
1. Oral iron within 24 h → false elevation
2. IV iron within 4 weeks → very high serum Fe + TSAT
3. Acute illness in past 2 weeks → false low
4. Hard endurance session within 24 h → false low
5. Afternoon/evening draw → ~30% lower than morning peak

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal serum iron target for healthy young men** — debated; conventional reference range 60–170 µg/dL (lab-dependent); functional medicine sometimes targets 80–130. **Insufficient RCT evidence** that targeting any specific serum iron value in non-deficient adults yields measurable benefit.
- **Single-snapshot reliability** — the 30–50% within-person day-to-day variability fundamentally limits serum iron's clinical utility independent of TSAT and ferritin context. Repeat draws often show wide swings.
- **Vitamin C added to iron supplement — chronic effect** — single-meal RCTs show clear absorption gain; chronic supplementation studies (Cook 2001; Li 2020) show smaller real-world ferritin/Fe gains than expected. The disconnect is likely adaptive intestinal iron regulation.
- **Hepcidin assay clinical use** — would be the ideal complement to serum Fe (resolves the inflammation vs deficiency ambiguity) but not yet widely available.
- **Intra-individual reference intervals** — emerging research (Kim 2017; AJH 2024) suggests serum Fe biological variation is large enough that population reference ranges may misclassify individuals; serial within-person tracking is more useful than single-point comparison to population.
- **Curcumin clinically meaningful effect on serum Fe in healthy adults** — case reports exist (Smith 2019) but population-level magnitude unknown.
