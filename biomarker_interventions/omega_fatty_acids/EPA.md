# EPA (Eicosapentaenoic Acid, 20:5n-3) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage:** Direct EPA intake from cold-water fatty fish (sardines, mackerel, salmon, anchovies) or EPA-rich fish oil. Fish oil 1–2 g/d EPA raises RBC EPA from typical Western ~0.5–0.8% to ~2–3% in 12–16 weeks. Vascepa (icosapent ethyl) 4 g/d is the pharmacological ceiling — RBC EPA fraction can triple. ALA → EPA conversion is <5% in adult men (Brenna 2009 *PLEFA*) — relying on flax/chia/walnuts to raise EPA in males is not viable.

**Cross-link:** Biology and reference ranges in `../../nutrients_omega_fatty_acids.md` §2. CV-outcome evidence (REDUCE-IT vs STRENGTH) covered in §11 of the same file. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: most data are from NW European/US adults. SA-specific notes in §7. Floor effects shrink magnitudes in those already at EPA ≥2%.

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Vascepa 4 g/d (Rx) for pharmacological ceiling, OR 1,500–2,000 mg EPA/d from EPA-enriched fish oil for non-Rx ceiling. Reaches RBC EPA 2.5–4% from baseline 0.7% in 12–16 weeks.
- **Best stack of 3:**
  1. Sardines or mackerel 2×/wk (~600 mg EPA/d dietary)
  2. EPA-enriched fish oil 1 g EPA/d
  3. Cease smoking, limit alcohol
  → Realistic EPA 2.5–3% in 16 weeks.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (EPA 2.5%, omnivore eating fish): +0.3 to 0.6 pp from added 1 g EPA/d. Floor effect.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for low baseline (EPA <0.7%, vegan or no fish): +1.0 to 2.0 pp from EPA-containing algal oil 500–1,000 mg EPA/d. Re-equilibration ~16 wk.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** Vascepa 4 g/d is reserved for secondary CV prevention with TG ≥150 mg/dL on statin (REDUCE-IT criteria). For lifestyle/biomarker purposes, OTC supplements suffice.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **FADS2 rs66698963 SA insertion variant:** ~70% prevalence; ~25–40% greater EPA conversion from ALA. Even with this advantage, plant-only intake yields RBC EPA <1%. Direct EPA still required for sufficiency.
- **Vegetarian SA males in UK cohorts** (Sarter 2015 *Clin Nutr*) show RBC EPA ~0.3–0.6%, well below the 1.5–2.5% intermediate range. Single largest opportunity in this subgroup.
- **AA:EPA ratio runs higher in SA carriers** at matched O3I — direct EPA supplementation has greater impact on this ratio than for European-ancestry peers.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **FADS1 rs174537, FADS2 rs174575**: minor allele "A" → less efficient ALA→EPA conversion; carriers need higher dietary EPA to match baseline.
- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** (SA "vegetarian allele"): +25–40% conversion efficiency.
- **PPARA rs4253778**: affects PPAR-α-mediated EPA-response gene expression.
- **CYP4F2 rs2108622**: affects EPA ω-hydroxylation; minor effect.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Warfarin + ≥3 g EPA/d:** modest INR ↑; monitor.
- **Aspirin + EPA:** synergistic platelet inhibition; clinically meaningful only at high EPA doses + dual-antiplatelet.
- **Statins + EPA:** no interaction; complementary on lipids/CV outcomes (REDUCE-IT confirmed).

**Lifestyle confounders / timing:**
- **RBC equilibration:** 16+ weeks for steady-state.
- **Recent fatty-fish meal:** does NOT shift RBC EPA (the value of using RBC over plasma).
- **Acute illness / surgery:** transiently lowers EPA via accelerated turnover — defer interpretation 4–6 wk.
- **High-volume endurance training:** elevated EPA oxidation; higher dietary intake needed.
- **Adipose pool size:** obese patients require longer/larger doses to saturate.

**Assay artifacts:**
- Lab matrix matters — RBC, whole-blood (Quest OmegaCheck), plasma PL, plasma total all differ. Compare within same lab.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **EPA-only vs EPA+DHA for hard CV outcomes:** REDUCE-IT (EPA-only) positive, STRENGTH (EPA+DHA) null. Whether the difference is the EPA-purity advantage, the mineral-oil placebo issue, or both is unresolved (Olshansky 2020 *Cardiovasc Drugs Ther* PMC7537802).
- **Optimal EPA-only target as biomarker:** the established target is the combined Omega-3 Index (≥8%); pure EPA-only target ranges (e.g., ≥2.5%) are derived from Japanese population data and are less validated.
- **Vegan EPA from algae:** newer Nannochloropsis-based EPA products lack large RCT bridging studies vs fish-oil EPA. Bioavailability inferred from phylogenetic similarity to existing algal DHA data.
- **EPA vs DHA on AA:EPA ratio:** EPA-only formulations lower AA:EPA more efficiently than mixed EPA+DHA per mg (mechanism: direct denominator increase). Extent of clinical translation contested.
- **Wearable correlates:** RHR/HRV responses to EPA are documented at group level; individual response variability is high.
- **Atrial fibrillation signal at high doses:** consistent across REDUCE-IT, STRENGTH, OMEMI, meta-analysis (Gencer 2021 *Circulation*); mechanism unclear (membrane fluidity? ion channel?). Not relevant at dietary or low-supplement doses (<2 g/d).
