# DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid, 22:6n-3) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage:** Direct DHA from fatty fish, fish oil, or algal oil at ~600–1,000 mg DHA/d for 12–16 weeks reliably moves RBC DHA from typical Western ~3–4% to ~5–7% (target ≥5%). Algal oil is bioavailability-equivalent to fish oil for DHA (Arterburn 2008, *AJCN*) — the only fully vegan source that works. ALA → DHA conversion in adult men is **0–4%, often undetectable** (Brenna 2009 *PLEFA*) — flax/chia/walnuts cannot raise DHA in males to a meaningful degree.

**Cross-link:** Biology, reference ranges, and clinical interpretation in `../../nutrients_omega_fatty_acids.md` §3. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## 3. Supplements

### Raising DHA

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard fish oil (TG, EPA:DHA ~1.5:1)** | 1,000 mg DHA/d (typically requires ~2.5 g total fish oil) | RBC DHA ↑ ~1.0–1.5 pp from 3.5% baseline | 12–16 wk | **[Meta]** | Harris *AJCN* 2022;116:1334–1342 | DHA fraction more conservative than EPA (slower turnover, larger pool). |

| **Krill oil (phospholipid)** | 1–2 g krill (~150–300 mg DHA) | DHA ↑ similar to TG fish oil per mg DHA at long timepoints; modest plasma advantage at 7 wk | 12–16 wk | **[RCT]** | Ulven 2011 *Lipids* 46:37–46 (PMID 21042875); Ramprasath 2013 *Lipids Health Dis* 12:178 | Lower DHA mg per capsule; cost per mg DHA high. |

| **Lovaza (omega-3 EE EPA+DHA, Rx)** | 4 g/d (~1.5 g DHA) | RBC DHA ↑ ~2–3 pp | 12–16 wk | **[RCT]** | FDA label; Davidson 2007 *Clin Ther* 29:1354–1367 | Mixed n-3, EE form. |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Algal oil OR DHA-enriched fish oil at 600–1,000 mg DHA/d for 12–16 weeks. Reaches RBC DHA 5–7% from baseline 3.5% reliably.
- **Best stack of 3:**
  1. Fatty fish 2×/wk (especially salmon or sardines, high-DHA species) → ~700 mg DHA/d dietary
  2. Fish oil OR algal oil 500 mg DHA/d
  3. Smoking/alcohol cessation
  → Realistic DHA 6–7% in 16 weeks.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (DHA ~5%, omnivore eating fish): +0.4 to 0.8 pp from added 500 mg DHA/d. Floor effect.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for low baseline (DHA <3%, vegan or no fish): +1.5 to 2.5 pp from algal oil 1,000 mg DHA/d. Re-equilibration ~16 wk.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** No DHA-specific Rx exists for primary biomarker correction. Lovaza (4 g/d EPA+DHA EE) provides ~1.5 g DHA/d for high-TG patients. For DHA alone, OTC algal oil suffices.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **FADS2 rs66698963 SA insertion** (~70% SA prevalence): +25–40% conversion advantage primarily helps EPA, not DHA. ALA → DHA conversion in SA men remains <1–2%. Direct DHA still required.
- **Vegetarian SA males** (Sarter 2015) have RBC DHA averaging 1.5–2.5%, the lowest subgroup measured in UK cohorts. Algal oil 500 mg DHA/d for 16 wk brings most to 4–5%. Single highest-yield intervention in this subgroup.
- **Sperm DHA and fertility:** SA men with vegetarian diet + low DHA have demonstrably worse sperm motility (Safarinejad 2011 *Andrologia*). Direct DHA supplementation improves sperm parameters at 8 weeks.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **FADS1 rs174537, FADS2 rs174575**: minor allele "A" → less efficient ALA→DHA conversion (already negligible in men); D-haplotype ~24% higher DHA at matched intake.
- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** (SA "vegetarian allele"): modest DHA conversion advantage but insufficient as monotherapy.
- **APOE4**: modifies brain DHA incorporation/cognitive response (Yassine *JAMA Neurol* 2017;74:339–347). APOE4 carriers may need higher DHA dose for cognitive endpoints; serum/RBC DHA rises similarly.
- **PEMT rs7946**: phosphatidylethanolamine N-methyltransferase variant — affects DHA-PC synthesis in the liver; modifies maternal DHA transfer in pregnancy.
- **ELOVL2 rs3756963**: elongase variant; modest effect on DHA synthesis from EPA precursor.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Warfarin + ≥3 g DHA/d:** mild INR ↑; monitor.
- **Antiplatelet + DHA:** additive bleeding risk theoretical; clinically modest at common doses.
- **Statins + DHA:** no interaction; modest LDL ↑ at high DHA dose (~5–7 mg/dL) is well documented (DHA is more LDL-raising than EPA at matched dose).
- **Orlistat:** blocks fat absorption — separate dosing by ≥2 h.
- **Estrogen-containing OCPs (women):** modestly raise endogenous DHA via FADS2 up-regulation.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing:**
- **RBC equilibration:** 16+ weeks for DHA (slower than EPA — DHA pool larger and turns over more slowly).
- **Recent fatty meal:** does NOT shift RBC DHA meaningfully.
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** maternal DHA depletion ~20–30%; supplementation recommended.
- **Acute illness/sepsis:** transient DHA drop via accelerated turnover — defer interpretation 4–6 wk.
- **Adipose pool size:** obese patients have larger DHA sink; require higher dose / longer time to saturate RBC.

**Assay artifacts:**
- DHA percent reported is matrix-dependent (RBC vs whole-blood vs plasma PL). Compare within same lab.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **DHA-only vs EPA+DHA for hard CV outcomes:** little head-to-head RCT data isolating DHA. Most evidence is on the combination. DHA alone reduces TG and BP more than EPA at matched dose, but raises LDL more — net CV impact unclear.
- **DHA and atrial fibrillation:** AF signal at high doses (≥3 g/d EPA+DHA) is documented; DHA-specific contribution unclear (REDUCE-IT was EPA-only and still showed AF signal).
- **Brain DHA in healthy young adults:** RBC DHA correlates with brain DHA (Connor & Neuringer transplant studies), but cognitive benefit in young healthy adults from DHA supplementation is mostly null. Benefits documented in older adults (Yurko-Mauro 2010) and infant neurodevelopment.
- **Algal vs fish DHA bioavailability:** equivalent at DHA matched dose (Arterburn 2008); long-term tissue distribution (e.g., brain) less directly studied for algal source — inferred from plasma/RBC bridging.
- **DHA and sperm parameters:** improvement at 8 wk documented (Safarinejad 2011); whether normalizing DHA in deficient men improves fertility outcomes (live birth) untested in large RCT.
- **Optimal upper bound:** RBC DHA 5–7% covers benefit; >8% no clear additional benefit and modest LDL ↑.
- **Timing of supplementation:** AM vs PM — no consistent evidence; take with largest fatty meal for absorption (especially EE form).
- **Krill DHA bioavailability:** slight phospholipid advantage at short timepoints; long-term RBC delta same as TG — cost-benefit poor.
