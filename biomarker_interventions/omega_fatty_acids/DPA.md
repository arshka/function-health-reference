# DPA n-3 (Docosapentaenoic Acid, 22:5n-3) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage:** DPA n-3 rises proportionally with EPA+DHA intake from fatty fish or fish oil — DPA is the EPA→DHA elongation intermediate that accumulates in tissues. Direct DPA supplementation (purified DPA capsules, e.g., Croda EPAX-DPA) raises RBC DPA more efficiently per mg than EPA but is niche, costly, and has no large CV-outcome RCT. Seal/whale-derived oils contain higher DPA than common fish oils. Most clinical lift comes from raising EPA+DHA intake.

**Cross-link:** Biology and reference ranges in `../../nutrients_omega_fatty_acids.md` §4. DPA is **not** part of the Harris Omega-3 Index (excluded because RBC DPA is less responsive to supplementation; Harris 2018 *PLEFA* PMID 30103927). This file is INTERVENTION-only.

**Naming caveat:** "DPA" without further specification on Function Health = DPA n-3 (22:5n-3). DPA n-6 (22:5n-6, "Osbond acid") is a different molecule and rises in DHA deficiency; it is not measured on the Function Health panel.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** Standard fatty-fish or fish-oil intake at the same dose used for EPA+DHA. DPA rises endogenously from EPA → DPA elongation; specific DPA targeting offers minimal additional benefit.
- **Best stack of 3:**
  1. Fatty fish (especially salmon, mackerel) 2×/wk
  2. Fish oil 1–2 g EPA+DHA/d
  3. Avoid heavy alcohol, smoking
  → DPA rises 0.5–1 pp in 16 weeks alongside EPA/DHA gains.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (DPA ~2.5%): +0.1 to 0.3 pp from added 1 g/d EPA+DHA. Floor effect.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for low baseline (DPA <2%, vegan or no fish): +0.5 to 1.0 pp from algal oil + indirect EPA→DPA pathway. Re-equilibration ~16 wk.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** No DPA-specific Rx exists; no clinical reason to target DPA in isolation. Treat DPA as a confirmatory marker of overall n-3 status.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **FADS2 rs66698963 SA insertion** carriers have modestly higher endogenous DPA from ALA conversion vs. non-carriers. Magnitude small in absolute terms — still need direct EPA/DHA for sufficiency.
- **No SA-specific DPA RCT data**; inferred from EPA/DHA studies in this population.
- DPA is the *best*-converted product of ALA in adult men generally — for SA vegetarian males with FADS2 advantage, DPA is the omega-3 fraction that actually shows some response to high-ALA diet (flax/walnut/chia), though absolute increments remain ~0.1–0.3 pp.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **FADS1 rs174537, FADS2 rs174575**: minor allele "A" → less efficient ALA→EPA→DPA conversion.
- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** (SA): modest DPA conversion advantage.
- **ELOVL2 rs3756963**: elongase variant directly affecting EPA→DPA elongation step.
- **ELOVL5**: also involved in n-3 elongation chain; less common variants studied.

**Drug interactions:**
- Same as EPA/DHA — no DPA-specific drug interactions documented.
- Fibrates (PPAR-α agonists) may modestly alter DPA levels via lipid-metabolism pathway redirection.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing:**
- **RBC equilibration:** ~120 days for full DPA turnover (similar to EPA/DHA).
- **Recent fatty meal:** does NOT shift RBC DPA meaningfully.
- **Acute illness:** DPA falls transiently; defer interpretation 4–6 wk.

**Assay artifacts:**
- DPA n-3 vs DPA n-6 must be distinguished — only DPA n-3 is the omega-3 elongation intermediate. Function Health reports DPA n-3.
- DPA % is matrix-dependent (RBC vs whole-blood vs plasma PL).

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **DPA-specific clinical benefit:** Mozaffarian 2013 *Ann Intern Med* showed plasma DPA inversely associated with stroke mortality, beyond EPA/DHA. Skulas-Ray 2015 *Nutrients* showed DPA inversely with TG and CRP. Whether DPA has independent CV benefit beyond being an EPA/DHA marker is contested.
- **Direct DPA supplementation:** small trials (Miller 2013 *Br J Nutr*) show direct DPA raises tissue DPA more than equivalent EPA — but no large RCT has tested CV outcomes with DPA-only supplementation.
- **Why Harris excluded DPA from the Omega-3 Index:** RBC DPA is less responsive to dietary EPA+DHA than EPA or DHA themselves (DPA is an intermediate that is rapidly elongated to DHA). The Index aims to track supplementation cleanly; including DPA would add noise (Harris 2018 *PLEFA* PMID 30103927).
- **Seal/whale oil:** highest natural DPA source but ethical/regulatory issues prevent commercial scale.
- **DPA-derived SPMs:** RvD-n3 DPA, PD1-n3 DPA, and protectins are novel SPMs from DPA; their clinical relevance is emerging but not yet driving treatment recommendations.
- **DPA and platelet function:** Phang et al. *Br J Nutr* 2009 showed DPA inhibits platelet aggregation independently of EPA — clinical relevance unclear.
- **No wearable or behavioral marker correlates well with DPA specifically** beyond what correlates with overall n-3 status.
