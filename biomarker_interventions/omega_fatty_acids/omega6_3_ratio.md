# Omega-6 : Omega-3 Ratio — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage:** The ratio is dominated arithmetically by the omega-3 denominator, especially in Western populations where omega-6 is high but reasonably stable, while omega-3 status varies dramatically. **Raising EPA+DHA is by far the most efficient intervention** — fish oil 2 g/d EPA+DHA reliably moves a typical Western ratio from ~10–20:1 down to ~3–5:1 in 16 weeks. Aggressively cutting omega-6 (LA suppression) is the inferior lever and contradicts the biomarker mortality evidence (Marklund 2019 *Circulation*). **Most lipidology mainstream — including Harris** — argues the ratio is mathematically uninformative because the same ratio can come from very different absolute levels (e.g., 1% n-6 / 0.2% n-3 vs 30% n-6 / 6% n-3 are both 5:1 but physiologically different). The validated CV-prognostic biomarker is the absolute Omega-3 Index, not the ratio. Treat the ratio as a sanity check: if >10, n-3 is too low.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: most data NW European/US adults, who have ratio 10–20:1. Mediterranean populations 4–8:1. Japan 2–4:1. "Ancestral" estimate ~1:1. Pragmatic targets cited: ≤4:1.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** **Raise the omega-3 denominator with fish or fish/algal oil 2 g/d EPA+DHA for 16 weeks.** Reliably moves Western ratio ~15:1 to ~4–5:1. By far the most efficient lever — 90% of the achievable ratio improvement comes from this single intervention.
- **Best stack of 3:**
  1. Fatty fish 2×/wk OR fish/algal oil 2 g EPA+DHA/d
  2. Replace refined seed oils with EVOO for daily cooking
  3. Limit ultra-processed foods and deep-fried fast food (composite n-6 reduction + food-quality benefits)
  → Ratio drops from ~15:1 to ~3–4:1 in 16 weeks. Most of the gain is from #1.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (ratio already ~5:1, omnivore eating fish): ratio drops to ~3–4:1 with added 1 g/d fish oil. Floor effect.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for someone with high baseline (ratio >15:1, no fish, heavy UPF): ratio can drop to ~4–6:1 with EPA/DHA 2 g/d alone in 16 wk. Adding dietary changes pushes to ~3–4:1.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** Never for the ratio per se. Vascepa or Lovaza 4 g/d would crash the ratio further but are reserved for CV outcome-driven indications, not biomarker cosmetics.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **High refined-oil intake + low fish intake + FADS efficiency** in many SA populations produces ratios commonly 20:1 or higher, especially in north Indian / Punjabi vegetarian cohorts.
- **FADS2 rs66698963 SA insertion** carriers do NOT meaningfully improve the ratio on plant-only diet because (a) ALA→EPA conversion still <5% in men even with FADS2 advantage, and (b) the SA insertion ALSO accelerates LA→AA conversion, raising the n-6 numerator.
- **Algal oil supplementation is the single highest-yield intervention in vegetarian SA males** — improves ratio from ~25:1 to ~5–7:1 in 16 weeks at 1 g DHA + 500 mg EPA/d.
- **Cooking-oil overhaul (substituting EVOO/coconut/mustard for sunflower/soybean) provides modest additional ratio improvement** but is much less efficient per effort than direct n-3 supplementation.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **FADS1 rs174537, FADS2 rs174575**: minor allele "A" → less efficient conversion both LA→AA and ALA→EPA; net effect on ratio ambiguous (depends on dietary balance).
- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** (SA "vegetarian allele"): accelerates BOTH LA→AA and ALA→EPA. Net ratio effect depends on relative dietary intake of LA vs ALA/EPA. Tends to push toward lower ratio at matched intake but baseline diet matters more.
- **PPARA rs4253778**: affects PPAR-α-mediated EPA gene expression; minor effect on ratio.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Fish oil + warfarin (≥3 g/d)**: modest INR ↑; relevant only at high doses.
- **Statins + fish oil**: complementary on lipid endpoints; no interaction on ratio.
- **Glucocorticoids**: suppress phospholipase A2 → reduce AA mobilization; no direct effect on RBC ratio.
- **Orlistat**: reduces fat absorption — reduces both n-6 and n-3 absorption proportionally; ratio largely unchanged.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing:**
- **RBC equilibration:** 16+ weeks for full ratio re-equilibration. Earlier draws underestimate the steady-state shift.
- **Recent fatty meal:** does NOT shift RBC ratio meaningfully; plasma ratio shifts within hours.
- **Acute illness:** AA mobilization from membrane PL → shift in RBC ratio; defer interpretation 4–6 wk.
- **Pregnancy/lactation (women):** ratio ↑ via DHA depletion.
- **Adipose pool size:** obese subjects have larger adipose LA reservoir; harder to lower n-6 numerator.

**Assay artifacts:**
- The ratio is highly assay-dependent. Different labs compute it as total/total vs LA/(EPA+DHA) — check the lab's footnote. RBC ratio numerically lower than plasma total ratio. Compare within same lab.
- Because the ratio is mathematically dominated by LA in the numerator and EPA+DHA in the denominator, raising EPA+DHA arithmetically lowers the ratio independent of any LA change. This is real biology (membrane competition) but the metric amplifies the appearance of intervention effect.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Is the ratio a useful biomarker?** Harris and colleagues (2018 review) argue it is mathematically uninformative because the same ratio can come from very different absolute levels — the absolute Omega-3 Index is more directly predictive of CV outcomes. Counterargument: the ratio captures the substrate competition at COX/LOX enzymes that drives eicosanoid balance, and may have prognostic value beyond either component alone (Frontiers in Nutrition 2025 NMR ratio analysis). The mainstream lipidology view (Harris) prefers absolute O3I.
- **Optimal target:** ≤4:1 commonly cited but not validated against hard outcomes vs absolute n-3 status. Japan averages 2–4:1 with low CHD; "ancestral" 1:1 is speculative.
- **Inflammatory disease applications:** RA, IBD, asthma — ratio improvements with n-3 supplementation correlate with symptom improvement in some trials (Cleland *J Rheumatol* 2003) but may reflect absolute n-3 effect, not ratio per se.
- **Cognitive / mood applications:** higher ratio associated with depression in some cohorts (Sublette 2011 *J Clin Psychiatry*); n-3 supplementation modestly improves depression in trials (Sarris 2016 meta) — but again, mediated by absolute n-3.
- **Pregnancy targets:** AA:DHA ratio important for fetal brain development; whole n-6:n-3 ratio less directly studied.
- **Whether reducing LA *while* raising n-3 adds value beyond raising n-3 alone:** sparse direct comparison RCTs. Most evidence favors "raise n-3, don't aggressively reduce LA" approach.
- **Wearable correlates:** all mediated through n-3 component (RHR, HRV, BP). Ratio per se does not add unique wearable signal.
- **Mendelian randomization on the ratio:** genetic instruments for the ratio are not well-developed; component MR (LA, AA, EPA, DHA) is the available evidence.
