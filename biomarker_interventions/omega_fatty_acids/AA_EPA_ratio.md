# AA : EPA Ratio (Arachidonic Acid : Eicosapentaenoic Acid) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage:** AA:EPA captures the direct competing-substrate balance at COX/LOX enzymes that produce eicosanoids — AA → 2-/4-series (PGE2, TXA2, LTB4 = pro-inflammatory) vs EPA → 3-/5-series (PGE3, TXA3, LTB5 = much weaker, plus E-series resolvins). It is the single biologically most-meaningful "omega-6-vs-omega-3" ratio. **Adding 2–4 g/d EPA+DHA can drop AA:EPA from 15+ down to 3–5 within 3–4 months** (Patani 2019 *Postgrad Med* review; Sears clinical literature). EPA-only formulations (Vascepa) lower the ratio more efficiently per mg than mixed EPA+DHA because they directly inflate the denominator. Reducing direct dietary AA (organ meats, very high egg-yolk intake) modestly lowers the numerator, but raising EPA is the larger lever. Target: ≤3 (Sears / OmegaQuant).

**Cross-link:** Biology, reference ranges, and CV evidence in `../../nutrients_omega_fatty_acids.md` §10. Component intervention files: `EPA.md`, `arachidonic_acid.md`. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention:** **EPA-enriched fish oil 1.5–2 g EPA/d for 16 weeks** — drops AA:EPA from ~15 to ~3–4 reliably. Vascepa 4 g/d is the pharmacological ceiling (drops to ~2). Most efficient single lever — 80–90% of achievable ratio improvement comes from EPA loading; AA reduction via dietary changes contributes the remaining 10–20%.
- **Best stack of 3:**
  1. EPA-enriched fish oil 1.5 g EPA/d (or fatty fish 2–3×/wk for ~600 mg EPA/d dietary)
  2. Reduce daily eggs to ≤4/wk; reduce poultry skin and organ meats
  3. Replace some refined LA-rich oils with EVOO
  → AA:EPA drops from ~15 to ~3–4 in 16 weeks. EPA loading drives most of the change.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (AA:EPA ~5, omnivore eating fish): ratio drops to ~3–4 with added 1 g EPA/d.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for someone with high baseline (AA:EPA >15, no fish, high egg intake): ratio drops to ~4–6 with EPA 1.5 g/d alone in 16 wk; combined with dietary AA reduction, ~3–4.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary:** Vascepa 4 g/d achieves AA:EPA ~2 — reserved for secondary CV prevention with TG ≥150 mg/dL on statin (REDUCE-IT criteria). For lifestyle/biomarker purposes, EPA-enriched OTC fish oil at 1.5 g EPA/d is sufficient.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **AA:EPA runs higher in SA carriers of FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** (~70% prevalence) at matched dietary intake. This is because the same FADS efficiency that raises AA from LA also accelerates ALA→EPA conversion — but the LA→AA flux is much larger volumetrically (LA is ~50× more abundant than ALA in typical SA diet) so net effect is higher AA:EPA.
- **Vegetarian SA males** in UK cohorts (Sarter 2015 *Clin Nutr*) commonly run AA:EPA >25, the highest measured subgroup. Driven by very low EPA (no fish, no algal oil) combined with FADS-driven AA from cooking-oil LA.
- **EPA supplementation has greater impact on AA:EPA in SA than in European-ancestry peers** because the FADS-efficient population has more "headroom" between current EPA and what EPA loading can achieve.
- **Algal oil with EPA (Schizochytrium + Nannochloropsis blends) at 500 mg EPA + 600 mg DHA/d** is the highest-yield intervention in vegetarian SA males — drops AA:EPA from ~25 to ~5–7 in 16 weeks.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **FADS1 rs174537, FADS2 rs174575**: minor allele "A" → less efficient conversion both LA→AA (lower AA) and ALA→EPA (lower EPA from plant sources). Net effect on ratio ambiguous in absence of fish; with fish, ratio is similar or modestly improved vs major-allele carriers.
- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** (SA "vegetarian allele"): higher efficiency on both pathways. Net ratio effect depends on dietary balance (LA vs ALA/EPA intake).
- **PPARA rs4253778**: affects PPAR-α-mediated EPA gene expression; modifies fish-oil downstream lipid response.
- **CYP4F2 rs2108622**: affects EPA ω-hydroxylation; minor effect on EPA pool size.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Aspirin / NSAIDs**: do NOT change AA:EPA biomarker ratio; modulate downstream eicosanoid output.
- **Glucocorticoids**: suppress phospholipase A2 → reduce AA mobilization into eicosanoid pool; RBC AA unchanged → ratio unchanged.
- **Fibrates**: minor ratio effect via lipid metabolism redirection.
- **Statins + EPA**: complementary on lipids/CV; no interaction on ratio.
- **Warfarin + ≥3 g EPA/d**: modest INR ↑; relevant only at high doses.

**Lifestyle confounders / timing:**
- **RBC equilibration:** 16+ weeks for full AA:EPA ratio re-equilibration. Earlier draws underestimate the steady-state shift.
- **Recent fatty meal:** plasma AA:EPA shifts within hours; RBC stable.
- **Acute illness / inflammation:** AA mobilization from membrane PL → eicosanoid synthesis; transient ratio shift; defer interpretation 4–6 wk.
- **Pregnancy / lactation:** ratio ↑ via DHA/EPA depletion.
- **High-volume endurance training:** accelerated EPA oxidation → modest ratio rise; need higher dietary EPA.
- **Insulin / hyperinsulinemia**: up-regulates FADS2 → higher AA; insulin-sensitizing interventions may modestly improve ratio.

**Assay artifacts:**
- AA:EPA ratio is highly assay-dependent. Whole-blood ratios differ from RBC and from plasma. Western average in plasma can sit at 15–25; in RBC closer to 8–12. Check the lab's printed reference range. OmegaQuant/Sears-style targets (≤3) refer to whole-blood / plasma-equivalent.
- Because the ratio uses both numerator and denominator measured as % of total, a relative shift in EPA mechanically changes the ratio — this is real biology (membrane competition) plus arithmetic.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal target:** Sears / OmegaQuant cite ≤3 as cardio-protective; not validated against hard outcomes vs absolute Omega-3 Index. Patani 2019 *Postgrad Med* review supports lower AA:EPA as inversely associated with CAD, ACS, stroke, CHF, PAD risk in observational data.
- **Causal vs prognostic:** the substrate competition mechanism is well-established in vitro; whether AA:EPA biomarker is independently predictive beyond absolute EPA / Omega-3 Index is contested. Harris and colleagues argue absolute EPA is more directly informative.
- **EPA-only vs EPA+DHA on ratio outcomes:** EPA-only formulations (Vascepa) lower AA:EPA more efficiently per mg than mixed EPA+DHA (Bhatt 2019 vs Nicholls 2020). Whether this translates to better hard CV outcomes (REDUCE-IT positive vs STRENGTH null) is partly attributed to ratio-shifting and partly to the mineral-oil placebo controversy (Olshansky 2020 *Cardiovasc Drugs Ther* PMC7537802).
- **Inflammatory disease applications:** RA, IBD, asthma — ratio improvements with EPA correlate with symptom improvement in some trials (Cleland *J Rheumatol* 2003; Lev-Tzion 2014 Cochrane on IBD). Magnitude of clinical benefit modest.
- **Mood / depression:** higher AA:EPA associated with depression in some cohorts (Sublette 2011 *J Clin Psychiatry*); EPA supplementation modestly improves depression in trials (Sarris 2016 *Am J Psychiatry* meta) — EPA-specific, not DHA.
- **AA reduction interventions in isolation (without raising EPA):** sparse direct comparison RCTs. Most evidence favors "raise EPA" approach.
- **Wearable correlates:** all mediated through EPA component (RHR, HRV, BP). Ratio per se does not add unique wearable signal.
- **DHA as a partial substitute for EPA on this ratio:** DHA → EPA retro-conversion is ~10–12% (Conquer 1997 *Lipids*); pure DHA supplementation modestly lowers AA:EPA but less efficiently than EPA-containing products. For ratio-specific targeting, choose products with EPA.
- **Long-term safety of very low ratios (<1):** data sparse; populations achieving this (heavy fish-oil users, Inuit traditional diet) generally show no adverse signal but optimal lower bound is not formally established.
