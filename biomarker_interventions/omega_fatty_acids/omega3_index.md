# Omega-3 Index (RBC EPA+DHA % of total fatty acids) — Intervention Research

> **Highest-leverage:** EPA+DHA (fish oil TG/rTG, algal oil) at ~2,000 mg/d for 4–6 months reliably moves a typical Western baseline (~4–5%) to ≥8% — the validated cardioprotective threshold (Harris & von Schacky, *Prev Med* 2004; Walker et al., *AJCN* 2019). Fatty fish 2–3×/week (~300 g cooked) approaches the same ceiling. ALA from flax/chia/walnuts is **not** a viable monotherapy in men (conversion <1% to EPA, ~0% to DHA). Re-equilibration follows the ~120-day RBC lifespan.

**Cross-link:** Biology, reference ranges, and clinical interpretation in `../../nutrients_omega_fatty_acids.md` §1. Effect-size summary in `../../effect_sizes_nutrients.md` "Omega-3 Index" section. This file is INTERVENTION-only.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs with consistent direction |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational; can't establish causality |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization — genetic proxies estimating causal effect |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation; no direct human RCT |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial worth knowing but not yet replicated |

Population caveat: most quantified data are NW European or US adults at baseline ~4–5%. Floor effects shrink absolute deltas in lean young adults already at 6–7%. SA-specific notes in §7.

### Raising / improving Omega-3 Index

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** (ceiling effect, magnitude, time): **2,000 mg/d EPA+DHA from fish oil (TG/rTG) or algal oil for 16 weeks.** Reliably moves baseline 4–5% to 7–8.5%. **[Meta]** — Harris 2022 *AJCN*.
- **Best stack of 3** (additive but with diminishing returns):
  1. Fatty fish 2×/wk (salmon or sardines) — ~600 mg EPA+DHA/d dietary
  2. Fish oil or algal oil supplement 1,500 mg EPA+DHA/d
  3. Reduce alcohol to ≤10 g/d, no smoking
  → Realistic O3I ceiling 9–10% in 4–6 months. Diminishing return above ~3,000 mg/d total EPA+DHA.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (already 7–8%, omnivore eating fish weekly): +0.3 to 0.8 pp from adding 1,000 mg/d supplement. Floor effect applies — small absolute gain above 8%.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for someone with low baseline (O3I <4%, vegan or no fish): +2.5 to 4 pp from algal oil 1,000 mg DHA + 500 mg EPA/d. Full re-equilibration takes 16–20 wk (RBC t½ ≈ 60 d, full turnover ≈ 120 d).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary** (when lifestyle ceiling is reached): Pharmacological dosing (4 g/d Vascepa or Lovaza) is reserved for **secondary CV prevention** with TG ≥150 mg/dL on statin (REDUCE-IT criteria). Not appropriate for primary prevention or for hitting an O3I number cosmetically — AF signal at this dose. For lifestyle-only purposes, supplement doses of 1–3 g/d fully cover the achievable benefit curve.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion ("vegetarian allele") ~70% prevalence in SA populations.** Carriers have ~25–40% greater Δ6-desaturase activity and modestly higher EPA/DHA at matched ALA intake (Kothapalli et al. *Mol Biol Evol* 2016;33:1726–1739, PMID 27188529; Mathias et al. *Mol Biol Evol* 2012). **Magnitude is insufficient** to reach O3I ≥8% on plant-only diet — vegetarian SA cohorts (UK SA vegetarians) average O3I 3–5% (Sarter 2015 *Clin Nutr*). Direct EPA/DHA (algal or fish) is still required.
- **Fish consumption in SA subpopulations:** Bengali, Keralite, Goan, Sri Lankan Tamil cuisines include regular fish; northern Indian / Punjabi vegetarian patterns supply almost zero EPA/DHA. Recommendation must consider sub-ethnicity, not "South Asian" monolithically.
- **Lean SA males have higher relative AA fraction** at baseline due to FADS2 efficiency; the AA:EPA ratio runs higher than European-ancestry peers at matched O3I. EPA-specific supplementation (Vascepa-style, or EPA-enriched fish oil) has greater impact on the ratio in SA carriers.
- **FADS2 advantage does NOT eliminate the need for direct DHA**: ALA→DHA conversion in even high-conversion-allele SA men is <1% in head-to-head controlled-feed studies (no formal SA-stratified data; inferred from FADS2 expression studies).

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **FADS1 rs174537 / FADS2 rs174575** — "A" haplotype (common in European ancestry, 40–55%) is the inefficient converter; "D" haplotype (common in African ancestry ~80%, SA ~70%) converts ALA→EPA more efficiently. (Mathias 2014 *J Lipid Res*; Kothapalli 2016 *Mol Biol Evol*).
- **FADS2 rs66698963 insertion** — SA "vegetarian allele"; +25–40% conversion vs deletion allele.
- **PPARA rs4253778** — affects PPAR-α-mediated downstream signaling; modifies fish-oil lipid response (TG drop) more than O3I directly.
- **APOE4** — modifies cognitive response to DHA; does not modify O3I per se but downstream brain DHA effects differ. APOE4 carriers may have blunted DHA brain incorporation (Yassine et al. *JAMA Neurol* 2017;74:339–347).
- **CYP4F2 rs2108622** — affects EPA/AA ω-hydroxylation; minor effect on O3I.

**Drug interactions:**
- **Warfarin + high-dose fish oil (≥3 g/d):** modest INR ↑; clinically significant in some patients. Monitor INR if starting.
- **Antiplatelet (aspirin, clopidogrel) + high-dose fish oil:** additive bleeding risk theoretical; multiple RCTs (including REDUCE-IT background aspirin) show no clinically meaningful excess bleeding at 4 g/d.
- **Statins + fish oil:** no interaction; statins do not blunt O3I rise; some literature suggests synergy on lipid endpoints.
- **Orlistat:** blocks fat absorption including fish oil — separate dosing by ≥2 h.
- **Estrogen-containing OCPs:** lower endogenous EPA conversion (women only).

**Lifestyle confounders / timing artifacts:**
- **RBC equilibration t½ ≈ 60 days; full re-equilibration ~120 days.** Re-test at 16+ weeks after starting/changing intervention. Earlier draws underestimate steady-state.
- **Recent fatty meal:** does NOT meaningfully shift O3I (RBC, not plasma) — this is the point of using RBC over plasma. Fasting status irrelevant for O3I per OmegaQuant methodology.
- **Recent acute illness / surgery / sepsis:** can transiently lower O3I via accelerated lipid turnover; defer interpretation by 4–6 weeks if acute event occurred.
- **High-intensity endurance training increases EPA/DHA oxidation** modestly; dietary intake should be proportionally higher in elite endurance athletes.
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** maternal DHA depletion as above; supplementation recommendation differs.
- **Adipose pool buffer:** in obese subjects, adipose serves as a sink — initial supplementation goes partly to fat depots before RBC saturates. Higher dose / longer time required.

**Assay artifacts:**
- **Assay matrix matters:** Function/Quest OmegaCheck is whole-blood, calibrated to RBC scale but absolute % differs slightly from pure RBC PL. Don't compare across labs without matrix conversion.
- **Sample handling:** PUFAs oxidize at room temperature — proper transport (cool, dark) required. Frozen DBS cards stable for weeks.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal upper bound:** O3I ceiling for benefit is poorly defined. Above 12% there is no observational evidence of additional CV benefit, and the AF signal at pharmacological doses suggests caution. The 8% target is the well-validated threshold; 8–10% the "comfortable" optimal zone; >12% achievable but not demonstrably better.
- **Mineral oil in REDUCE-IT:** unresolved whether 25% MACE reduction reflects pure EPA effect or partly placebo-arm harm. STRENGTH (corn oil placebo) was null. FDA estimated <3% of REDUCE-IT effect attributable to mineral oil but the question is contested (Olshansky 2020 *Cardiovasc Drugs Ther* PMC7537802; Sharma et al. 2022 *Eur Heart J* commentary).
- **EPA-only vs EPA+DHA:** for hard CV outcomes, EPA-only (Vascepa) outperformed EPA+DHA (STRENGTH) in matched-population trials. Whether DHA is truly inert or merely additive at lower magnitude is unresolved. For O3I as a *biomarker*, both raise it equivalently per mg.
- **Krill phospholipid advantage:** the per-mg bioavailability advantage of krill is real in plasma at short timepoints (Ulven 2011) but the long-term RBC O3I delta is similar to TG fish oil. Cost-benefit for krill is poor at current prices.
- **Algal EPA equivalence:** newer algal products containing both EPA and DHA have not been head-to-head tested vs fish oil for O3I in large RCTs. Bioavailability inferred from DHA-only studies + EPA-only fungal/bacterial sources. Likely equivalent but data gap exists.
- **Plant-based algal CV outcome data:** no large RCT has tested algal oil vs placebo for CV endpoints. Inferred equivalence to fish oil based on biomarker bridging.
- **Oxidation/rancidity in commercial fish oil:** TOTOX values vary widely across products; some "fresh" supplements exceed GOED limits at point of sale (Albert et al. *Sci Rep* 2015;5:7928). Rancid oil may transiently raise oxidative-stress markers and modestly blunt benefit. Consumer testing (ConsumerLab, IFOS) helps but is non-mandatory.
- **Wearable correlates:** RHR and HRV responses to O3I correction are documented at the group level; individual-level prediction remains noisy.
- **Optimal dosing schedule:** daily vs every-other-day vs weekly bolus — sparse RCT data. Weekly bolus (e.g., 14 g once a week) appears to give similar steady-state RBC incorporation (Browning 2012 dosing-pattern subset) but daily is the safest bet for adherence and absorption.
