# Free T4 (Free Thyroxine) — Intervention Research

> Free T4 is a slow, stable pro-hormone reservoir (T4 t½ ≈ 6.7 days) and is **almost completely refractory to lifestyle interventions** in a euthyroid adult. The two interventions that reliably move FT4 are **levothyroxine** (predictable dose-response) and **biotin assay artifact** (falsely elevated, the most common reason a young optimizer "looks hyperthyroid"). Foods, supplements, exercise, and sleep changes produce changes within assay noise (CV ~10–15%) in healthy people. Mid-normal FT4 (~1.1–1.5 ng/dL) is the practical target — *low-normal* is associated with depression risk and *high-normal* with atrial fibrillation in older cohorts.

## 1. Foods that LOWER / improve FT4

In a euthyroid adult with mid-normal FT4, "lowering" is rarely the goal. The realistic intervention targets are **(a) iodine excess that has driven FT4 above optimal** (Wolff-Chaikoff lift, then escape) or **(b) repleting iodine in a deficient subject whose FT4 is low**. Healthy 20yM with FT4 1.3 ng/dL: no food meaningfully lowers it.

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Iodine restriction** (avoid kelp, restrict iodized salt, low-fish diet) | 100–200 µg/day total iodine | If FT4 was elevated due to iodine excess: FT4 normalizes within weeks (escape from Jod-Basedow / Wolff-Chaikoff) | 4–12 wk | [Cohort/Mechanism] | Leung & Braverman *Nat Rev Endocrinol* 2014; Teng *NEJM* 2006 | Only relevant if iodine excess was driving the FT4 elevation (verify with urinary iodine/creatinine). Not applicable to euthyroid mid-range FT4. |
| **Soy (large quantities, taken with levothyroxine)** | High soy intake within 30 min of LT4 | FT4 ↓ in LT4-treated patients (absorption interference); no effect in untreated | Days | [1-Trial/Case series] | Bell & Ovalle *Endocr Pract* 2001; Sathyapalan *JCEM* 2011; Otun *Endocr Pract* 2023 narrative review (mixed results from single crossover RCT) | Only relevant in LT4-treated patients with timing collisions; **separate LT4 from soy by ≥4 h.** Not a dietary intervention for native FT4. |

(Note: foods that "lower FT4" in a euthyroid adult are essentially absent from the literature.)

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Methimazole** [Rx] | 10–40 mg/d (hyperthyroid) | Inhibits TPO → blocks de novo T4/T3 synthesis; FT4 falls into normal over 4–8 wk | 4–8 wk | [RCT/Guideline] | Ross et al. | **Propylthiouracil (PTU)** [Rx] | 100–600 mg/d | Inhibits TPO; also inhibits D1 (peripheral T4→T3); FT4 ↓; FT3 ↓ slightly faster than methimazole | 4–8 wk | [RCT] | Ross 2016 ATA | Hepatotoxicity 0.1–0.2%; reserved for pregnancy T1, methimazole intolerance, thyroid storm. |
| **Lithium carbonate** [Rx, off-label] | 600–1 200 mg/d | Inhibits T4 release from thyroid; FT4 ↓ within 1–2 wk; used short-term in thyrotoxicosis adjunct | 1–2 wk | [1-Trial] | Bogazzi *Thyroid* 1999; Bartalena 1996 | Therapeutic index narrow; nephrogenic DI risk; not first-line. |

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sleep deprivation (acute)** | 1 night | FT4: typically **unchanged**; small studies show <5% variation | Hours | [RCT] | Allan & Czeisler *JCEM* 1994 | Less sensitive than TSH or T3. |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** (when FT4 is genuinely abnormal): **diagnostic workup** to identify underlying cause (TSH, TPO Ab, TRAb if low TSH, biotin status, recent contrast/amiodarone). Treating the cause (LT4 for hypothyroid, methimazole for Graves') is the only thing that reliably normalizes FT4. There is no lifestyle intervention that meaningfully moves FT4 in a euthyroid adult.
- **For a "high FT4 + low TSH" pattern in a healthy young supplement user**: **stop biotin for 72 h and repeat the panel.** This resolves the great majority of such cases (Trambas 2020 *Clin Biochem Rev*; Barbesino *Thyroid* 2016).
- **Best stack of 3** (for elevated FT4 with documented Graves'): methimazole + non-cardioselective beta-blocker (propranolol, also inhibits D1) + selenium 200 µg/d (Marcocci CATS trial *NEJM* 2011 reduced ophthalmopathy progression in mild Graves'-associated orbitopathy).
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for a healthy 20yM at near-optimal baseline (FT4 1.3 ng/dL): ≤ 0.1 ng/dL change from any non-pharmacological intervention. FT4 is buffered by TBG and the slow T4 turnover.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for someone with elevated FT4 due to Graves': methimazole 20–40 mg/d → FT4 normalized in 4–8 wk in ~80%; remission rate 30–50% after 12–18 mo of antithyroid therapy (Bartalena *NEJM* 2009 review).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**: any clinically significant FT4 abnormality (overt hypothyroid → LT4; Graves'/toxic nodule → methimazole, RAI, or surgery). For truly mid-range FT4 with all other thyroid markers normal in an asymptomatic adult, the answer is "do nothing."

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- Korean NHANES IV (Kim *PLoS One* 2018) showed mean FT4 in males 1.29 ± 0.003 ng/dL vs females 1.20 ± 0.005 ng/dL — males run slightly higher on average. SA-specific reference data sparse but Marwaha 2008 *Clin Endocrinol* (Indian cohort) shows similar ranges to NHANES.
- No SA-specific evidence base for FT4 interventions distinct from general population.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **THRB mutations** (RTHβ) → high TSH + high FT4 with no symptoms; ~1 in 20 000–40 000 (Dumitrescu/Refetoff *Endocr Rev* 2013).
- **TBG variants (SERPINA7)**: TBG excess raises total T4 dramatically while FT4 stays normal — only an artifact for total T4 assays, not FT4.
- **DIO2 rs225014**: not relevant to native FT4 (only impacts T4→T3 conversion).

**Drug confounders (most important):**
- **Biotin ≥ 5 mg/d** → falsely **elevated** FT4 on competitive immunoassays (Roche, Abbott, etc.). Magnitude can be dramatic (1146% false positive at 400 ng/mL biotin on Roche Elecsys per Trambas 2020). Stop ≥72 h before draw. **Single most common cause of unexpectedly high FT4 in a healthy young supplement user.**
- **Heparin (IV/SC)** → falsely high FT4 in vitro (LPL activation → free fatty acids displace T4 from TBG); minutes onset; not relevant to outpatient draws unless on anticoagulation.
- **Furosemide (high IV dose)** → displaces T4 from TBG → falsely elevated FT4 acutely.
- **Phenytoin, carbamazepine, salicylates, valproate** → displace T4 from TBG; analog FT4 may read low even with normal free hormone (Surks & Sievert *NEJM* 1995).
- **Estrogens (OCP, HRT, pregnancy)**: TBG ↑ → total T4 ↑; FT4 typically unchanged (analog assay artifacts can mislead).
- **Amiodarone**: 37% iodine; FT4 typically ↑ (decreased peripheral conversion + iodine load); rT3 ↑; complex picture (Bartalena ETA 2018).
- **Levothyroxine over-replacement** → suppressed TSH + high-normal/high FT4; goal is mid-range FT4 with TSH 0.5–2.5.
- **PPIs, calcium, iron, soy, coffee, fiber** → impair LT4 absorption → low FT4 in treated patients (separate by ≥4 h from LT4).

**Lifestyle / timing artifacts:**
- **Recent intense exercise** → small acute ↑ from plasma volume contraction.
- **Acute illness** → unpredictable FT4 (NTIS); defer testing.
- **TBG-altering drugs** → use FT4 (not total T4); equilibrium dialysis FT4 if assay artifact suspected.

**Population artifact:**
- Mid-life pregnancy and OCP use → use trimester-specific FT4 ranges per ATA 2017.
- Aging: FT4 stable across decades in most cohorts (less age drift than TSH).

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Optimal FT4 within the reference range** for cardiovascular and cognitive outcomes: low-normal FT4 has been associated with depression risk (Demartini *Psychother Psychosom* 2014), reduced cognition (van Boxtel *J Clin Endocrinol Metab* 2004), and (in pregnancy) fetal neurodevelopmental concerns (Henrichs *J Clin Endocrinol Metab* 2010); high-normal FT4 with atrial fibrillation and bone loss in older cohorts (Cappola *JAMA* 2006). Causality unestablished.
- **Does mid-range FT4 (~1.1–1.5) confer benefit over either tail?** Plausible but no RCT evidence directly testing this.
- **Ashwagandha for low-FT4 SCH** (Sharma 2018): single trial, not adequate to recommend.
- **Whether equilibrium dialysis FT4 should replace analog FT4 in routine labs**: cost-prohibitive; ATA accepts analog FT4 for screening.
