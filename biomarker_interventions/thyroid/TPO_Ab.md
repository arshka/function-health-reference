# TPO Antibodies (Anti-Thyroid Peroxidase / Anti-Microsomal) — Intervention Research

> **Selenium 200 µg/d (selenomethionine) is the best-evidenced intervention** for TPO Ab reduction in autoimmune thyroid disease — multiple RCTs (Gärtner 2002, Negro 2007, Nordio 2017) and meta-analyses (Toulis 2010, Wichman 2016, Huwiler 2024) consistently show TPO Ab ↓ 20–55% over 3–6 months. Critically, **this antibody-titer reduction has not translated into delayed progression to overt hypothyroidism** in any controlled trial (Huwiler 2024 confirms no clinical endpoint benefit). Iodine excess (kelp, megadose iodine) is the most important *avoidable* trigger for AITD. Vitamin D repletion modestly reduces TPO Ab in deficient AITD patients (Jiang 2022 meta). Gluten elimination shows TPO Ab reduction in one small RCT (Krysiak 2018) and observational studies, but is **only justified in confirmed celiac disease** absent stronger data. Smoking is paradoxically associated with *lower* TPO positivity but is not a useful intervention. Most "thyroid support" supplements have no TPO Ab evidence; some (high-dose iodine) actively raise antibody titers and trigger Hashimoto's flares.

**Cross-link:** Biology, Hashimoto's progression risk (Whickham 20-year follow-up: 2%/yr → overt hypothyroidism if TPO+, 4–5%/yr if TPO+ and TgAb+), assay platform variation, and the dual-antibody clinical picture in `../../thyroid_panel.md` §4. Effect sizes also in `../../effect_sizes_endocrine.md` "TPO Antibodies." This file is **intervention-only**.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** for an established TPO+ individual: **Selenium 200 µg/d selenomethionine × 6 months.** Reliably lowers TPO Ab 20–55% (Toulis 2010 meta; Wichman 2016 meta; Huwiler 2024 meta). **Honest caveat: this antibody-titer reduction has not translated into delayed clinical progression to overt hypothyroidism** in any controlled trial. The clinical benefit, if any, is uncertain.
- **Best stack of 3** for a TPO+ Hashimoto individual:
  1. **Selenium 200 µg/d × 6 months** (Toulis 2010, Huwiler 2024).
  2. **Vitamin D3 to 25(OH)D 40–60 ng/mL** (Jiang 2022 meta; modest effect, replete for general health regardless).
  3. **Avoid iodine excess** (no kelp/kombu daily; no iodine-containing "thyroid support" supplements; routine iodized salt OK; check label of any multivitamin for iodine >150 µg).
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for a healthy 20yM with TPO Ab negative at baseline: no expected change. TPO seroconversion in healthy young men is rare; selenium prophylaxis has no benefit.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for a TPO+ subject (e.g., baseline 200 IU/mL) on selenium 200 µg/d: expect 20–40% reduction over 3–6 months (~120–160 IU/mL at 6 mo). Wide individual variability.
- **Realistic 12-week delta** for a TPO+ subject on selenium + vitamin D + iodine avoidance: 30–50% reduction; not consistently to undetectable.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**:
  - **Overt Hashimoto's (TSH > 10 or symptomatic SCH with TPO+)**: levothyroxine per ATA 2014 (Jonklaas et al. *Thyroid* 2014;24:1670). LT4 normalizes TSH but **does not significantly reduce TPO Ab titers** (Padberg 2001).
  - **Subclinical hypothyroidism in pregnancy with TPO+**: lower threshold for LT4 treatment (TSH > 2.5 in T1 if TPO+; per ATA 2017 Alexander *Thyroid* 2017).
  - **No pharmacotherapy is indicated for isolated TPO+ with normal TSH in non-pregnancy**: monitor annually.
  - **Symptomatic hypothyroidism + TPO+ + TSH 4.5–10**: trial of LT4 reasonable per ATA 2014.
  - The TRUST trial (Stott 2017 *NEJM* n=737, mean age 74.4, TSH 6.4) showed TSH normalization but **zero symptom benefit** in elderly mild SCH — sets the conservative bar in the elderly TPO+ population.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- South Asian regional populations have higher Hashimoto prevalence (particularly South Indian, Sri Lankan; Usha Menon *Thyroid Res Pract* 2011; Marwaha 2008 *Clin Endocrinol*); HLA-DR3/DR5 association is similar across ancestries.
- Vitamin D deficiency is **extremely common in SA populations** even in temperate climates (darker skin + limited sun + dietary patterns). Replete to 30–50 ng/mL regardless of TPO Ab status — both for autoimmune-modulation rationale (Jiang 2022 meta) and general health.
- Iron deficiency is more common in SA vegetarians; check ferritin if TSH or TPO Ab are abnormal.
- Iodine status in Indian regions is variable (iodization mandatory but supply-chain inconsistent in some states; Marwaha 2008); urinary iodine spot test useful if AITD evaluation is in progress.
- Genetic data on selenium response (SELENOP variants) is sparse in SA populations.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

**Genetic modifiers:**
- **HLA-DR3, HLA-DR5, HLA-DQ2/DQ8** → predispose to AITD (and concurrent celiac, pernicious anemia, vitiligo, type 1 diabetes — polyglandular autoimmune syndromes). Not routinely tested.
- **CTLA-4, PTPN22, FOXP3 polymorphisms** → confer modest AITD risk (Tomer *Endocr Rev* 2014). Not actionable.
- **SELENOP (SEPP1) variants** (rs7579, rs3877899) → affect selenoprotein P transport of selenium to thyroid; lower-SELENOP variants may need higher Se intake to achieve equivalent intra-thyroidal Se. Not routinely tested. Mechanistic only.
- **DIO2 rs225014** → not directly relevant to TPO Ab; relevant for downstream T4→T3 conversion in treated hypothyroid.
- **VDR polymorphisms** (BsmI, FokI) → may modulate vitamin D effect on TPO Ab; data sparse.

**Drug confounders (most important):**
- **Biotin ≥ 5 mg/d** → falsely **LOW** TPO Ab on streptavidin-biotin sandwich immunoassays (can mask autoimmunity). Stop ≥72 h before draw. **Most important confound for AITD screening in young supplement users.**
- **Heterophile antibodies, HAMA, anti-streptavidin IgM** → may cause both spurious positive and negative results; cannot be "washed out"; consider PEG precipitation or alternative assay platform if pattern is implausible.
- **Lithium** → induces hypothyroidism in 20–30% of chronic users; may unmask latent AITD.
- **Amiodarone** → 37% iodine; can trigger AITD in TPO+ patients (Wolff-Chaikoff or Jod-Basedow).
- **Iodinated contrast media (CT)** → Wolff-Chaikoff iodine load; can trigger Hashimoto's flare in TPO+.
- **Interferon-α therapy** → induces AITD in 5–20% of treated patients; TPO Ab seroconversion common (Tomer 2007 *J Hepatol*).
- **Tyrosine kinase inhibitors (sunitinib, sorafenib, axitinib)** → drug-induced hypothyroidism and rarely AITD-like patterns.
- **Immune checkpoint inhibitors (nivolumab, pembrolizumab, ipilimumab)** → high rate (~10–20%) of immune-mediated thyroid dysfunction, often with TPO Ab seroconversion (Barroso-Sousa *JAMA Oncol* 2018).
- **Alemtuzumab (MS therapy)** → Graves'-type AITD in ~30% post-treatment.

**Lifestyle / timing artifacts:**
- **Pregnancy / postpartum**: TPO Ab declines during pregnancy (immunosuppression of pregnancy) and rebounds postpartum 3–12 months. Postpartum thyroiditis affects 5–10% of TPO+ women; can present as transient hyper followed by hypo phase.
- **Acute illness / viral infection**: may transiently modulate TPO Ab; recheck after 2–4 wk if pattern unexpected.
- **Iodine status changes**: starting/stopping kelp, iodized salt, "thyroid support" supplements can shift TPO Ab over weeks.
- **Selenium supplementation timing**: TPO Ab responds at 3 mo and continues to fall through 6 mo; recheck at 6 mo.
- **Assay platform variation**: TPO Ab cutoffs differ by lab (LabCorp <34 IU/mL; Quest <9 IU/mL with one assay, <34 with another). Always interpret against the same lab/platform across time. **Never compare absolute TPO Ab values across labs.**

**Population artifact:**
- **Background TPO Ab positivity in healthy population** (Hollowell 2002 NHANES III, n=17 353): 11.3% overall; 12.3% Whites; 4.5% Blacks; ~10–11% Mexican Americans; higher in females; rises with age. **In a 20yM, baseline expected positivity is ~6–8%.**
- **Titer-progression relationship**: borderline (<60 IU/mL) — ~20% revert to negative on repeat; moderate (60–500) — consistent with active Hashimoto's; very high (>500) — strongly associated with active autoimmune destruction and faster progression.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Does TPO Ab reduction (e.g., from 200 → 100 IU/mL on selenium) actually delay progression to overt hypothyroidism?** No. Huwiler 2024 meta confirms antibody reduction without clinical endpoint benefit. This is the single most important honesty caveat for the recommendation engine.
- **Gluten elimination in non-celiac TPO+ individuals**: Krysiak 2018 *Exp Clin Endocrinol Diabetes* (n=34, women with AITD + positive tTG; GFD × 6 mo → TPO/TgAb modestly reduced) is the only RCT and the population had positive transglutaminase antibodies (i.e., not strictly "non-celiac"). Subsequent work (Krysiak 2022 *Nutrients*) and Ülker 2024 *Food Sci Nutr* (n=64) suggest modest effect; Frontiers in Endocrinology 2023 meta and a 2025 systematic review (Nutrients PMC11865394) generally conclude evidence is insufficient to recommend GFD universally for non-celiac TPO+. **Honest framing: GFD has weak supporting evidence and significant nutritional/social costs; recommend GFD only in confirmed celiac or biopsy-confirmed non-celiac gluten sensitivity.**
- **Vitamin D supplementation in already-replete TPO+ subjects**: no clear additional benefit per most trials.
- **Selenium dose-response**: 200 µg/d is the studied dose; 100 µg/d less studied; >400 µg/d unsafe (selenosis).
- **Is selenium prophylaxis warranted in TPO-negative individuals with family history of AITD?** No direct RCT; not recommended on current evidence.
- **Does combined selenium + myo-inositol (Nordio 2017) deliver clinically meaningful benefit beyond selenium alone?** Single industry-funded trial; needs independent replication.
- **Does ashwagandha or other adaptogens reduce TPO Ab?** No RCT evidence; potential harm (thyrotoxicosis case reports).
- **Effect of bariatric surgery / large weight loss on TPO Ab in obese AITD patients**: limited data; some studies show TPO Ab reduction with weight loss.
- **Does treating subclinical hypothyroidism with LT4 reduce TPO Ab?** No (Padberg 2001).
- **Effect of fasting / time-restricted eating on TPO Ab**: no direct RCT data.
- **Does selenium supplementation prevent AITD onset in healthy TPO-negative subjects?** No; not recommended for prevention.
- **Effect of microbiome modulation (prebiotics, probiotics) on TPO Ab**: speculative; weak data; one small Italian trial (Spaggiari 2017) suggested modest effect of *Lactobacillus reuteri*.

## Source format conventions

- Primary literature, ATA/ETA guidelines, and replicated meta-analyses preferred. Functional-medicine and commercial sources not cited; recommendations involving GFD, adaptogens, or megadose supplements are explicitly labeled with evidence weakness.
