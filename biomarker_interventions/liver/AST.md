# AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) — Intervention Research

> **Highest leverage**: Same hepatic interventions that move ALT also move AST (weight loss, alcohol cessation, exercise after washout, coffee), but with smaller magnitude (typically 10–25% reduction vs. 20–40% for ALT in MASLD weight-loss trials). The dominant non-liver confounder — recent strenuous exercise — must be excluded before attributing any AST change to a liver intervention. AST/ALT (DeRitis) ratio interpretation is the highest-yield analytic step: ratio ≥2 → alcohol; ratio <1 → MASLD; ratio rising toward 1+ during weight loss can reflect improving fibrosis.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../liver_panel.md` §2 (AST) and `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §AST. This file is INTERVENTION-only — what moves the marker, by how much, in how long, with what evidence. **Read `ALT.md` first** — most lifestyle and supplement effects on AST are derived from the same MASLD trials, with AST consistently moving in tandem at smaller magnitude.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: AST trial data is almost always reported alongside ALT in NAFLD/MASLD trials. AST half-life (cytosolic 16–18 h, mitochondrial ~10 d) is shorter than ALT (~36 h), so AST tends to fall faster and to a smaller relative magnitude in chronic injury. AST is also released from erythrocytes, heart, and muscle — extra-hepatic sources confound interpretation.

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** (after washout from exercise confound): 5–10% body-weight loss in MASLD → AST ↓ 10–25%.
- **Best stack of 3**:
  1. Caloric deficit yielding 5–10% weight loss (Mediterranean or low-carb).
  2. Exercise 150–250 min/wk MVPA + 2 resistance sessions/wk — but **schedule labs ≥72 h from heavy session**.
  3. Coffee 3–5 cups/day; alcohol ≤7 drinks/wk if any.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20-year-old male, baseline AST 18 U/L**: ≤3 U/L of movement; floor effects dominate.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (AST 50 U/L, MASLD)**: 5–10% weight loss + diet quality + coffee → AST ~30–40 U/L (roughly 30–40% reduction).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**: same as ALT — biopsy-proven MASH F2+, persistent elevation despite 6 mo lifestyle. Resmetirom (MAESTRO-NASH 2024), semaglutide 2.4 mg (ESSENCE 2025), vitamin E 800 IU, pioglitazone 30 mg.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- Lean MASLD in South Asians (BMI <23) shows AST elevation patterns identical to higher-BMI MASLD; AST/ALT typically <1 (Eslam *J Hepatol* 2020 PMID 32278004).
- PNPLA3 rs738409 GG carriers have higher AST and ALT for any given liver fat; AST/ALT ratio interpretation unchanged.
- **Macro-AST**: rare immunoglobulin-bound AST that doesn't clear normally; presents as persistent isolated AST elevation with no other abnormality (Litin et al. *Mayo Clin Proc* 1987 PMID 3320556). Worth ruling out in unexplained isolated AST elevation.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **CRITICAL: Recent strenuous exercise (24–72 h pre-draw)** raises AST 2–5× ULN from muscle. This is the most common false-positive AST signal. Engine MUST check "last hard workout" before flagging an AST elevation; ideally pair with CK.
- **Hemolysis** (in vitro from sample handling, or in vivo from hemolytic anemia): AST is in RBCs and rises with hemolysis. Lab report typically flags hemolysis; LDH high + low haptoglobin = in vivo.
- **AST/ALT (DeRitis) ratio** — the highest-yield analytic step:
  - <1 → MASLD, viral hepatitis without cirrhosis (typical 0.5–0.8).
  - ≥2 → alcohol (~70% sensitivity); also fibrosis/cirrhosis if both elevated.
  - >>1 with normal ALT and high CK → muscle source (rhabdomyolysis, statin myopathy, exercise).
- **Statin myopathy**: ~7–29% have muscle symptoms; CK elevation in fewer (Stroes *Eur Heart J* 2015 PMID 25694464).
- **B6 (P5P) deficiency**: reduces measured AST activity in assays without P5P supplementation (less than for ALT).
- **Macro-AST**: rare; isolated persistent AST with no other abnormality — order PEG precipitation if suspected.
- **Diurnal variation**: smaller than for ALT; modest effect.
- **Hemoconcentration / posture**: minor effect.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- AST-specific intervention RCTs are virtually never done — AST is reported as secondary endpoint in MASLD trials with ALT as primary. Most "AST effect sizes" are actually AST-tracking-ALT in trials powered for ALT.
- Mitochondrial-AST (mAST) measurement is not standard; long-half-life mAST elevation (~10 d) is a more specific marker of severe hepatocyte injury (eClinpath; ScienceDirect AST isoenzyme 2) but is research-only.
- AST/ALT ratio thresholds for MASLD-fibrosis transition: improvement in ratio toward 1+ during weight loss may indicate improving fibrosis but is not a validated standalone monitoring tool.
- AST response to time-restricted eating independent of weight loss: like ALT, mixed evidence (Cai 2024 vs. Wei 2023).
- Whether interventions on muscle quality (e.g., creatine, protein adequacy) modify the muscle-source AST baseline is essentially unstudied.
