# GGT (Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase) — Intervention Research

> **Highest leverage**: Alcohol cessation produces the fastest and largest GGT reduction (30–50% within 2–4 wk; often normalizes by 6–8 wk if no underlying disease). Coffee 3–5 cups/day is the most replicated lifestyle modifier (cohort effect ~10–20% lower GGT). 5–10% body-weight loss in MASLD drops GGT 20–40%. Omega-3 ≥2 g/d EPA+DHA: GGT ↓ MD −5.4 U/L (Yan *Clin Nutr* 2025 meta). GGT is **not just an alcohol marker** — it independently predicts all-cause and CV mortality (Ruttmann *Circulation* 2005, n=163,944, HR 1.66 per log-GGT increase) because it reflects systemic oxidative stress.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../liver_panel.md` §4 (GGT) and `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §GGT. This file is INTERVENTION-only — what moves the marker, by how much, in how long, with what evidence.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: GGT is the most sensitive and least specific liver enzyme. It responds to alcohol, oxidative stress, MASLD, drugs, smoking, and metabolic syndrome independently. Effects in lean healthy 20-year-old males with GGT <20 U/L are typically smaller than cited magnitudes (floor effects).

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention (if alcohol >7 drinks/wk)**: alcohol reduction or cessation → GGT ↓ 30–50% within 2–8 wk.
- **Highest-leverage single intervention (no significant alcohol)**: 5–10% body-weight loss in MASLD → GGT ↓ 20–40% in 8–16 wk.
- **Best stack of 3**:
  1. Alcohol audit & reduction (if applicable) — fastest and largest effect.
  2. Weight loss via Mediterranean / low-carb pattern (5–10%).
  3. Coffee 3–5 cups/day + exercise 150–250 min/wk MVPA.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20-year-old male, baseline GGT 14 U/L**: ≤3 U/L of movement; GGT is already near floor.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (GGT 45 U/L, no significant alcohol)**: coffee 4 cups/d + 5% weight loss + exercise 4×/wk → GGT ~25–32 U/L (30–45% reduction).
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (GGT 100 U/L, heavy alcohol)**: alcohol cessation → GGT ~30–50 U/L within 8 wk.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**: persistently elevated GGT (>50 U/L) in MASLD/MASH despite 6 mo lifestyle → consider resmetirom 80–100 mg/d (MAESTRO-NASH) or semaglutide 2.4 mg/wk (ESSENCE) for biopsy-proven MASH. For PBC/PSC (rare in 20yM): UDCA 13–15 mg/kg/d (PBC) or obeticholic acid; these are disease-specific, NOT useful for MASLD GGT.

**The under-appreciated point: GGT < 20 U/L is a longevity target.** Ruttmann 2005 *Circulation* (n=163,944, 17-yr follow-up): adjusted HR per log-GGT increase 1.66 (men) for CV mortality, stronger in younger participants. Lee 2007 Framingham Offspring (PMID 17110611, *ATVB*): 1-SD log-GGT elevation → 13% higher CV risk after adjusting. UK Biobank (Kunutsor *eClinicalMedicine* 2022 PMID 35146407): dose-response GGT and all-cause/CV/liver mortality. GGT is a systemic oxidative-stress biomarker, not just an alcohol marker.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **GGT may be disproportionately elevated in lean South Asians with MASLD** because lean MASLD shows higher inflammatory/metabolic activity per unit liver fat than obese MASLD (Eslam *J Hepatol* 2020 PMID 32278004). A lean SA male with GGT 30–45 U/L in absence of alcohol warrants MASLD evaluation regardless of BMI.
- PNPLA3 rs738409 GG carriers show higher GGT for given liver fat (Sookoian *Hepatology* 2011 PMID 21381068).
- **Alcohol metabolism gene variants** common in East/South Asians (ADH1B*47His, ALDH2*504Lys) cause flushing reaction; ALDH2 variants accumulate acetaldehyde and may produce greater GGT response per unit alcohol despite lower consumption thresholds.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **Alcohol** within prior 24 h–2 wk is the single biggest confounder. Engine should ask "alcohol intake last 2 weeks" before interpreting GGT.
- **Enzyme-inducing medications**: phenytoin, carbamazepine, barbiturates, statins, warfarin, OCPs raise GGT via microsomal induction, NOT hepatocellular injury. Isolated GGT ↑ on enzyme inducer is expected, not alarming. Engine must check medication list before flagging.
- **Smoking** independently raises GGT via oxidative stress.
- **GGT polymorphisms**: ~10–15% of heavy drinkers don't elevate GGT (genetic variation in GGT induction). Normal GGT does NOT exclude heavy alcohol use; consider PEth (phosphatidylethanol) or CDT for alcohol confirmation.
- **HFE C282Y/H63D**: iron overload → GGT elevation; iron supplementation contraindicated.
- **Hypothyroidism**: modestly *lowers* GGT (Beckett *JAMA Intern Med* 1985); a low GGT in someone with fatigue + cold intolerance — check TSH.
- **Obesity / metabolic syndrome / T2D**: independent GGT drivers even without measurable liver fat.
- **Diurnal variation**: small for GGT.
- **Gilbert syndrome**: doesn't affect GGT directly but co-elevation of bilirubin can confuse pattern interpretation.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **GGT mortality mechanism**: is GGT *causally* linked to CV mortality (oxidizes LDL, accelerates atherogenesis) or a *marker* of upstream oxidative stress? Mendelian-randomization studies (Lee *Hepatology* 2014 PMID 24114942) suggest GGT is mostly marker, not mediator. Implication: lowering GGT pharmacologically without addressing upstream oxidative stress may not reduce events.
- **Optimal GGT target**: cohort data point to <20 U/L for lowest CV mortality, but lifestyle interventions plateau around 15–20 in healthy young males. No RCT has tested whether driving GGT below 15 reduces hard outcomes.
- **Coffee mechanism**: hepatoprotective effect consistent across cohorts but no large RCT specifically powered for GGT endpoint. Mechanism multi-compound (chlorogenic acids, kahweol, cafestol, melanoidins).
- **Sulforaphane/broccoli sprout extract**: single supportive RCT (Kikuchi 2015); replication needed before strong recommendation.
- **Omega-3 effect on GGT vs. ALT**: meta-analyses consistently show larger GGT effect than ALT effect with EPA+DHA. Mechanism may relate to reduced lipid peroxidation reducing GSH cycling demand. Not yet well-characterized.
