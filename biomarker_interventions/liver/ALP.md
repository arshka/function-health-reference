# ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase) — Intervention Research

> **Highest leverage**: ALP is **less responsive to lifestyle interventions** than ALT/AST/GGT because its serum signal is split between liver (canalicular membrane) and bone (osteoblast) sources, plus intestinal contribution. The first analytic step is always to **fractionate liver-vs-bone source via concomitant GGT** (high ALP + high GGT = hepatic; high ALP + normal GGT = bone). Bone-source ALP in late puberty/young adulthood is **physiologic** and not a target. Liver-source ALP in MASLD responds modestly to weight loss (smaller effect than ALT/GGT). Vitamin D repletion in deficient individuals reduces bone ALP via parathyroid axis normalization. Cholestatic-disease ALP (PBC, PSC) responds to UDCA (40–60% reduction at 13–15 mg/kg/d) and obeticholic acid.

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../liver_panel.md` §3 (ALP) and `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §ALP. This file is INTERVENTION-only — what moves the marker, by how much, in how long, with what evidence.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial, unreplicated |

Population caveat: ALP is **multi-tissue** (liver ~50%, bone ~50% in adults; bone fraction higher in adolescents/young adults during/after growth). A 20-year-old male with ALP 110 U/L and normal GGT may simply have residual bone ALP from late skeletal maturation — not pathology. Intervention magnitudes below assume **liver-source ALP** is the target unless explicitly noted.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Mediterranean diet + weight loss (combined MASLD intervention) | PREDIMED-Plus protocol | Hepatic ALP fraction ↓ along with liver fat ↓ | 12–24 wk | **[RCT]** | Salas-Salvadó *Diabetes Care* 2011 PMID 21285389 | Bone fraction unaffected. |

| Adequate protein intake | ≥0.8 g/kg/d | Maintains hepatic synthetic capacity; not a direct ALP intervention | Chronic | **[Mechanism]** | — | Severe protein-calorie malnutrition can lower ALP — repleting normalizes. |

## 3. Supplements

### Lowering / improving

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vitamin D₃ (if 25(OH)D <30 ng/mL) | 2000–4000 IU/d (or higher to repletion) | Bone ALP ↓ 10–20 U/L in vitamin-D-deficient individuals | 3–6 mo | **[Meta]** | Holick *NEJM* 2007 PMID 17634462; Bouillon *J Bone Miner Res* 2018 PMID 30187521 | Mechanism: vitamin D deficiency → secondary hyperparathyroidism → ↑ bone ALP. Repletion normalizes the parathyroid axis. **Effect is on bone ALP, not liver ALP.** |
| Calcium (with vitamin D, if intake low) | 1000 mg elemental Ca/d (food + supplement) | Bone ALP modestly ↓ when combined with vitamin D | 6–12 mo | **[RCT]** | Bouillon 2018 review | Combine with D; calcium alone modest. |
| Zinc (if deficient) | 10–25 mg elemental Zn/d | ALP ↑ toward normal — zinc is an obligate cofactor; severe deficiency lowers ALP | 6–12 wk | **[Mechanism][1-Trial]** | Prasad *Adv Nutr* 2013 PMID 23493534 | Relevant only if deficient (e.g., malabsorption, anorexia). Not a target for "lowering" ALP. |
| Magnesium (if deficient) | 300–400 mg elemental Mg/d | Mg is also an ALP cofactor; deficiency lowers ALP, repletion can normalize | 6–12 wk | **[Mechanism]** | DiNicolantonio *Open Heart* 2018 PMID 29387426 | Same caveat as zinc. |
| Vitamin K₂ (MK-4 or MK-7) | 90–360 µg MK-7/d | Bone ALP modestly modified; primarily through γ-carboxylation of osteocalcin | 6–12 mo | **[1-Trial]** | Knapen *Osteoporos Int* 2013 PMID 23525894 (MK-7 osteoporosis trial) | Effect on serum ALP modest; better-validated effect on bone density. |
| UDCA (ursodeoxycholic acid) | 13–15 mg/kg/day | ALP ↓ 40–60% in **PBC** (primary biliary cholangitis); biochemical response defined as ALP <1.67× ULN | 6–12 mo | **[RCT]** | Lindor et al. *Hepatology* 2009 PMID 19614127 (PBC AASLD guideline); EASL PBC 2017 | **Disease-specific**: PBC, PSC. **Null for MASLD-related ALP** (Lindor *Gastroenterology* 2004 PMID 15057724). Do NOT use UDCA for MASLD. |
| Obeticholic acid (FXR agonist) | 5–10 mg/d | ALP ↓ 30–40% in PBC; combined with UDCA synergistic | 6–12 mo | **[RCT]** | Nevens et al. POISE *NEJM* 2016 PMID 27532829 | PBC-specific. Pruritus AE. |
| Bezafibrate (PPAR pan-agonist) | 400 mg/d | ALP ↓ ~35–50% in PBC inadequate responders to UDCA | 12–24 mo | **[RCT]** | Corpechot et al. BEZURSO *NEJM* 2018 PMID 29874528 | Off-label for PBC; nephrotoxicity risk. |
| Bisphosphonates (alendronate, risedronate; in Paget's disease or osteoporosis) | Standard osteoporosis doses | Bone ALP ↓ 30–50% in Paget's disease | 3–6 mo | **[RCT]** | Reid et al. *NEJM* 2005 PMID 15917382 (Paget's bisphosphonate) | Disease-specific (Paget's, osteoporosis with high turnover). Not relevant in healthy young male. |
| Asfotase alfa (recombinant TNSALP enzyme replacement) | Hypophosphatasia treatment | Restores ALP function; serum ALP doesn't fall but biochemical markers (PEA, PLP) normalize | Months | **[RCT]** | Whyte *NEJM* 2012 PMID 22397652 (HPP) | HPP is the treatment indication; serum ALP measurement is the diagnostic clue (low ALP). |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** (depends on source):
  - If **bone-source** (high ALP + normal GGT) and vitamin D-deficient: **vitamin D₃ 2000–4000 IU/d** to repletion → bone ALP ↓ 10–20 U/L in 3–6 mo.
  - If **bone-source** in healthy young male with recent skeletal maturation: **no intervention needed** — physiologic.
  - If **liver-source** (high ALP + high GGT) due to MASLD: 5–10% weight loss → liver ALP fraction ↓ modestly (smaller magnitude than ALT/GGT response).
  - If **cholestatic disease** (PBC/PSC): UDCA 13–15 mg/kg/d → ALP ↓ 40–60% in PBC.
- **Best stack of 3** (when liver-source ALP elevated in MASLD):
  1. Weight loss 5–10%.
  2. Mediterranean diet + 150 min/wk MVPA (Properzi 2018).
  3. Coffee 3–5 cups/day.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20-year-old male, baseline ALP 90 U/L (normal range, possibly residual bone)**: minimal lifestyle-driven change expected. Verify GGT to confirm source; reassure if bone.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (ALP 140 U/L, MASLD-confirmed via co-elevated GGT)**: 5–10% weight loss → ALP ~115–125 U/L (~10% reduction).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**:
  - PBC/PSC (rare in young male): UDCA first-line; obeticholic acid or bezafibrate as second-line.
  - Paget's disease (rare <40): bisphosphonates.
  - Hypophosphatasia (low ALP): asfotase alfa enzyme replacement in severe cases.
  - For MASLD-related ALP elevation: same drugs as for ALT (resmetirom, semaglutide for biopsy-proven MASH); ALP not the primary endpoint.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Vitamin D deficiency** is highly prevalent in South Asians regardless of latitude (skin pigmentation reduces UVB-driven D synthesis; cultural/dietary patterns); 70–90% of SA adults in UK/US cohorts have 25(OH)D <30 ng/mL (Mithal et al. *Osteoporos Int* 2009 PMID 19557330). A modestly elevated bone ALP in a South Asian male should prompt vitamin D level — repletion may normalize it.
- **Hypophosphatasia (HPP)**: rare in any population; consider in persistent low ALP with stress fractures, premature dental loss, chondrocalcinosis (Whyte *GeneReviews* 2016).

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **CRITICAL: Liver vs. bone source must be disambiguated first** — co-measure GGT.
  - ALP ↑ + GGT ↑ → hepatic source.
  - ALP ↑ + GGT normal → bone source (growth, Paget's, vitamin D deficiency, healing fracture, primary HPT). Do not assign liver interventions.
- **Age / late skeletal maturation**: 20-year-old males often still have bone ALP at upper-normal range from late growth-plate closure (NHANES skeletal maturity data). Not pathology.
- **Postprandial fatty meal**: total ALP can rise 10–30 U/L within 2–4 h in O/B-blood-group secretors. Fast 8 h before draw.
- **Recent fracture / orthopedic surgery**: bone ALP ↑ during healing, may persist 3–6 mo.
- **Vitamin D deficiency**: secondary hyperparathyroidism → bone ALP ↑. Order 25(OH)D + PTH.
- **Pregnancy** (NA for males): placental ALP raises total dramatically in 2nd/3rd trimester.
- **Drugs**: phenytoin, carbamazepine, barbiturates, OCPs, antibiotics, methotrexate raise ALP. Anabolic steroids cause cholestatic ALP elevation.
- **Zn / Mg deficiency** lowers ALP (cofactor depletion); rarely a target for "raising."
- **Hypophosphatasia (ALPL gene loss-of-function)**: persistent low ALP; consider PEA, PLP measurements.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Liver-fraction-specific ALP intervention data** is sparse — most "ALP ↓" in trials is reported as total ALP without fractionation. Effect sizes for liver vs. bone fraction separately are usually unavailable.
- **Coffee and ALP**: cohort signal exists but smaller and less consistent than for GGT/ALT; mechanism unclear.
- **Vitamin D mechanism on ALP**: well-established for bone fraction; ambiguous for liver fraction.
- **Sulforaphane / Nrf2 activation effect on ALP**: mechanism plausible, no targeted RCT.
- **Bezafibrate for MASLD-related ALP**: PROMINENT-style fibrate trials show modest hepatic enzyme effect; not validated for MASLD-specific ALP endpoint.
- **Bone ALP isoform measurement** (BALP, P1NP) is more useful than total ALP for monitoring osteoporosis/Paget's intervention; total ALP is a blunt instrument.

## Source format conventions

- Citations include Author + Journal + Year + PMID.
- ALP fractionation (BALP vs. liver ALP) requires immunoassay or electrophoresis — usually not on standard panels. Co-measure GGT as a low-cost surrogate for source attribution.
