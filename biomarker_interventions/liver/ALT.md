# ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) — Intervention Research

> **Highest leverage**: 5–10% body-weight loss drops ALT 20–40% in 8–16 wk in MASLD (Promrat 2010 *Hepatology*; Vilar-Gomez 2015 *Gastroenterology*); 15-kg loss (DiRECT VLC) drops ALT 38–51% (Zhyzhneuskaya 2025). Coffee 3–5 cups/day is the most replicated dietary modifier in cohorts (AASLD-acknowledged hepatoprotection). Vitamin E 800 IU/d normalizes ALT in ~50% of non-diabetic NASH (PIVENS *NEJM* 2010). For pharma-equivalent: resmetirom 100 mg (MAESTRO-NASH 2024) and semaglutide 2.4 mg (ESSENCE 2025).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../liver_panel.md` §1 (ALT) and `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §ALT. This file is INTERVENTION-only — what moves the marker, by how much, in how long, with what evidence.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs with consistent direction |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT (≥100 participants or pivotal design) |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational; can't establish causality |
| **[MR]** | Mendelian randomization — genetic proxies estimating causal effect |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation; no direct human RCT for *this specific effect* |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial worth knowing but not yet replicated |

Population caveat throughout: most cited evidence is from adults aged 30+ with elevated ALT (NAFLD/MASLD baseline 50–100 U/L). Floor effects shrink magnitudes ~30–60% in lean healthy young adults already at ALT ≤25 U/L. South-Asian-specific notes flagged in §7.

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Coffee (caffeinated or decaf) | 3–5 cups/day habitual | ALT ↓ ~10% in highest vs. lowest consumers; ALT-elevation risk ↓ 44% (>2 cups/day vs. none) | Chronic (≥6 mo of habit) | **[Meta][Cohort]** | Saab et al. *Liver Int* 2014 PMID 24267065; Kennedy et al. *Aliment Pharmacol Ther* 2017 PMID 27880985 (meta of 16 cohorts, n>3,000 NAFLD); Ruhl & Everhart *Gastroenterology* 2005 NHANES PMID 16344056 | Effect persists with decaf → polyphenols/cafestol/melanoidins, not just caffeine. ## 2. Foods that RAISE ALT (or worsen direction)

| Food | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sugar-sweetened beverages (SSB), high-fructose | ≥1 SSB/day | NAFLD risk ↑ ~60%; ALT ↑ in cohort and controlled-feeding | 4–10 wk | **[RCT][Cohort]** | Stanhope et al. *J Clin Invest* 2009 PMID 19381015 (25% kcal fructose for 10 wk); Maersk et al. *Am J Clin Nutr* 2012 PMID 22205311 | Fructose-specific effect (not equivalent calorie-matched glucose) confirmed in metabolic-ward conditions. |
| Saturated-fat overfeeding (palmitic-acid-rich) | +750 kcal/day SFA × 3 wk | Liver fat +5% absolute by MRS; ALT ↑ modestly | 3 wk | **[RCT]** | Rosqvist et al. *Diabetes* 2014 PMID 24917578 | Palmitic acid drives DNL and hepatic insulin resistance > linoleic acid (PUFA) at identical kcal surplus. |
| Refined-carbohydrate / high-glycemic-load diet | High-GI breads, processed cereals, white rice as >50% kcal | ALT ↑ in NAFLD cohorts; insulin-mediated DNL | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Zelber-Sagi et al. *Hepatology* 2007 PMID 17567829 (NAFLD soft-drink and meat cohort) | Mechanism: hyperinsulinemia → SREBP-1c → DNL → liver fat. |
| Red and processed meat | >150 g/day red meat or processed meat | NAFLD risk ↑ ~50%; ALT modestly ↑ | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Zelber-Sagi et al. *J Hepatol* 2018 PMID 29571924; Etemadi et al. *BMJ* 2017 PMID 28487287 (heme iron, NAFLD/liver mortality) | Heme iron, AGEs from cooking, and saturated fat all contribute. |
| Fructose (excess; fruit juice in large volumes; HFCS) | ≥50 g/day | ALT ↑; liver fat ↑; effect dose-dependent | 4–12 wk | **[RCT]** | Stanhope 2009; Hieronimus et al. *Am J Clin Nutr* 2020 (PMID 32945831 / PMC) | Whole fruit ≠ fruit juice (matrix + fiber blunt effect). |
| Ultra-processed foods (NOVA-4) | >50% kcal from UPF | NAFLD prevalence ↑; ALT higher in cross-sectional | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Zhang et al. *Hepatology* 2022 PMID 34796536 (UK Biobank UPF and liver) | Independent of BMI in some analyses; effect partly via additives, emulsifiers, refined carbs. |
| Alcohol (any-dose, dose-response) | ≥14 g/day (1 standard drink) | ALT modestly ↑ in cohorts; sharper rise >3 drinks/day | Weeks | **[Cohort]** | Loomba et al. *Am J Epidemiol* 2010 PMID 20530463; Alatalo NHANES analysis | Below ~14 drinks/wk effect on ALT modest; GGT more sensitive (see GGT.md). AST/ALT >2 → alcohol-suggestive. |
| Industrial trans fat (partially-hydrogenated oils) | Any regular intake | NAFLD risk ↑; ALT ↑ in cohort | Chronic | **[Cohort]** | Mozaffarian et al. *NEJM* 2006 PMID 16611951 (TFA review) | Largely banned in US food supply since 2018, but persists in some imports. |
| Coconut oil / high lauric/myristic intake | Habitual high-SFA tropical-oil intake | Liver fat ↑ in overfeeding studies | 8–16 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Eyres et al. *Nutr Rev* 2016 PMID 26946252 (coconut oil cardiometabolic) | Lauric/myristic SFAs raise hepatic DNL similar to palmitic. |
| Ketogenic diet (acute, high-SFA composition) | High SFA + animal-fat-heavy keto | Transient ALT ↑ in some during weight loss; usually ↓ as fat loss proceeds | 2–8 wk transient | **[Cohort]** | Watanabe et al. *Front Nutr* 2020; Goday et al. very-low-cal ketogenic NAFLD trials | Net effect of ketogenic weight loss is ALT ↓ (matches DiRECT pattern). Transient rise from rapid hepatic-fat mobilization is the confound to know. |
| Salt-cured meats / aflatoxin-contaminated foods | Endemic exposure (corn, peanuts in mycotoxin-prone storage) | ALT ↑ acutely; HCC risk ↑ | Years | **[Cohort]** | Liu & Wu *Lancet Glob Health* 2016 review | Geographically restricted; not a primary US/EU concern. |

## 3. Supplements

### Lowering / improving

| Supplement | Dose | Direction & magnitude | Time to peak | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vitamin E (rrr-α-tocopherol) | 800 IU/day (~536 mg) | ALT ↓ significantly vs. placebo (P<0.001); NASH resolution 43% vs. 19% placebo | 24–96 wk | **[RCT]** | Sanyal et al. PIVENS *NEJM* 2010 PMID 20427778; Lavine et al. TONIC pediatric *JAMA* 2011 PMID 21540794 | **Non-diabetic NASH only**; fibrosis not improved. Long-term safety: meta-analysis Miller 2005 *Ann Intern Med* PMID 15537682 suggested all-cause mortality ↑ at >400 IU/d (debated; confounded). SELECT trial: prostate cancer ↑ at 400 IU/d in men (Klein 2011 *JAMA* PMID 21990298). Discuss before chronic use. |
| Omega-3 (EPA+DHA) | ≥2 g/day combined | ALT ↓ MD ~−2 to −5 U/L; liver fat ↓ MD ~−4% absolute by MRS | 12–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Parker et al. *J Hepatol* 2012 PMID 22023985 (meta of 9 trials); Yan et al. *Clin Nutr* 2025 (n>30 trials, MASLD); Sanyal et al. WELCOME-style trials | Effect more reliable on liver fat and GGT than ALT; <2 g/day rarely effective. EPA-rich fractions and Epanova trials mostly null for histologic NASH. |
| Pioglitazone | 30–45 mg/day | ALT ↓ 20–30% from baseline; histologic improvement | 24–96 wk | **[RCT]** | Sanyal PIVENS *NEJM* 2010; Belfort et al. *NEJM* 2006 PMID 17135584 | Non-diabetic NASH and T2D-NASH both responsive. | Berberine | 500 mg TID | ALT ↓ ~10–20% in NAFLD trials; HOMA-IR ↓ | 12–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Yan et al. *Plos One* 2015 PMID 25923722; Wei et al. *Phytomedicine* 2019 (NAFLD meta) | Mostly Chinese-population trials; transportability uncertain. GI side effects common. |
| Curcumin (bioavailable formulations: phytosome, Meriva, BCM-95) | 500–1500 mg/day curcuminoids | ALT ↓ modest in NAFLD trials; MD −7 to −10 U/L | 8–12 wk | **[Meta]** | Goodarzi et al. *Adv Pharm Bull* 2019 PMC6776959 (NAFLD curcumin meta); Mansour-Ghanaei *Integr Med Res* 2019 | **Caution**: case reports of curcumin/turmeric DILI especially with piperine co-formulation (Lombardi *Hepatology* 2022 PMID 35718729; LiverTox database) — paradoxical elevation in susceptible individuals. |

| Vitamin D₃ (if 25(OH)D <30 ng/mL) | 2000–4000 IU/d | ALT ↓ modest; MASLD-related mostly null in repleted | 12–24 wk | **[RCT]** | Barchetta et al. *BMC Med* 2016 PMID 27353492 (D-FINE; null primary endpoint); subgroup-positive only in deficient | Overall null at population level; possible benefit in deficient subgroup. |
| Choline (intake at AI: 550 mg/d male) | RDA-level intake (food + supplement) | ALT may ↓ in choline-deficient NAFLD; insufficient data in adequately fed | Months | **[Mechanism][Cohort]** | Guerrerio et al. *Am J Clin Nutr* 2012 PMID 22302926; PEMT genotype interactions Da Costa 2006 | Mechanism: choline required for VLDL secretion; deficiency causes hepatic TG accumulation. | Phosphatidylcholine (essential phospholipids) | 3–6 g/day | ALT ↓ in some Eastern European NAFLD trials | 12–24 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Gundermann et al. *Pharmacol Rep* 2011 PMID 21346389 (PPC review) | Heterogeneous trials; mostly non-blinded. |
| TUDCA (tauroursodeoxycholic acid) | 750 mg/day | ALT ↓ in small NAFLD trials | 24 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Pan et al. *Eur J Clin Pharmacol* 2014 PMID 24729124 | Mechanism: ER-stress reduction. Magnitude small; UDCA itself is **null for MASLD** (Lindor *Gastroenterology* 2004 PMID 15057724). |
| Probiotics / synbiotics (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Saccharomyces blends) | Multi-strain, ≥10⁹ CFU/day | ALT ↓ MD −7 U/L in MASLD meta | 8–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Sharpton et al. *Aliment Pharmacol Ther* 2018 PMID 29460417 (MASLD probiotic meta); Liu et al. 2024 update | Strain heterogeneity limits prescribing precision. | Resveratrol | 150–500 mg/day | ALT: **mostly null** in MASLD RCTs | 12 wk | **[RCT]** | Heebøll et al. *Am J Clin Nutr* 2016 PMID 27544444 | Largely oversold; null in best NAFLD trials. |
| L-carnitine | 1–2 g/day | ALT ↓ ~5–10 U/L in NASH trials | 24 wk | **[1-Trial]** | Malaguarnera et al. *Am J Clin Nutr* 2010 PMID 20410100 | Single-center; needs replication. |

## 4. Activities & Behaviors

| Activity | Dose / pattern | Direction & magnitude | Time to effect | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Body-weight loss (caloric restriction) | 5–10% baseline body weight | ALT ↓ 20–40% from baseline; >10% → ALT ↓ ~50% with NASH resolution in ~90% | 8–24 wk | **[Meta][RCT]** | Promrat et al. *Hepatology* 2010 PMID 19937697; Vilar-Gomez et al. *Gastroenterology* 2015 PMID 25917783 (paired-biopsy n=293) | Single highest-leverage intervention. | Very-low-calorie diet (DiRECT-style) | 825 kcal/d × 12 wk → maintenance | ALT ↓ 38–51% from baseline; ~15 kg loss; liver fat ↓ ~30% | 12–24 wk | **[RCT]** | Lean et al. | Aerobic exercise (moderate-vigorous) | 150–300 min/wk, ≥12 wk | ALT ↓ 5–20 U/L in MASLD; effect proportional to liver fat ↓ | 12–24 wk | **[Meta]** | Keating et al. *J Hepatol* 2012 PMID 22561023; Orci et al. *Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol* 2016 PMID 27184241 | Independent of weight loss in some trials (modest residual effect ~5 U/L). |

## 5. Wearable-Trackable Metric Relationships

| Wearable metric | Direction of relationship | Magnitude / effect size | Evidence | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| VO₂max (cardiorespiratory fitness) | Inverse with ALT in cross-sectional; high VO₂max → 30–50% lower NAFLD prevalence | Per 1-MET ↑, NAFLD risk ↓ ~10–15% | **[Cohort]** | Kantartzis et al. *Gut* 2009 PMID 19584031; Pälve et al. *Med Sci Sports Exerc* 2017 PMID 27776006 | Mediated partly by lower visceral fat. |

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention**: 5–10% body-weight loss → ALT ↓ 20–40% in 8–24 wk (Promrat 2010; Vilar-Gomez 2015). >10% body-weight loss approaches NASH resolution in ~90%.
- **Best stack of 3 (additive with diminishing returns)**:
  1. Caloric deficit yielding 5–10% weight loss (Mediterranean or low-carb pattern; both work).
  2. Exercise: 150–250 min/wk MVPA + 2 resistance sessions/wk (independent liver-fat reduction even without weight loss; Hallsworth 2011).
  3. Coffee 3–5 cups/day (low-cost, additive, AASLD-acknowledged).
- **Realistic 12-week delta for a healthy 20-year-old male at near-optimal baseline (ALT 18 U/L)**: ≤5 U/L of movement; floor effects dominate. Avoid muscle-source confound; do not expect a dramatic shift.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for elevated baseline (ALT 50 U/L, presumed MASLD)**: 5–10% weight loss + diet quality + coffee + exercise → ALT ~25–35 U/L (a 30–50% reduction is realistic).
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**: biopsy-proven MASH with fibrosis F2+, ALT >2× ULN persisting after 6 mo of intensive lifestyle intervention, or progressive disease despite weight loss. First-line: resmetirom 80–100 mg/d (MAESTRO-NASH; FDA 2024) for F2-F3 MASH; semaglutide 2.4 mg/wk (ESSENCE 2025; FDA Aug 2025) for non-cirrhotic MASH. Vitamin E 800 IU and pioglitazone 30 mg are second-line in non-diabetic NASH per AASLD 2023.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- **Lean MASLD** in South Asians presents at BMI <23 kg/m² (Eslam et al. *J Hepatol* 2020 PMID 32278004 consensus). A lean SA male with ALT 25–35 U/L should be evaluated for MASLD by the same criteria as a heavier patient — normal weight does not exclude metabolic liver disease.
- **PNPLA3 rs738409 GG** carriers have ~73% more liver fat than CC, more aggressive fibrosis, and may benefit disproportionately per kg of weight lost (Sookoian & Pirola *Hepatology* 2011 PMID 21381068; Romeo *Nat Genet* 2008 PMID 18820647). Allele frequency in South Asians is intermediate between European (~22%) and East Asian (~50%).
- **TM6SF2 rs58542926** carriers (T allele) have higher liver fat but paradoxically lower LDL/CV risk (Kozlitina *Nat Genet* 2014 PMID 24531328).
- Lean-MASLD intervention response data sparse; weight-loss magnitudes proportionally similar but absolute kg targets smaller (3–7 kg may achieve ALT response).
- Prevalence of HFE C282Y homozygous hemochromatosis is much lower in South Asians (~1 in 5,000 vs. 1 in 200 NW European); ferritin elevation in SA more often reflects MASLD or inflammation than iron overload.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **Vigorous exercise within 24–72 h pre-draw** raises ALT modestly (and AST significantly) from skeletal-muscle source. Recommendation engine must check "last hard workout" before flagging an ALT spike. Pair with CK if available.
- **PNPLA3 rs738409 GG**: bigger MASLD risk; weight-loss response per kg may be larger; flag for amplified intervention priority.
- **HFE C282Y/H63D**: iron overload causes ALT elevation; iron supplementation contraindicated; phlebotomy normalizes ALT.
- **UGT1A1*28 (Gilbert)**: not directly affecting ALT, but co-elevated bilirubin can confuse pattern interpretation.
- **B6 (pyridoxal-5-phosphate) deficiency**: artifactually lowers measured ALT activity (assays without P5P supplementation). Sarcopenia and elderly low ALT are partly B6-driven.
- **Statins**: 1–3% have transient ALT ↑ <3× ULN; clinically irrelevant in absence of symptoms; AASLD/ACC do not recommend routine LFT monitoring after baseline.
- **Acetaminophen "therapeutic" use**: 4 g/d × 14 d → mild ALT ↑ in healthy volunteers (Watkins 2006); recommend tracking OTC analgesic use.
- **Ashwagandha, green-tea-extract supplements, anabolic steroids/SARMs**: increasingly common in young male demographic; flag at intake.
- **Diurnal variation**: ALT can vary ~45% within a day; lowest early morning, highest afternoon (Cordoba *Hepatology* 1998 PMID 9820968). Standardize draw time.
- **BMI**: ALT rises with BMI even within "normal" (Prati 2002 PMID 12093239).
- **Recent illness / infection**: transient ALT bump from any acute hepatitis-like event (EBV, CMV, COVID).

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **Vitamin E long-term safety**: 800 IU/d effective for non-diabetic NASH but flagged for prostate cancer signal (SELECT 2011) and possible all-cause mortality (Miller 2005, debated). Recommendation should be time-limited and shared decision.
- **Berberine**: most efficacy data from Chinese-population RCTs; transportability to NW European or South Asian populations uncertain.
- **Curcumin paradox**: meta shows ALT ↓ but DILI case-reports increasing (especially with piperine bioavailability enhancers). Net benefit-risk unclear at supplement doses; safe at culinary doses.
- **TRE (time-restricted eating) effect independent of weight loss**: Cai 2024 TREATY-FLD shows benefit; Wei 2023 mostly null beyond weight effect. Open question whether TRE adds beyond caloric deficit alone.
- **Resmetirom durability and broader population effects**: MAESTRO-NASH was 52-wk; longer-term outcomes pending. Cost / access is a major real-world limit.
- **Semaglutide for ALT in non-MASH populations**: ESSENCE used biopsy-proven MASH; effects in non-MASH MASLD or simple ALT elevation extrapolation only.
- **Coffee mechanism**: hepatoprotective effect consistent across cohorts but no large RCT specifically powered for ALT endpoint. Mechanism multi-compound (chlorogenic acids, kahweol, cafestol, melanoidins) — implausible to replicate in a single-isolate trial.
- **Choline / phosphatidylcholine**: mechanism solid; trial data heterogeneous; PEMT genotype interaction (rs7946) under-utilized in personalization.
