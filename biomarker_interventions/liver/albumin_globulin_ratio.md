# Albumin / Globulin Ratio (A/G) — Intervention Research

> **Highest leverage**: A/G ratio is **purely derived** (Albumin ÷ Globulin). It moves only because albumin or globulin moves. There are NO interventions that act on the ratio per se. The ratio's value is as a **screening signal** — abnormal A/G should trigger fractionation of components and workup of the underlying cause (chronic inflammation, monoclonal gammopathy, immune deficiency, liver disease, kidney loss). The most actionable scenarios: low A/G (<1.0) → workup chronic liver disease, autoimmune, chronic infection, or (much less likely at 20) hematologic malignancy via SPEP. High A/G (>2.5) → workup immunodeficiency (e.g., CVID).

**Cross-link:** Reference biology / clinical interpretation lives in `../../liver_panel.md` §8 (A/G ratio) and `../../effect_sizes_metabolic_liver.md` §Globulin/A:G/Total Protein. **Read `albumin.md` and `globulin.md` first** — A/G is a derived metric that moves only when its components move. This file is INTERVENTION-only and largely *redirects* to the component files.

## Evidence-Grading Legend

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[Meta]** | Replicated meta-analysis of ≥5 RCTs |
| **[RCT]** | Single high-quality RCT |
| **[Cohort]** | Prospective observational |
| **[Mechanism]** | Pharmacology / physiology extrapolation |
| **[1-Trial]** | Single small trial |

Population caveat: A/G ratio has essentially zero RCT data with itself as primary endpoint. All "intervention effects" on A/G are derived from interventions on albumin (→ raises A/G) or globulin (→ lowers A/G).

## 6. Synthesis & Highest-Leverage Stack

- **Highest-leverage single intervention** depends on the abnormality:
  - **Healthy 20yM with A/G 1.5–2.0**: NO targeted intervention — this is optimal.
  - **Low A/G (<1.0)**: workup the cause —
    - SPEP if globulin >4.0 g/dL or persistent → distinguish polyclonal (chronic infection, autoimmune, chronic liver disease) from monoclonal (gammopathy/myeloma — extremely rare at 20).
    - Chronic infection workup: HIV, HBV/HCV, TB, parasitic.
    - Autoimmune workup: ANA, RF, anti-CCP, ASMA, anti-LKM, IgG/IgA/IgM.
    - Liver disease: full LFT panel + imaging.
    - Nephrotic syndrome: urine protein-creatinine ratio.
  - **High A/G (>2.5)**: workup immunodeficiency —
    - Quantitative immunoglobulins (IgG, IgA, IgM).
    - Specific antibody response post-vaccination.
    - Refer immunology if CVID or other primary immunodeficiency suspected.
- **Best stack of 3** (general anti-inflammatory practices that may modestly raise low A/G):
  1. Treat the underlying cause (infection, autoimmune, alcohol use, MASLD, etc.).
  2. Mediterranean diet + 150 min/wk MVPA + adequate sleep.
  3. Standardize lab conditions.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for healthy 20-year-old male, baseline A/G 1.7**: essentially no movement. Within-person CV ~5%.
- **Realistic 12-week delta for low baseline (A/G 0.9 with confirmed chronic inflammation/autoimmune)**: addressing source → A/G ↑ 0.2–0.4 over 8–24 wk depending on disease control.
- **Where pharmacotherapy becomes necessary**:
  - Autoimmune: DMARDs / biologics.
  - Chronic infection: antiviral / antibiotic per pathogen.
  - Multiple myeloma / Waldenström: hematology / oncology.
  - CVID / primary immunodeficiency: IVIG.

**A/G as a screening signal, not a target.** When abnormal, dig into components — A/G is shorthand for "is the albumin-vs-globulin balance disturbed?" The interventions act on albumin or globulin individually.

## 7. South Asian / lean-male-specific notes

- South Asian males have similar A/G reference ranges to NW European males.
- **Higher background prevalence of chronic infection** (TB, HBV, parasitic) in some SA cohorts → polyclonal hypergammaglobulinemia → low A/G; cohort-specific interpretation.
- **Lean MASLD** in SA: A/G usually preserved until late-stage cirrhosis; low A/G in lean SA male warrants the same workup as in any population.
- **CVID** prevalence in SA is similar to other populations.
- **Vegetarian SA diets**: do not directly affect A/G in adequately-nourished adults.

## 8. Interactions & Confounders for Recommendation Engine

- **CRITICAL: A/G is purely derived — interventions act on components**. Engine should redirect any "improve A/G" recommendation to the underlying albumin and globulin drivers.
- **A/G is more robust to pre-analytical variation than components alone** because hydration affects both numerator and denominator equally. However, if albumin and globulin are differentially affected (e.g., hemolysis: globulin slightly ↑ from intracellular protein release), the ratio can shift.
- **Acute vs. chronic inflammation patterns**:
  - Acute: α-globulins ↑ (positive APR) and albumin ↓ (negative APR) → A/G ↓ rapidly.
  - Chronic: γ-globulin ↑ (chronic immune activation) and albumin ↓ (sustained inflammation) → A/G ↓ persistently.
- **Liver disease**: cirrhosis paradoxically raises γ-globulin and lowers albumin → very low A/G (≤0.8).
- **Plasma vs. serum**: standardize sample type (fibrinogen contributes to plasma globulin).
- **Drug interactions**: glucocorticoids and rituximab raise A/G (lower γ-globulin). Phenytoin can lower A/G via drug-induced polyclonal hypergammaglobulinemia.
- **Recent vaccination**: transient γ-globulin ↑ → A/G ↓ for 2–4 wk.
- **Recent infection**: A/G ↓ during and immediately after; wait 4 wk for stable measurement.
- **Genetic variants**: α₁-AT deficiency (PI*ZZ) lowers α₁-band → minor A/G shift; relevant for liver/lung workup.
- **Macroglobulinemia (rare at 20)**: IgM monoclonal ↑ → very high globulin, very low A/G, hyperviscosity symptoms.
- **Pregnancy** (NA): hemodilution lowers albumin disproportionately → A/G ↓.

## 9. Open questions / weak evidence

- **A/G has no targeted intervention RCT data** — it's a derived composite. All "intervention effects" are inferred from albumin or globulin trials.
- **Optimal A/G range**: cohort data (Suh *PLoS One* 2014 PMID 25244021; Yang *J Cancer* 2018 PMID 29736141) show monotonic mortality risk with low A/G, but the signal is largely from low albumin (inflammation marker), not the ratio per se.
- **Whether A/G adds independent prognostic information beyond CRP + albumin** is unclear; in most analyses A/G's predictive value attenuates after adjustment for inflammation markers.
- **A/G as a cancer screening tool**: low A/G has been proposed as a non-specific cancer-risk signal in cohort studies, but has poor specificity (any chronic inflammation lowers A/G) and is not a recommended cancer screen.
- **"A/G ratio optimization" via supplements**: marketing claim without mechanism. There is no supplement that selectively raises albumin or selectively lowers globulin in healthy adults beyond what general anti-inflammatory practices achieve.
