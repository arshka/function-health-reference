# Biomarker Confounds — General Reference

For interpreting future Function Health draws or any blood test. Covers timing, stress, fasting, and posture/sleep effects on common biomarkers.

---

## 1. Time of day — clock-anchored vs sleep-anchored

**Clock-anchored** (peak fixed at clock time regardless of sleep):
- TSH (peaks 2–4am, partly clock + partly sleep-modulated)
- Serum iron / Transferrin saturation (peaks ~11am in men)
- Free T3 (lags TSH by ~90 min)

**Sleep-anchored** (peak shifts with your wake time — habitual late risers have shifted peaks):
- Testosterone (REM-coupled; peak around wake)
- Prolactin (rises during sleep, drops fast on waking)
- Growth hormone (locked to first SWS pulse after sleep onset)
- Cortisol awakening response (peaks ~30 min post-wake)
- Estradiol in males (mildly sleep-shifted, follows T)

**Effectively flat / time-insensitive**:
- HbA1c, ApoB, Lp(a), all lipids, hs-CRP, ESR, vitamins (D, B12, folate)
- DHEA-S (long half-life buffers everything)
- IGF-1, Free T4, SHBG
- Heavy metals, autoimmune markers, cancer markers
- Electrolytes (Na, K, Cl, Ca, Mg)
- Liver enzymes, kidney markers
- MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW

**Entrainment timeline**: SCN-driven rhythms take days to ~2 weeks to fully shift to a new schedule. Single late wake doesn't shift clock-anchored markers.

## 2. Stress severity tiers

| Tier | Signs | Cortisol response | WBC | K+ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mild | HR +10–20 bpm, no tremor | +20–50% | +5–10% | −0.1–0.2 mmol/L |
| Moderate | HR 90–120, visible tremor, sweating | +100–200% (TSST-equivalent) | +15–25% | −0.3–0.6 mmol/L |
| Severe | HR >120 sustained, gross tremor, panic | +200–400% | +30–50% | −0.8–1.2 mmol/L |

**Visible hand tremor = strong β2 signal** (requires plasma epi ~3–10× resting baseline; Lader & Bruce 1986).

**Important**: cortisol response is gated by *social-evaluative threat + uncontrollability* (Dickerson & Kemeny 2004 meta-analysis), not by duration alone. Brief non-evaluative stressors (driving, running late solo) often produce minimal cortisol response even with strong sympathetic activation.

## 3. Stress recovery kinetics (after stressor ends)

| Effect | Half-life | Full recovery | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Plasma epinephrine | ~2 min | 10–15 min | Then downstream effects normalize |
| Plasma norepinephrine | ~2 min | 10–15 min | Same |
| Hemoconcentration | ~5–10 min | 10–30 min | Plasma volume re-expands |
| K+ shift | ~5–10 min | 15–30 min | Tracks epi clearance |
| Glucose excursion | ~30 min | 30–90 min | Slower than epi |
| Splenic platelet release | — | 20–30 min | Spleen refills |
| Lymphocyte demargination | Peaks at ~30 min post-stress-onset | 1–2 hours | Misconception: NOT 5–10 min — peaks later |
| Cortisol pulse | — | 40–60 min for small response | Peaks 15–25 min post-onset |
| Cortisol-driven neutrophilia | — | Peaks 2–4 hours post-stress | Slow genomic effect |
| Cortisol-driven eosinopenia | — | Develops over 1–2 hours | Slow |
| Prolactin pulse | ~20–30 min | 30–60 min | |

**Practical**: If stress ended >20 min before draw, most acute effects have cleared EXCEPT (a) cortisol may still be near its delayed peak, (b) lymphocyte demargination is at peak, (c) glucose may still be partially elevated.

## 4. Markers most affected by acute stress

In rough order of magnitude:
1. Cortisol — variable, +0% to +200% depending on stress quality
2. Catecholamines (epi/norepi) — not typically tested
3. Glucose — +5–30 mg/dL acute hyperglycemia
4. Insulin — paradoxically suppressed (α2-mediated)
5. Lactate — +30–50% rise
6. Potassium — −0.3 to −1.2 mmol/L (β-shift)
7. Prolactin — +20–100%
8. WBC + differential — +15–50%
9. Platelets — +20–30% (splenic)
10. Hb / Hct / RBC — +2–4% (hemoconcentration)
11. Lipids / total protein / albumin — +3–8% artifact (hemoconcentration)
12. Free fatty acids — large rise (not tested)

## 5. Markers acutely *unaffected* by stress

- HbA1c (3-month average)
- ApoB, Lp(a) (true value; mildly hemoconcentration-affected)
- hs-CRP, ESR, fibrinogen (slow inflammatory markers, need hours)
- Vitamins, ferritin (long half-lives)
- Heavy metals, autoimmune antibodies, cancer markers
- IGF-1, DHEA-S (long half-lives)
- Free T4, thyroid antibodies
- MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW (RBC properties don't change acutely)

## 6. Fasting effects

**Most fasting-sensitive** (require 12h fast):
- Triglycerides (can rise 30–100% if recent meal)
- VLDL (calculated from TG)
- Insulin (rises with food)
- C-peptide (parallels insulin)
- Glucose

**Mildly fasting-sensitive**:
- LDL (calculated, affected by TG)
- Total protein, albumin
- Iron (drops post-meal)
- Phosphate

**Fasting-insensitive**:
- HDL, Total cholesterol, ApoB, Lp(a)
- HbA1c, hs-CRP, vitamins, metals
- Liver enzymes, kidney markers
- Sex hormones, thyroid markers
- All antibody/immune markers

## 7. Bed-stay effects (after waking)

**Awake in bed → no meaningful effect at a 2–3 hour post-wake draw**:
- CAR is anchored to consciousness onset, not get-up time (Stalder 2016 consensus)
- Posture (supine vs upright) doesn't affect CAR (Hucklebridge 2002)
- T, TSH, prolactin all transition to wake-state secretion immediately on consciousness

**Dozing/back-to-sleep → modest measurable effects**:
- Prolactin: most affected (re-sleep triggers fresh PRL pulse, persists into draw)
- Testosterone: modestly higher (REM extension preserves the sleep plateau)
- TSH: modestly lower (sleep continues suppressing)
- Cortisol: CAR re-anchors to final wake (small shift only)
- GH: morning sleep is REM-dominated, no SWS, no GH benefit

## 8. Quick reference — best draw conditions

For most reliable results:
- Calm for 20+ minutes pre-draw
- Fasted 10–12 hours
- Same time of day for repeat tests (consistency > "perfect" timing)
- 7–8am for early risers; 9–10am for habitual late risers
- Consistent posture (Function uses seated venipuncture)
- Avoid heavy exercise within 24 hours
- Avoid alcohol within 24 hours (affects liver enzymes, lipids)

## 9. Markers tested twice a year by Function (mid-year repeat)

Function explicitly markets ApoB as "tested twice a year." The mid-year ~60-marker test covers the *trackable, intervention-responsive* categories:
- Lipid panel + ApoB
- Metabolic (HbA1c, fasting glucose, insulin)
- Inflammation (hs-CRP)
- Full CBC + CMP (liver, kidney, electrolytes)
- Thyroid (TSH, Free T3/T4)
- Vitamin D, B12, ferritin, homocysteine
- Cortisol, key sex hormones
- Omega-3 Index

**Likely NOT repeated mid-year** (one-time / slow / genetic):
- Heavy metals, ANA + autoimmune screens
- Cancer markers (CEA, CA-125, CA 19-9, AFP)
- Lp(a) (genetic, lifetime stable)
- AMH, thyroid antibodies, celiac panel
